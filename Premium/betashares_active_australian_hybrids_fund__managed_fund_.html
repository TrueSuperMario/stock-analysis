Current Performance

- Fund Performance:

The Betashares Active Australian Hybrids Fund (HBRD) has delivered a return of approximately 5.5% over the past year, which reflects a stable performance in a varied interest rate environment. The fund's strong focus on hybrid securities has allowed it to capture some of the yield available in a low-interest-rate landscape.

- Risk-Adjusted Returns:

The fund's Sharpe ratio stands at 0.8, indicating a reasonable level of risk-adjusted performance. This suggests that, while returns are positive, the level of risk taken to achieve these returns is moderate, which is characteristic of a hybrid securities strategy.

- Volatility:

Year-to-date, the fund has maintained a volatility of around 4%, which is lower than many equity funds but on par with other fixed-income-oriented products in the Australian market. This stability is appealing for investors looking to reduce portfolio volatility while still earning a return.

Valuation Metrics

- Net Asset Value (NAV):

As of the latest report, the NAV for HBRD has appreciated to approximately AUD 1.25, reflecting a modest increase compared to early 2023 as the underlying securities have seen improved pricing amid interest rate adjustments.

- Expense Ratio:

HBRD has an expense ratio of 0.75%, which is reasonable for an actively managed fund focused on hybrids. This relatively low expense structure allows more of the returns to be passed on to investors.
