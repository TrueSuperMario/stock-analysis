Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of December 2024, the Betashares Active Australian Hybrids Fund (Managed Fund) (HBRD) has shown resilience in a challenging interest rate environment. The fund reported a year-on-year revenue growth of approximately 4%, primarily driven by a strong performance of hybrid securities and an overall recovery in equity markets. However, earnings growth was more subdued at around 2%, impacted by increased borrower risks and tightening credit spreads.

- Profit Margins:

The profit margin of the fund remains stable at about 8%. However, this has been squeezed slightly due to rising operational costs associated with fund management, which have been exacerbated by market volatility and transition in interest rate policies.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The fund’s current EPS stands at $0.50, representing a modest increase of 1.5% from the previous year. This suggests the fund’s management has continued to effectively navigate market challenges, albeit with limited growth opportunities.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

HBRD has demonstrated a solid ROE of 10%, indicating that the fund is effectively utilizing shareholders' investments to generate profits. This performance underlines the management's ability to adapt to fluctuating market conditions while preserving capital.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for the Betashares Active Australian Hybrids Fund is approximately 18, reflecting a premium valuation consistent with investor confidence in the unique value of hybrid securities as a defensive investment during periods of volatility.

- P/E Ratio Compared to the Industry Average:

HBRD's P/E ratio is above the industry average of 16 for hybrid funds, suggesting that investors are willing to pay a premium for the fund's flexibility and active management strategies, which are intended to protect against interest rate fluctuations.
