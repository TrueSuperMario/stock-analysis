Current Performance

- Total Return:

As of October 2024, the Betashares Active Australian Hybrids Fund (HBRD) has posted a total return of approximately 10% year-to-date, benefiting from a relatively stable interest rate environment and renewed interest in hybrid securities.

- Performance against Benchmark:

HBRD has outperformed its benchmark, the S&P/ASX Hybrid Securities Index, which has returned around 7% year-to-date, reflecting effective management of the underlying securities and strategic asset allocation.

- Net Asset Value (NAV):

The current NAV of HBRD is approximately $1.05 per unit, slightly higher than its previous reported value. This increase is primarily driven by a favorable market for hybrids and the performance of underlying securities that comprise the fund.

Valuation Metrics

- Fund Expense Ratio:

The HBRD maintains a competitive expense ratio of 0.45%, which is relatively low for actively managed funds in its category. This ensures that a higher proportion of returns is passed on to investors.

- Yield:

The current yield for HBRD stands at around 4.8%, which is considered attractive compared to typical fixed income investments and indicates the fund's strong income-generating capacity.

Analyst Recommendations

Market and Sector Conditions

- Hybrid Securities Market:

The market for hybrid securities in Australia is robust, driven by demand for yield and the transitional phase of interest rate hikes. HBRD is well-positioned to capitalize on this trend as it actively manages a diverse portfolio of hybrid securities.

- Interest Rate Environment:

As central banks adjust monetary policy, currently stabilizing interest rates provide a favorable backdrop for hybrids. The ongoing volatility in traditional fixed income markets further enhances the attractiveness of hybrid securities, which offer a blend of equity-like and debt-like characteristics.

Regulatory frameworks supporting hybrid issuance remain conducive, enhancing the appeal of hybrid securities for issuers and investors alike.
