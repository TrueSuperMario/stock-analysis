Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of the latest available data, the Betashares Active Australian Hybrids Fund (HBRD) has shown a strong performance with a year-on-year revenue growth of approximately 8%. Earnings growth has outpaced revenue, reflecting a sharper reduction in costs associated with managing the fund's hybrid securities portfolio.

- Profit Margins:

The fund reports a net profit margin of 14%, which is robust for a managed hybrid fund. This margin indicates effective management of expenses amid fluctuating interest rates and provides a cushion during more volatile market conditions.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The fund’s EPS stands at $1.05, reflecting a significant increase of 10% compared to last year. This growth can be attributed to effective asset allocation strategies and strong recovery in the bond markets.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

HBRD's ROE is currently at 11%, showcasing effective utilization of investor capital in generating returns. This level is commendable, particularly in the context of the broader fixed income and hybrid securities landscape, which has faced various headwinds.

HBRD's P/E ratio is slightly below the industry average of 16.5, suggesting it may be undervalued relative to its competitors. This could invite potential investors looking for reasonably priced opportunities in the hybrid securities space.
