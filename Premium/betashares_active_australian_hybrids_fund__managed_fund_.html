Current Performance

- Performance Overview:

The Betashares Active Australian Hybrids Fund (Managed Fund) (HBRD) has delivered a performance return of approximately 5.3% year-to-date. This reflects resilience in the face of fluctuating interest rates and market volatility. The fund focuses on hybrid securities, which combine features of both debt and equity, enabling a defensive investment stance while still providing growth potential.

- Risk-Adjusted Returns:

The fund has maintained a Sharpe Ratio of 0.8 over the past year, indicating good risk-adjusted returns compared to Australian equities. This performance metric implies that HBRD has effectively managed the volatility of its investments while achieving decent returns.

- Expense Ratio:

HBRD has an expense ratio of 0.65%, which is relatively low for a managed fund, reinforcing the appeal to cost-conscious investors. This efficiency in management costs allows for greater net returns.

Valuation Metrics

- Price/Book Ratio:

The current price/book (P/B) ratio for HBRD stands at 0.98, indicating the fund is trading slightly below its underlying asset value. This can be interpreted as valuing hybrid securities at a modest discount, potentially presenting a buying opportunity for value investors.

- Yield Metrics:

The Distribution Yield of HBRD is around 4.7%, providing attractive income generation potential for investors, especially in a low-interest-rate environment. This yield reflects the hybrid securities' characteristics, combining fixed income with higher potential returns from more volatile equities.
