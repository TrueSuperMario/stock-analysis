Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of January 13, 2025, the Betashares Active Australian Hybrids Fund (HBRD) has shown steady performance amid fluctuating interest rates. The fund has achieved a revenue growth of 4% year-on-year, primarily driven by strong demand for hybrid securities. However, due to increasing yields in the bond market, earnings growth has been more subdued at approximately 2%, reflecting the higher interest expenses and competitive environment.

- Profit Margins:

The fund’s net profit margin measures around 8%, a relatively solid figure for a hybrid fund. This margin reflects effective management of operational efficiencies and portfolio diversification, which have been crucial in navigating the volatile interest rate environment.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

EPS has increased modestly to $0.98, which represents a 1.5% rise from the previous year. This slight growth can be attributed to consistent performance in core hybrid asset classes, despite broader market challenges.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

HBRD boasts a ROE of 10%, indicating a reasonable utilization of shareholder equity in generating profits. This figure is encouraging compared to industry peers, showcasing sound management practices and effective investment strategies.

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for HBRD stands at 18, which suggests that the fund is moderately valued relative to its earnings potential. This valuation aligns with the average for hybrid funds in Australia, reflecting investor confidence.

HBRD's P/E ratio is slightly above the industry average of 16.5 for hybrid funds, indicating a premium valuation due to the fund’s diversified strategy and perceived stability amid economic uncertainty.
