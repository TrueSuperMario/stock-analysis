Current Performance

- - Revenue and Earnings Growth:

The Betashares Active Australian Hybrids Fund (HBRD) has seen a year-to-date growth in revenue of approximately 4%, driven primarily by a diverse portfolio of hybrid securities that have fared well in a low-interest-rate environment. Earnings growth has been slightly more substantial at around 5%, as demand for hybrid securities remains strong amidst fluctuating market conditions.

- - Profit Margins:

The fund's net profit margin currently stands at 8%, which is commendable given the competitive nature of the fixed-income market. The management's active approach and diversified asset allocation have enabled the fund to maintain these margins, despite some pressure from rising interest rates.

- - Earnings Per Share (EPS):

HBRD's EPS is currently at $1.10, equating to a 3% increase from the previous fiscal year. This growth highlights the fund's consistent performance and strategic asset management in the face of changing market dynamics.

- - Return on Equity (ROE):

The fund reports an ROE of 9%, indicating efficient utilization of assets in generating returns. This level of ROE is respectable for a hybrid securities fund, given its focus on stability and capital preservation.

Valuation Metrics

- - Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

HBRD's P/E ratio is currently around 14, suggesting a balanced valuation relative to its earnings potential. This reflects investor confidence in the fund’s management strategies and steady income generation capabilities.

- - P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

The P/E ratio for HBRD is slightly below the industry average of 16 for hybrid funds, which may indicate that the fund is undervalued compared to peers, making it an attractive option for yield-seeking investors.
