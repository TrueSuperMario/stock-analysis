Current Performance

- - Net Asset Value (NAV) and Performance:

As of September 26, 2024, the Betashares Active Australian Hybrids Fund (HBRD) has shown stable performance with a year-to-date return of approximately 5%. The fund's NAV currently stands at around AUD 103.50, reflecting a steady appreciation in value over the past year, supported by a diverse portfolio of hybrid securities.

- - Portfolio Composition:

HBRD invests primarily in a mix of hybrid securities issued by Australian companies, including both equity-like and debt-like instruments. This approach has allowed the fund to capitalize on the yield offered by these securities while maintaining a level of risk that is generally lower than pure equities.

- - Risk-Adjusted Returns:

The fund boasts a Sharpe ratio of 0.9, implying that it has provided strong risk-adjusted returns compared to its benchmark. This demonstrates effective management in navigating market volatility and extracting value from the hybrids sector.

Valuation Metrics

- - Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

Currently, the P/E ratio of HBRD is not applicable as it is a managed fund focused on hybrid securities rather than traditional equities. However, the yield on the underlying securities averages around 5.6%, providing a strong indicator of relative valuation compared to fixed-income assets.

HBRD offers a distribution yield of approximately 4.5%, which is appealing for income-focused investors. This yield is competitive within the fixed-income sector, reflecting the income-generating capacity of hybrid securities.
