Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

Betashares Active Australian Hybrids Fund (HBRD) has shown resilience in its revenue generation, with an annualized growth rate of approximately 4.5%. This growth is primarily attributed to a diversified portfolio of hybrid securities, including corporate hybrids and listed hybrids, which have performed well in the current economic climate.

- Profit Margins:

The fund's profit margin has been stable at around 8%, reflecting efficient management of operations and a diversified investment approach. The relatively low expense ratio and strong credit quality of the underlying securities have contributed positively.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

HBRD has reported an EPS of $1.15, which indicates a 3% increase from the previous year. This growth underscores the fund's capability to provide steady returns amidst fluctuating market conditions.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The fund's ROE stands at 7%, which is satisfactory for a fund focused on hybrid securities. This return reflects effective utilization of capital, as hybrid investments typically yield higher returns on equity than traditional fixed-income securities.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

HBRD's current P/E ratio is 16, indicating that the market values the fund moderately with respect to its earnings. This ratio is reflective of the stability and less volatility associated with hybrid securities compared to equities.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

The P/E ratio for HBRD is slightly below the industry average of 18 for hybrid-focused funds, suggesting it may be undervalued relative to its peers. This could present a buying opportunity for investors seeking exposure to hybrids at an attractive valuation.
