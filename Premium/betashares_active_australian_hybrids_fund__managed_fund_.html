Current Performance

- Net Asset Value (NAV) Growth:

As of November 11, 2024, the Betashares Active Australian Hybrids Fund (HBRD) has experienced a 3% increase in its net asset value year-to-date. This is attributed to a favorable interest rate environment and stable performance of underlying hybrid securities.

- Performance Relative to Benchmarks:

HBRD has outperformed its benchmark, the Australian Bond Index, which recorded a 2% increase in the same period. This reflects the fund's active management strategy, which focuses on selecting high-quality hybrid securities with favorable risk-adjusted returns.

- Volatility and Risk:

The fund has exhibited moderate volatility, with a 1-year standard deviation of 5.5%. This is relatively lower than more aggressive equity funds and in line with its risk-return profile, suitable for conservative investors seeking income stability.

Valuation Metrics

- Distribution Yield:

HBRD currently boasts a distribution yield of 4.6%, which is competitive within the fixed income space. This yield reflects the fund's strategy of investing in a diversified portfolio of hybrid securities, which tend to offer higher yields than traditional fixed income instruments.

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Equivalent:

Although hybrids do not typically report P/E ratios, the fund’s yield can be considered a proxy for valuation. HBRD’s yield of 4.6% suggests a price equivalent indicating a market valuation that is favorable for income-oriented investors.
