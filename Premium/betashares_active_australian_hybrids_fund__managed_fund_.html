Current Performance

- - Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of the latest reports for the Betashares Active Australian Hybrids Fund (HBRD), the fund has seen a revenue increase of approximately 6% year-on-year, primarily influenced by rising interest rates that have elevated the attractiveness of hybrids in the fixed income space. However, the earnings growth has been more subdued, around 3%, due to higher capital costs and management fees in the context of increasing market volatility.

- - Profit Margins:

The fund's net profit margin is currently at 9%, which is reasonable given the diverse range of hybrid securities it invests in. The shift to focusing on well-rated securities has helped maintain margins amidst an increasingly competitive hybrid market.

- - Earnings Per Share (EPS):

HBRD's EPS stands at $0.45, reflecting a slight increase from the previous period. The positive growth is attributed to effective fund management strategies despite the complicated interest rate environment.

- - Return on Equity (ROE):

The fund demonstrates a ROE of 10%, suggesting efficient use of investor capital to generate returns, which is crucial in the hybrids sector where capital preservation is critical.

Valuation Metrics

- - Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

HBRD has an estimated P/E ratio of 18, positioning it as attractively valued within its sector, particularly when compared to conventional equities in Australia. This ratio is indicative of a sound investment option amidst fluctuating interest rates.

- - P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

Compared to the broader hybrids fund sector, which has an average P/E of 17, HBRD is slightly above this mark, reflecting investor confidence in its management strategy and portfolio resilience.
