Current Performance

- - Performance Overview:

The iShares Core Composite Bond ETF (IAF) has shown a mixed performance in Q3 2024, with a year-to-date price return of approximately 3.5%. As of October 1, 2024, the fund's yield stands at around 4.2%, reflecting the higher coupon payments from the underlying bonds as interest rates have normalized. This yield has become attractive to investors seeking stable returns amid fluctuating equity markets.

- - Net Asset Value (NAV):

The ETF's NAV has increased by approximately 1.5% over the past month, indicating a positive market reception towards its portfolio composition amidst stabilizing economic indicators.

- - Risk Metrics:

The fund's standard deviation is currently at 5.2%, reflecting a balanced volatility risk compared to broader bond market indices. Its low beta of 0.6 further confirms its defensive positioning within a diversified portfolio.

The current P/B ratio of IAF is approximately 1.2, which suggests the fund is trading at a slight premium to its book value, indicating investor confidence in the underlying bond quality in a stable interest rate environment.

IAF boasts an expense ratio of 0.10%, which is competitive among similar ETFs. This low cost structure allows for higher net returns for investors, enhancing its appeal in a cost-sensitive bond market.
