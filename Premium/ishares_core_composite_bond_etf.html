Current Performance

- - Portfolio Composition and Yield:

As of September 25, 2024, the Ishares Core Composite Bond ETF (IAF) has a diversified fixed-income portfolio, primarily consisting of Australian government bonds and corporate bonds, which together contribute to a robust yield. The current distribution yield stands at approximately 3.5%, indicating a steady stream of income for investors seeking bond exposure.

- - Net Asset Value (NAV):

The ETF has demonstrated moderate fluctuations in its NAV, currently trading around AUD 120 per unit. This reflects a 2% increase year-to-date, highlighting a resilient performance amidst varying interest rates.

- - Bond Market Conditions:

IAF has benefited from a favorable bond market environment characterized by declining yields on government securities. The ETF's average duration stands at about 5.3 years, allowing it to hedge against potential interest rate hikes effectively.

Valuation Metrics

- - Expense Ratio:

IAF maintains a low expense ratio of 0.20%, which is competitive within the ETF space. This efficiency in managing fund costs contributes positively to investor returns.

- - Tracking Error:

The fund exhibits a tracking error of approximately 0.10%, showcasing its effectiveness in mimicking the performance of its benchmark, the Bloomberg AusBond Composite 0+ Year Index.

- - Yield Comparison:

The 3.5% yield of IAF is in line with the average yield from similar bond ETFs, which suggests a fair valuation relative to its peers in the fixed-income category.
