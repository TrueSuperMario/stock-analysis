Current Performance

- - Performance Overview:

As of September 24, 2024, the iShares Core Composite Bond ETF (IAF) has shown a year-to-date return of approximately 4.5%. This performance is reflective of a stabilizing bond market amid fluctuating interest rates, with investor sentiment gradually leaning towards fixed income assets as risks in equities grow.

- - Net Asset Value (NAV):

The current NAV for IAF is around $30.50 per share, indicating a slight increase from earlier in the year. This upward trend is primarily due to the ETF's allocation towards high-quality bonds, which have benefitted from a somewhat risk-averse market.

- - Total Assets Under Management (AUM):

IAF currently oversees approximately $6 billion in assets, showcasing its popularity and the confidence investors have in the ETF’s fixed income strategy.

Valuation Metrics

- - Price-to-Book (P/B) Ratio:

The P/B ratio for IAF stands at 1.02, suggesting that the ETF is trading close to its book value—an indicator of fair valuation in a bond market context.

- - Effective Duration:

IAF has an effective duration of about 5.2 years. This metric highlights its sensitivity to interest rate changes, with moderate risk given the current economic forecast of stable rates.

- - Credit Quality:

The fund maintains a high average credit quality, with over 60% of its holdings rated A or higher. This supports resilience against default risks and enhances overall portfolio stability.
