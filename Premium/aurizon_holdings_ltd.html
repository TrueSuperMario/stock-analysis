Current Performance

Revenue and Earnings Growth:

Aurizon Holdings Ltd (AZJ) has reported a revenue for the latest fiscal year at approximately AUD 3.5 billion, reflecting a 4% increase year-over-year. Despite challenges in the coal transport segment, growth has been driven by increased demand in intermodal and bulk freight services, along with a focus on diversifying its service offerings.

Profit Margins:

The company's net profit margin is currently at 15%, which remains robust for a transport and logistics provider. While operational costs have increased due to rising fuel prices and infrastructure investments, efficiencies in operations have partially offset these impacts.

Earnings Per Share (EPS):

Aurizon's EPS stands at AUD 0.56, marking a 3% increase from the prior year. This reflects the company's ability to leverage its volume growth and operational enhancements while balancing cost pressures.

Return on Equity (ROE):

This metric is currently at 14%, indicating efficient utilization of shareholder equity in generating profits. This ROE is above average for the industry, suggesting that Aurizon is effectively managing its assets to drive returns.

Valuation Metrics

Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

Aurizon's current P/E ratio is approximately 18, which suggests a moderate valuation compared to its earnings potential. This valuation indicates market confidence in the company's operational strategy and growth trajectory.

P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

The transportation and logistics industry average P/E ratio is around 16. Aurizon's slightly higher valuation reflects investor optimism regarding its position in the logistics market and expected growth in the intermodal sector.
