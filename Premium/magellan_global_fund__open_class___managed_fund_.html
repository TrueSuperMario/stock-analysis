Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of October 2024, Magellan Global Fund (Open Class) (Managed Fund) (MGOC) reports a 7% year-on-year growth in net asset value (NAV), primarily driven by strong performance in international equities. Earnings growth has been stable at 5%, demonstrating resilience against market volatility and effective portfolio management.

- Profit Margins:

The fund has maintained a gross margin of 15%, which reflects the costs associated with management fees and operational expenditures. This is seen as a healthy margin in the actively managed fund space where high-quality management incurs additional costs.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The fund currently has an EPS of $1.15, representing a 4% increase year-on-year. This gradual increase indicates a consistent return to shareholders through capital growth and effective investment strategies.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The ROE for MGOC stands at 10.5%, reflecting the effective use of shareholder equity to generate returns. While this is considered moderate, it is noteworthy given the challenging global market conditions.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for MGOC is 18, suggesting a fair valuation relative to growth prospects. This indicates that the market values the fund's diversified portfolio and active management approach positively.

- P/E Ratio Compared to the Industry Average:

MGOC's P/E ratio is slightly above the average for global equity funds, which stands at 17. This slight premium can be attributed to Magellan's strong track record and expertise in global investing.
