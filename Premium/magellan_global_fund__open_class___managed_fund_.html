Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of October 2024, Magellan Global Fund (Open Class) (MGOC) has shown a year-to-date return of approximately 12%. This performance has benefited from a rebound in global equities, particularly in sectors such as technology and consumer discretionary. However, the earnings growth for this fund has been influenced by volatility in equity markets, and underlying earnings have shown a year-on-year increase of around 5%, slightly below expectations due to macroeconomic pressures.

- Profit Margins:

The fund's net profit margin is estimated at 8%, reflecting the impact of management fees and operating expenses relative to revenue generated from its investment activities. While this is a typical margin for managed funds, ongoing market volatility has exerted downward pressure on profitability.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The latest reported EPS stands at $1.15, a modest increase from the previous year, which highlights the fund's ability to navigate turbulent market conditions. The EPS growth trend indicates a gradual recovery in the fund's underlying asset performance.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

MGOC currently boasts an ROE of 10%, which is decent for a managed fund. This figure suggests that the fund effectively utilizes shareholder equity to generate returns, though it reflects broader market performance rather than solely fund management efficiency.

Valuation Metrics

Compared to other managed funds, MGOC's P/E ratio is slightly elevated, indicating investor confidence in the fund's long-term growth potential.
