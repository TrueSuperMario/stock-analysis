Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of December 2024, Magellan Global Fund (Open Class) (MGOC) has demonstrated a robust annual revenue growth of approximately 10%. This increase has been supported by a strong performance in global equity markets, particularly in developed markets. However, earnings growth has been slightly lower at 8%, reflecting the challenges associated with fluctuating market conditions and investment strategy adjustments.

- Profit Margins:

The fund currently boasts a net profit margin of 12%, which is commendable in the managed fund sector. This reflects the efficiency of the fund's management and its ability to minimize expenses while maximizing portfolio performance, despite market volatility.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The EPS for MGOC has increased to $1.05, marking a 5% increase compared to the previous year. This growth can be attributed to effective stock selection and market timing by the fund managers.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The fund’s ROE is reported at 15%, indicating a strong performance relative to shareholder equity. This is a good sign of the fund's ability to generate profits from its investments.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for Magellan Global Fund is approximately 18, suggesting that the fund is moderately valued in relation to its earnings potential. This ratio reflects the market's overall confidence in the fund's long-term growth strategy.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

MGOC's P/E ratio is marginally above the managed funds industry average of 16. This premium suggests that investors have higher expectations for MGOC’s future performance relative to its peers.
