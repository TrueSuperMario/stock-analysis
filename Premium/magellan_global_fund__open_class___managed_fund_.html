Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of January 2025, the Magellan Global Fund (Open Class)(MGOC) has reported a revenue growth of approximately 6% year-on-year, attributed to a recovery from market downturns and strengthening performance in various geographical markets. Earnings growth is recorded at 4%, reflecting continued investment in high-quality equities and a diversified portfolio that has weathered volatility.

- Profit Margins:

The fund has maintained a net profit margin of 9%. While market conditions have presented challenges, Magellan's strategic asset allocation and diligent research process have allowed it to retain strong margins relative to peers in the managed fund space.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The fund's EPS has improved to $1.15, marking a 5% increase from the previous year. This growth has been supported by a gradual recovery in global markets, especially in technology and healthcare sectors, where Magellan has concentrated its investments.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The fund's ROE stands at 10%, indicating efficient management of equity capital to generate returns. This figure is considered favorable in the managed fund sector, particularly given the recent market volatility.

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for MGOC is 18, suggesting that it is trading at a slightly elevated level relative to its historical averages. This valuation reflects the market's optimism regarding the fund's long-term growth potential amidst cyclical recovery.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

The P/E ratio is slightly above the average P/E of 16 for managed equity funds. This premium could be justified by the fund's strong performance history and rigorous investment strategy that focuses on high-quality global equities.
