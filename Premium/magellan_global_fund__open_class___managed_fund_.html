Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of December 2024, the Magellan Global Fund (Open Class) (Managed Fund) (MGOC) has demonstrated a robust year-on-year revenue growth of approximately 8%. This growth is attributed to effective asset allocation in high-performing global equities, leading to an increase in management fees. The fund's earnings growth has surpassed revenue growth at approximately 10%, benefiting from a favorable investment climate and meaningful capital appreciation of its holdings.

- Profit Margins:

The fund's net profit margin currently stands at around 25%, reflecting strong operational efficiency and margin control in fund management expenses. This margin is commendable, particularly in a market with fluctuating investment returns.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The EPS for MGOC is currently reported at AUD 1.10, which shows a significant increase from the previous year's AUD 0.95. This reflects not just growth in the fund's portfolio but also effective cost management.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The ROE for the Magellan Global Fund is at 15%. This healthy figure indicates that the fund managers are efficiently utilizing shareholders' equity to generate returns, positioning MGOC as one of the more aggressive funds in terms of achieving returns compared to its peers.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for MGOC is 21, suggesting that the fund is slightly premium-priced relative to its earnings potential. This reflects the market's confidence in the fund's management and its investment strategies.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

The average P/E ratio for managed funds in the global equity space is around 18. MGOC’s higher ratio indicates that investors expect superior growth from this fund, which is ultimately supported by strong past performance.
