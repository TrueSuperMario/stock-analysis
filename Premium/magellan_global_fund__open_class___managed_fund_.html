Current Performance

As of October 2024, the Magellan Global Fund (Open Class) (MGOC) has experienced an annual revenue growth of approximately 7%, attributed to strong stock market performance and an effective investment strategy. Earnings growth has been consistent, with a reported increase of around 6% year-on-year, reflecting solid returns in a diversified portfolio.

- Profit Margins:

The fund has maintained healthy profit margins, with a net profit margin of about 12%. This level of margin is commendable, particularly given the volatility of the global markets and the challenges faced by managed funds during economic fluctuations.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The EPS for the fund currently stands at $3.40, indicating a 5% increase compared to the previous year. This growth can be linked to the fund's robust selection of international equities and strategic allocations in high-growth sectors.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The fund's ROE is reported at 15%, reflecting its efficiency in generating profits from its equity investments. This is a strong performance metric, suggesting successful investment strategies and management effectiveness.

Compared to the managed funds industry average, MGOC’s P/E ratio indicates a premium valuation, signifying a strong belief in its ability to outperform in the medium to long-term due to its focus on global equities.
