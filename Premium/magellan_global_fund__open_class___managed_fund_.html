As of December 30, 2024, the Magellan Global Fund (Open Class) (Managed Fund) (MGOC) has reported a revenue increase of 7% year-on-year, primarily due to successful international investments and a recovery in global markets post-pandemic. Earnings growth has been slightly more robust at 9%, reflecting an improved market sentiment and strategic asset allocation decisions by the management team.

- Profit Margins:

The fund’s net profit margin stands at 14%, indicating a healthy return relative to the operational priorities and expenses associated with managing a global investment fund. This margin reflects effective cost control and successful execution of investment strategies.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

EPS for MGOC is reported at $2.45, showing a notable increase of 5% from the previous year. This improvement is attributed to strategic stock selections and favorable market conditions that have enhanced the fund's overall profitability.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The fund has an ROE of 10%, which suggests effective use of investor capital to generate profits. This figure is competitive, particularly given the diversified global investment approach pursued by Magellan.
