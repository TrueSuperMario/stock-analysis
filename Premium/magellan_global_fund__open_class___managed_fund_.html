Current Performance

- Fund Performance:

As of October 26, 2024, the Magellan Global Fund (Open Class) (Managed Fund) (MGOC) has achieved a year-to-date return of approximately 12%, outperforming its benchmark index, which has returned around 8%. This strong performance is attributed to effective stock selection, particularly in the technology and consumer discretionary sectors.

- Net Asset Value (NAV):

The current NAV of MGOC stands at AUD 3.15, reflecting steady growth over the past year, largely driven by an upward trend in global equities. The fund has effectively capitalized on market rebounds, enhancing its overall value.

- Volatility:

The fund's annualized volatility is 10%, which suggests it has maintained relatively stable performance compared to broader market fluctuations. This reflects Magellan's investment strategy aimed at mitigating risk while still pursuing growth opportunities.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The P/E ratio of the holdings within MGOC averages around 20, which is in line with the global equities market. This indicates a fair valuation considering the growth potential of the portfolio.

- Price-to-Book (P/B) Ratio:

The fund’s weighted average P/B ratio is currently 3.2, suggesting a slight premium compared to the average for global equity markets, indicating investor confidence in the quality of the underlying assets.

- Comparison to Benchmark:

Compared to its benchmark, the MGOC has a higher P/E ratio, reflecting the growth-oriented nature of its investments in sectors expected to benefit from long-term trends.
