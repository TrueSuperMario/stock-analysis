Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of January 13, 2025, Vanguard Australian Shares INDEX ETF (VAS) has seen a revenue increase of approximately 7% year-over-year, largely attributable to a steady recovery in the Australian equity market post-pandemic. The earnings growth has been robust at around 8%, bolstered by a favorable mix of large Australian companies gaining traction in global markets.

- Profit Margins:

VAS operates passively and doesn't generate profits in the traditional sense as a company; however, the underlying assets it tracks have maintained average profit margins of about 15% across its largest holdings, reflecting a healthy earnings environment in the Australian market.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The ETF structure does not report EPS, but the collective performance of its holdings suggests an effective earnings yield of around 5%, indicating attractive returns relative to its price.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The ROE for the underlying companies represented in VAS is approximately 12%, indicating that these companies effectively generate profits from their equity. This is a solid performance indicative of the underlying companies’ efficiency.

Compared to the broader Australian market, with an average P/E of 17, VAS’s valuation reflects a premium, indicating optimism surrounding its top holdings that include large financial and materials companies.
