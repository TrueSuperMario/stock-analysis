Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of October 2024, Vanguard Australian Shares INDEX ETF (VAS) has experienced a revenue growth of approximately 7% year-on-year, supported by a broad recovery in the Australian equity market post-pandemic. The earnings growth mirrors this trend, with a robust 8% increase attributed to strong performances from key sectors such as materials and financial services.

- Profit Margins:

The profit margins for the ETF are in line with industry standards, sitting at around 15%. This level is robust given the diversified nature of the underlying securities, which comprise large-cap Australian companies that generally maintain healthy margins.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The EPS of VAS is reported at $3.10, which indicates a 10% increase compared to the previous year, showcasing effective management of the underlying holdings and their contributions to overall performance.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The ETF’s underlying assets reflect an ROE of approximately 13%, indicating efficient use of equity to generate returns. This is particularly strong for an index fund that aims to replicate the performance of the wider Australian share market.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

VAS has a current P/E ratio of 18.5, suggesting that it is fairly valued in relation to its estimated future earnings. This valuation indicates that investors are willing to pay a premium for exposure to the leading companies in the Australian market.

- P/E Ratio Compared to Industry Average:

The P/E ratio is slightly above the Australian equity market average of 17.5. This premium may suggest investor confidence in the long-term growth prospects of the companies included in the index and their resilience in a challenging economic environment.
