Current Performance

- Year-to-Date Performance:

As of October 3, 2024, the Vanguard Australian Shares INDEX ETF (VAS) has shown a modest year-to-date increase of approximately 8%, reflecting a recovery in the Australian equities market. This performance aligns with broader market trends influenced by easing monetary policy and improved investor sentiment.

- Net Asset Value (NAV):

VAS currently has a NAV of approximately AUD 92.50. The ETF has consistently tracked its benchmark, the S&P/ASX 300 Index, with a tracking error of less than 0.5%, indicating efficient management and alignment with its investment objective.

- Market Capitalization:

VAS has a market capitalization of approximately AUD 6 billion, positioning it as one of the largest ETFs in Australia, which contributes to its liquidity and attractiveness for institutional investors.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for VAS is approximately 15, indicative of a reasonable valuation compared to historical averages. This suggests the ETF is fairly priced relative to its earnings potential within the Australian market.

- Price-to-Book (P/B) Ratio:

VAS holds a P/B ratio of 1.8, which is in line with the average for the ASX 300 Index. This reflects a balanced assessment of the underlying companies in the index, with a slight premium for growth expectations.

Summary

The Vanguard Australian Shares INDEX ETF (VAS) is currently exhibiting strong performance, with modest year-to-date gains and solid valuation metrics.
