Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth: The Vanguard Australian Shares INDEX ETF (VAS) has experienced stable performance over the past year, reflecting the robust recovery of the Australian equity market post-pandemic. The ETF reported a return of approximately 9% year-to-date as of mid-September 2024, buoyed by gains in key sectors such as financials and materials, which constitute significant portions of its holdings.

- Profit Margins: As a passive investment vehicle tracking the S&P/ASX 300 Index, VAS does not generate operational profits like an active company. However, the underlying companies within the index, on average, are showing improved margins driven by enhanced demand and operational efficiencies, translating to overall growth in the market value of the ETF.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS): The concept of EPS does not apply directly to an ETF. Instead, investors should evaluate the individual performance of constituent companies. Many companies within the index reported healthy EPS growth this fiscal year, contributing positively to the ETF's performance.

- Return on Equity (ROE): ROE metrics for the ETF's underlying holdings vary, but leading companies within the index, like BHP Group and Commonwealth Bank, maintain ROEs above 15%, indicative of strong shareholder returns relative to their equity.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio: The current P/E ratio for VAS, reflecting its underlying index, is approximately 18.5, which aligns closely with the historical average for the Australian market, suggesting that the ETF is fairly valued compared to its historical performance.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average: Compared to the broader global equity markets, VAS's P/E ratio is slightly higher than international averages. This reflects the relative strength and perceived stability of the Australian economy, particularly in sectors such as banking and mining.
