Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

JB Hi-Fi Ltd has reported a revenue of AUD 7.68 billion for the most recent fiscal year, marking a decline of approximately 2% year-on-year, primarily due to a slowdown in consumer electronics demand following earlier pandemic-related surges. Earnings before interest and tax (EBIT) have dropped by 5%, reflecting increased competition and changing consumer behavior.

- Profit Margins:

The company maintains a net profit margin of around 5%, which is a respectable figure in the retail sector, particularly under current market pressures, including inflation and supply chain challenges. The slight reduction in margin has been attributed to rising costs of goods sold and operational expenses.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

JB Hi-Fi has delivered an EPS of AUD 1.92, a decrease of 6% from the previous year. This decline reflects reduced earnings in line with falling revenues, with the company focusing on cost management strategies to protect margins.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The current ROE for JB Hi-Fi stands at approximately 25%, indicating strong profitability relative to shareholders' equity. This high ROE showcases the company's efficiency in generating returns from its capital investments, despite recent performance challenges.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:
