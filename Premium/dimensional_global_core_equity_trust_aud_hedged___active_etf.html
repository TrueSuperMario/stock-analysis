Current Performance

- Return on Investment (ROI):

Dimensional Global Core Equity Trust AUD Hedged (DFGH) has delivered a return of approximately 8.5% year-to-date, reflecting a solid performance against broader market indices which have grown around 7% over the same period. This illustrates the fund’s effective investment strategy and resilience in varying market conditions.

- Net Asset Value (NAV) Growth:

As of October 10, 2024, the NAV per unit for DFGH stands at AUD 1.35, a rise from AUD 1.25 at the beginning of the year. This represents an increase of about 8%, aligning with its year-to-date performance and indicating strong underlying asset appreciation.

- Risk-Adjusted Returns:

The fund has a Sharpe ratio of 1.1, suggesting that it has achieved favorable returns relative to the volatility taken. A ratio above 1 generally indicates that the fund is providing good returns for the level of risk.

- Expense Ratio:

DFGH maintains a low expense ratio of 0.35%, which is competitive among actively managed ETFs. This cost efficiency contributes positively to the overall returns experienced by investors.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

DFGH currently has a trailing P/E ratio of 17.5, which is in line with the average for core equity funds but slightly below the broader market average of 18. This suggests that the fund may present a more attractive valuation relative to its potential earnings.

- Price-to-Book (P/B) Ratio:

The fund’s P/B ratio is 1.8, which is indicative of a slight premium, suggesting that investors are willing to pay more for each dollar of net assets, reflecting confidence in its long-term growth prospects.

- Discount to NAV:

The current market price of DFGH is trading at a slight discount of about 1% to its NAV, which is reasonable and indicates that investors are not overly bullish nor bearish, but are taking a measured approach towards its valuation.
