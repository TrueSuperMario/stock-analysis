Current Performance

- - Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of September 30, 2024, the SPDR S&P/ASX 200 Fund (STW) has demonstrated solid year-to-date performance, with revenue from the underlying S&P/ASX 200 index rising approximately 8%. This growth has been buoyed by a strong performance in sectors such as materials, healthcare, and consumer discretionary.

- - Profit Margins:

The fund's profit margins have remained stable, reflecting the overall health of the ASX 200 constituents. The average profit margin for the index stands at around 12%, indicating the resilience of companies within the index against economic headwinds.

- - Earnings Per Share (EPS):

EPS for the top 10 holdings in the ASX 200 has shown a growth rate of about 6% year-on-year, bolstered by improved operational efficiencies, cost management, and a recovery in consumer spending post-pandemic.

- - Return on Equity (ROE):

The weighted average ROE of the ASX 200 constituents is approximately 14%, reflecting efficient utilization of equity capital across leading firms in the index.

Valuation Metrics

- - Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

STW currently has a P/E ratio of 18.5, which aligns closely with the historical average for the ASX 200. This suggests a fair valuation relative to earnings, indicating that the market is reasonably optimistic about future earnings growth.

- - P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

The P/E ratio for STW slightly exceeds the broader market average of 17.5 for Australian equities, suggesting that investors are willing to pay a premium for exposure to the top performers in the index.
