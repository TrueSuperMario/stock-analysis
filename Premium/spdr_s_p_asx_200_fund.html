Current Performance

- - Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of September 25, 2024, the SPDR S&P/ASX 200 Fund (STW) has shown steady performance in line with the broader Australian equity market. The fund’s year-to-date return stands at approximately 7%, reflecting robust underlying performance from the ASX 200 constituents. The earnings growth among these constituents averaged around 10%, assisted by a resurgence in consumer spending and recovery in several sectors post-COVID.

- - Profit Margins:

The average profit margin for the companies within the ASX 200 has improved to approximately 12%, driven largely by efficiency gains and cost management strategies employed across various sectors. This suggests that firms within the ETF are managing to maintain healthy margins despite broader economic pressures.

- - Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The current EPS for the underlying companies aggregated in the ASX 200 index is around AUD 4.22, showing a solid increase from AUD 3.85 in the previous year, reflecting the improving profitability of Australian corporates.

- - Return on Equity (ROE):

The ROE for the ASX 200 as a whole stands at around 14%, which is indicative of effective management and strong capital utilization within its portfolio companies.

Valuation Metrics

- - Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for STW is approximately 18.5, suggesting a slightly overvalued status compared to historical averages. This indicates that investors might be expecting continued growth despite recent volatility in global markets.

- - P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

The average P/E ratio of the ASX 200 is higher than the global market average of 16.1, reflecting investor confidence in the Australian economy and the potential for continued growth in sectors such as technology and healthcare.
