Current Performance

- - Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of September 2024, the SPDR S&P/ASX 200 Fund (STW) has shown a year-to-date revenue growth of around 8%, primarily fueled by an overall bullish trend in the Australian equity market. Earnings per unit (EPU) have increased by approximately 5%, reflecting positive contributions from key sectors such as financials and materials, which make up a significant portion of the ASX 200 index.

- - Profit Margins:

The Fund's profit margins are indirectly indicated by the returns of its underlying assets. The average net profit margins of the top 10 holdings are stable at about 12%, showcasing solid profitability across various industries, particularly among banking and mining sectors.

- - Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The current EPS for STW is reported at AUD 1.45, reflecting a steady increase from AUD 1.38 in the prior year. This growth is attributed to a recovering economy and a resurgence in consumer confidence, positively impacting the performance of large-cap equities.

- - Return on Equity (ROE):

The Fund’s approximate ROE is 11%, indicating efficient use of equity capital across the diversified portfolio it holds. The ROE aligns with historic averages for the ASX 200 index, showcasing a solid performance from its constituent companies.

Valuation Metrics

- - Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for STW is approximately 17, which is in line with the broader market average for the ASX 200. This indicates the fund is fairly valued when considering growth potential and market conditions.

- - P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

Comparing STW’s P/E ratio with that of the broader Australian market, STW is slightly below the average of 18 for diversified equity funds, suggesting a modest undervaluation relative to projected earnings growth.
