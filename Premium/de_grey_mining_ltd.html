The company currently operates at a negative net profit margin of -6%, reflecting significant expenditures on exploration and development activities. While this is typical for junior mining companies in high-growth phases, attention to cost management will be crucial for future profitability.

- - Earnings Per Share (EPS):

DEG's EPS is currently at -$0.05, illustrating ongoing investment phases with no profits generated yet. Analysts expect a turning point as the company transitions into production within the next couple of years.

- - Return on Equity (ROE):

ROE is currently difficult to measure meaningfully, as the company has yet to produce sustainable profits. Nonetheless, the cash reserve position is robust, allowing for continued investments in exploration.
