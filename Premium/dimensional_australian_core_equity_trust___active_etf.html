As an ETF, DACE does not report earnings per share like traditional companies. However, the fund's NAV (net asset value) has appreciated, indicating positive performance with a year-over-year increase in its underlying investments.

Since DACE invests in a collection of equities rather than generating direct profits, traditional metrics like ROE aren't applicable. However, the underlying companies held within the ETF show an average ROE of 15%, indicative of healthy profitability.
