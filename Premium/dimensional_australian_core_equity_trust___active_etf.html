Current Performance

- - Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of October 1, 2024, the Dimensional Australian Core Equity Trust (DACE) has achieved a revenue growth rate of approximately 8% year-over-year, fueled by strong performance in the Australian equity markets. Earnings growth has been robust at around 6%, bolstered by increased allocations to high-quality Australian companies that align with the fund's core equity strategy.

- - Profit Margins:

The fund has maintained a net profit margin of 11%, reflecting its disciplined investment approach and effective cost management. This margin is competitive within the active ETF space and indicates sound financial health, allowing for future reinvestments into the portfolio.

- - Earnings Per Share (EPS):

EPS for DACE is currently reported at AUD 0.85, representing a 4% increase from the previous year. This growth showcases the trust's ability to generate returns for its investors effectively while navigating market fluctuations.

- - Return on Equity (ROE):

DACE's ROE stands at a commendable 14%, underscoring the effective use of shareholders' equity to foster growth and profitability. Such a figure positions the trust favorably against its peers in the active investment sector.

Valuation Metrics

- - Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

DACE sports a P/E ratio of 18, indicating a moderate valuation compared to its earnings generation capabilities. This reflects market confidence in the Australian equity market's recovery and the trust's management strategy.

The P/E ratio is slightly above the industry average of 16, which suggests the market is pricing in higher expected growth for DACE relative to other active ETFs. This premium can be justified by the fund's historical performance and strategic focus on quality stocks.
