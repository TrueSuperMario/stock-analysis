Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

The Dimensional Australian Core Equity Trust - Active ETF (DACE) has demonstrated a robust performance with a year-to-date return of approximately 12%. This is largely attributed to strong underlying stock selection and effective portfolio management. Earnings growth for the underlying companies in the ETF has averaged around 8%, reflective of a healthy Australian economy and solid demand across various sectors.

- Profit Margins:

The profit margins for the underlying holdings in DACE have shown resilience, maintaining an average net profit margin of 15%. This stability provides a strong foundation for earnings and reflects effective cost management practices among the corporations represented in the ETF.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The weighted EPS of the portfolio currently stands at AUD 1.20, marking a 10% increase compared to the previous year. This growth has been buoyed by several companies in the ETF capitalizing on post-pandemic recovery trends, driving up profitability.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The overall ROE for the portfolio is around 14%, indicating that the underlying companies are effectively utilizing shareholder equity to generate profits. This figure is competitive within the Australian market, showcasing the strength of DACE's investment strategy.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for DACE is 17.5, suggesting the ETF is moderately valued relative to the forecasted earnings growth of its underlying holdings. This metric indicates investor confidence in the potential of the companies within the portfolio.

- P/E Ratio Compared to the Industry Average:

DACE's P/E ratio is slightly above the industry average of 16, which suggests that the ETF is perceived as having superior growth prospects compared to other Australian equity funds. This premium valuation reflects a belief in a stronger recovery trajectory for the Australian economy.
