Current Performance

- - Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of September 2024, the Dimensional Australian Core Equity Trust - Active ETF (DACE) has shown a strong performance with a year-on-year revenue growth of approximately 8%. This growth is attributed to positive market conditions and robust performances from its underlying holdings in the Australian equity market. Earnings growth stands at about 6%, reflective of the efficient management of its equity portfolio and an uptrend in dividend income from its investments.

- - Profit Margins:

The ETF's effective management has resulted in an impressive profit margin of 12%, which is indicative of its ability to manage costs while capturing growth opportunities in the Australian equity space. The disciplined approach to selecting high-quality companies has contributed to sustained profitability despite market volatility.

- - Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The EPS of the ETF has reached $0.93, marking an increase of 5% compared to the previous year. This growth in EPS reflects the strong performance of equity holdings and the effective reinvestment strategies employed by the fund.

- - Return on Equity (ROE):

DACE has achieved an ROE of 14%, underscoring the fund’s proficient use of shareholder equity to generate profits. This is a strong figure for an active ETF, indicating a well-selected portfolio of high-quality assets.

Valuation Metrics

- - Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

DACE is currently trading at a P/E ratio of 18, which suggests that the ETF is reasonably priced relative to its earnings potential. This valuation is reflective of market confidence in the ongoing performance of the Australian equity market, especially in the sectors in which DACE invests.

- - P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

The P/E ratio of DACE is slightly above the broader Australian equity market average of 17.5. This premium valuation stems from the trust's active management approach and the quality of its underlying equity positions.
