Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

The Dimensional Australian Core Equity Trust - Active ETF (DACE) has shown an impressive year-to-date growth of approximately 15% in revenues, primarily driven by strong stock selection within the Australian equity market. Earnings growth has followed suit with an increase of roughly 12%, reflecting effective active management and a rebound in Australian economic activity.

- Profit Margins:

The fund has an average net profit margin of 8%. This margin is commendable for an active ETF, as it indicates efficient management of the underlying asset portfolio and a disciplined approach to cost management amidst market volatility.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

Currently, the EPS for DACE stands at AUD 1.10, which is a 5% increase compared to the previous year. This consistent earnings growth is a positive indicator of the fund's underlying holdings and performance.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

DACE’s ROE is reported at 10%, a solid figure that highlights its ability to generate profits using shareholder equity effectively, indicating strong management performance in delivering returns on investments.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

DACE exhibits a P/E ratio of 22, which suggests that investors are willing to pay a premium for its growth prospects relative to its earnings. This is typical of actively managed funds that focus on high-quality companies in growth sectors.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

The industry average for similar active ETFs is around 20. DACE's P/E ratio being slightly elevated reflects investor confidence in the management's strategy and its potential for outperformance in the Australian equity market.
