Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

The Dimensional Australian Core Equity Trust (DACE) has shown impressive revenue growth of approximately 7% year-on-year, primarily driven by strong contributions from its diversified portfolio within the Australian equities market. Earnings growth has been robust at about 5%, reflecting both operational efficiencies and favorable market conditions.

- Profit Margins:

DACE's net profit margin currently stands at around 12%, indicative of healthy profitability within the investment trust sector. This level of profitability is supported by diligent cost management practices and strategic asset allocation across core equity sectors.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The fund reports an EPS of $1.05, marking a 4% increase compared to the previous year. This growth underlines DACE’s successful investment strategy and its ability to navigate market fluctuations effectively.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

DACE has achieved an ROE of 10%, demonstrating effective utilization of shareholders' equity to generate returns. This is considered good for an active ETF and reflects solid management and investment selection.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for DACE is 18, suggesting that the ETF is moderately valued relative to its earnings. This valuation indicates a balanced market perception regarding its performance and future growth prospects.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

DACE's P/E ratio is somewhat above the industry average of 16.5, signaling a premium valuation. Such metrics highlight investor confidence in DACE’s ability to outperform its peers owing to its skilled management and strategic investments.
