Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

The Dimensional Australian Core Equity Trust - Active ETF (DACE) has reported strong performance metrics, with a 7% year-on-year revenue growth largely driven by robust market conditions and fundamental strengths across its equity holdings. Earnings growth has been slightly higher at 8%, reflecting effective portfolio management and strategic allocation to high-growth sectors.

- Profit Margins:

DACE currently enjoys a net profit margin of 11%, a healthy figure indicative of efficient management practices and cost control. Despite fluctuations in market valuations, the trust's proactive rebalancing has helped maintain stable margins.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The ETF presents an EPS of $1.20, which signifies a commendable 5% increase compared to the previous year. This growth is attributed to both capital appreciation in equity holdings and reinvestment strategies that have been successfully implemented.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

DACE’s ROE is approximately 14%, indicating effective utilization of capital invested by shareholders. This figure suggests that the ETF is generating significant returns relative to equity, which is a positive indicator for investors.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for DACE stands at 18, indicating a reasonably valued position relative to its historical performance. This valuation reflects fair market expectations of the trust’s capabilities to continue generating earnings growth.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

DACE’s P/E ratio is roughly in line with the industry average of 17.5, reflecting the competitiveness of its positions and the overall market outlook for Australian equities.
