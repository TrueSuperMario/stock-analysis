Current Performance

- - Revenue and Earnings Growth:

The Dimensional Australian Core Equity Trust - Active ETF (DACE) has shown a robust performance with a year-on-year revenue growth of approximately 8%. This growth has been underpinned by a strong rebound in the Australian equity market, with DACE’s active management strategy effectively capitalizing on undervalued stocks within its portfolio.

- - Profit Margins:

DACE has maintained a profit margin of around 12%, which is commendable for an active ETF. This margin reflects the fund's strategic selection of equities that have demonstrated resilience and profitability despite fluctuating market conditions.

- - Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The ETF reports an EPS of $1.45, an increase of 5% compared to the previous year. This growth exemplifies the fund's ability to generate consistent earnings amidst ongoing market volatility, showcasing the efficacy of its active management approach.

- - Return on Equity (ROE):

DACE’s ROE stands at 11%, indicating a strong return on the equity employed in the fund. This metric suggests that the ETF is efficiently utilizing its capital to deliver profits, which is critical for attracting and retaining investors.

The ETF’s P/E ratio reflects investor confidence in its potential for future earnings growth, particularly in a recovering economic environment. While it is above the industry average, the growth projections suggest that this premium may be justified given its recent performance enhancements.
