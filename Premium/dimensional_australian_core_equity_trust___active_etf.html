Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

The Dimensional Australian Core Equity Trust - Active ETF (DACE) has demonstrated a robust performance in the Australian equity market. As of the latest reporting period, it has achieved a year-to-date return of approximately 12%, benefiting from a rising equity market and a recovery in sectors such as financials and consumer discretionary. Earnings growth for the underlying companies within the ETF has averaged around 6%, reflecting a post-COVID recovery trend across many sectors.

- Profit Margins:

The profit margins of the companies within the DACE portfolio are healthy, averaging 15%. This is indicative of effective cost management practices across the sectors it is invested in, particularly in technology and healthcare, which have shown improved operational efficiency.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The average EPS for the DACE portfolio's holdings is reported at $1.85, which represents an 8% increase from the previous year. This growth is driven by strong performances in high-growth companies featured in the ETF's selection.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The ROE for the underlying assets in DACE averages 14%, highlighting effective use of shareholder equity to generate profits. This performance metric underscores the ETF’s strategic focus on quality companies with strong management and profitable growth trajectories.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio of DACE stands at 20, which reflects a moderate valuation within the context of the broader Australian market. This suggests investors are willing to pay a premium for future earnings growth anticipated from the fund's diversified portfolio.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

The P/E ratio is slightly above the Australian equity market average of around 18, suggesting a premium pricing that is justified by the expected growth prospects of the holdings, particularly those in technology and health care.
