Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of October 2024, the Dimensional Australian Core Equity Trust - Active ETF (DACE) has shown robust performance with a year-to-date return of approximately 12%. This growth has been bolstered by strong performances from key sectors, particularly financials and materials, which have rebounded after a period of volatility. Earnings growth for the underlying companies in the ETF portfolio has been around 8%, reflecting a recovery in corporate earnings alongside improved economic conditions.

- Profit Margins:

The ETF's portfolio companies report an average net profit margin of around 15%, which is healthy within the Australian market. This margin demonstrates the companies' ability to manage expenses effectively while benefitting from higher revenue. Some sectors, such as technology, have contributed significantly to these margins due to operational efficiencies.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The weighted average EPS for the top holdings within DACE stands at AUD 1.30, marking a 5% increase compared to the prior year. This growth indicates a positive trajectory for profitability across the portfolio.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The ETF shows an aggregate ROE of 13%, suggesting that the underlying companies effectively utilize shareholder equity to generate returns. This ROE is in line with historical averages for Australian equities, reinforcing the ETF's appeal.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for DACE is approximately 18, reflecting a slight premium compared to the broader Australian market average of 16. This suggests that the market has a favorable outlook on the growth prospects of the ETF’s holdings.

- P/E Ratio Compared to the Industry Average:

DACE’s P/E ratio is above the average for the ASX 200 index, indicating a strong growth expectation among investors. The quality of underlying stocks, particularly in technology and healthcare, supports this valuation premium.
