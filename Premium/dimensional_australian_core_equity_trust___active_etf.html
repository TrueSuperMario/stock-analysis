Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of October 2024, the Dimensional Australian Core Equity Trust - Active ETF (DACE) has recorded a year-on-year revenue growth of approximately 8%. This growth is attributed to strong performance in the Australian equity market, particularly in sectors such as healthcare and consumer goods. Earnings growth for DACE, on the other hand, has been more modest at around 5%, reflecting moderate increases in operating expenses and market volatility.

- Profit Margins:

DACE maintains a net profit margin of 12%. This is indicative of effective fund management and investment strategies, enabling it to weather economic fluctuations better than some of its peers. The fund's focus on quality companies has helped maintain these margins despite rising costs.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The ETF’s EPS is currently at AUD 1.20, which shows a 4% increase from the previous year, highlighting the steady growth in earnings from its underlying holdings. This performance is supported by a diversified portfolio across various sectors that have remained resilient.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

DACE’s ROE stands at 15%, reflecting efficient use of capital in generating earnings. This is a strong indicator of the ETF's effective investment strategy, particularly in a competitive market environment.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for DACE is 18, suggesting that the ETF is fairly valued compared to its growth potential. This level reflects investor confidence yet is still within range of historical averages for Australian equities.

- P/E Ratio Compared to the Industry Average:

DACE’s P/E ratio is slightly above the average for Australian equity ETFs, which is around 16. This premium valuation can be justified by its robust performance metrics and experienced management team, which tends to focus on quality growth stocks.
