Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of November 2024, Dimensional Australian Core Equity Trust - Active ETF (DACE) reported a year-to-date revenue growth of 7%, benefiting from a robust equity market recovery and strong performance from its core Australian investments. Earnings growth is more pronounced, at 10%, due to effective cost management and a strategic focus on high-quality companies.

- Profit Margins:

The fund maintains a net profit margin of 12%, which is favorable within the equity fund space. This is reflective of a disciplined investment approach aimed at quality companies with sustainable competitive advantages, allowing for better margin preservation despite market volatility.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The ETF's current EPS is reported at $0.85, representing a 5% increase compared to the previous year. This growth is indicative of strong portfolio performance as well as a diversified investment strategy that mitigates risks effectively.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The reported ROE stands at 14%, showcasing effective management of shareholder equity to generate returns. This is a solid metric, especially for an actively managed fund, emphasizing the trust's capability to choose winning stocks.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for DACE is approximately 18, suggesting the ETF is moderately valued in comparison to its potential earnings. This level reflects optimism in the Australian stock market's recovery, though it indicates some caution as valuations are not excessively high.

- P/E Ratio Compared to the Industry Average:

DACE's P/E is slightly higher than the average of 16 for its sector, implying that investors are willing to pay a premium for the fund’s growth potential and management effectiveness.
