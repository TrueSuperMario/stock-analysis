Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of December 2024, Dimensional Australian Core Equity Trust (DACE) has demonstrated a robust revenue growth rate of approximately 8% year-on-year, which can be attributed to a favorable market environment and solid performance from underlying Australian equities. The earnings growth reflects a similar trend, with an increase of around 6%.

- Profit Margins:

The net profit margin for DACE currently stands at approximately 12%. This margin is reasonable for an equity-focused fund, indicating effective cost management and operational efficiencies in navigating market volatilities.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

DACE has an EPS of $1.15, which reflects a 5% increase compared to last year's figure. This growth underscores the fund's ability to generate consistent income from its core equity holdings in the Australian market.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The trust has reported an ROE of 10%, indicating a stable utilization of equity capital to produce earnings. This figure is indicative of a well-managed fund and its effectiveness in leveraging investor capital.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for DACE is 18, which suggests a moderate valuation relative to its earnings potential. This valuation indicates confidence in the trust’s investment strategy and its potential for further earnings growth.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

DACE’s P/E ratio is slightly above the average for similar Australian equity funds, which stands at 16. This premium valuation reflects the trust’s strategic positioning and the quality of its underlying assets within the portfolio.
