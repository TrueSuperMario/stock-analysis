Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of January 27, 2025, the Dimensional Australian Core Equity Trust - Active ETF (DACE) has shown a steady increase in revenue, with a year-on-year growth rate of 8%, primarily driven by positive movements in the Australian equity market and strong performances from its underlying companies. However, earnings growth has lagged slightly at 6%, impacted by rising operational costs and market volatility.

- Profit Margins:

The ETF’s net profit margin stands at 13%, which reflects a strong performance for an equity fund, especially considering the equity market's inherent volatility. The focus on core Australian equities provides a level of stability to these margins.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

DACE's EPS has reached $1.10, indicating a 5% increase from the prior year. This reflects the ETF's adaptability in a fluctuating market, and its ability to generate suitable returns for investors through selective stock picking.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The ETF reports an ROE of 9%, which is a positive indicator of how effectively it utilizes shareholder equity to generate profits, maintaining a balanced and performance-oriented investment strategy.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

DACE currently has a P/E ratio of 18.5, which reflects a moderate valuation considering the growth expectations of the underlying assets. This suggests that investors are willing to pay a premium for equity exposure in a diversified Australian portfolio.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

The P/E ratio for DACE is slightly above the industry average of about 17, indicating a premium valuation. This is supported by the ETF’s strong performance and rigorous stock selection strategy in the core equity space in Australia.
