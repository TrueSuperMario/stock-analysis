Current Performance

- - Revenue and Earnings Growth:

Dimensional Global Core Equity Trust Unhedged - Active ETF (DGCE) has demonstrated a year-to-date revenue growth of approximately 8%, fueled by a rebound in global equity markets and strategic stock selections that capitalize on undervalued companies. Earnings growth has been slightly more moderate at around 5%, reflecting a diversified portfolio approach and robust active management strategies.

- - Profit Margins:

The fund's net profit margin currently stands at around 12%, indicative of efficient operational management and the ability to generate income beyond expenses. This margin remains attractive, especially when benchmarked against other actively managed equity ETFs.

- - Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The EPS for DGCE has reached $1.85, a 3% increase compared to the previous year. This growth can be attributed to effective stock picking and a favorable market environment for the constituents of the fund.

- - Return on Equity (ROE):

DGCE maintains an ROE of 11%, suggesting a strong ability to generate profits from shareholder equity. This figure is competitive, especially within the context of diversified equity ETFs, reflecting the trust's adept management style.

Valuation Metrics

- - Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The P/E ratio for DGCE is currently at 18, which is slightly above the broader market average of 17. This suggests that investors may be willing to pay a premium for the trust's active management and perceived ability to outperform passive indices.

- - P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

Compared to similar actively managed funds, DGCE's P/E ratio is competitive, indicating that while there may be a premium due to the fund’s active management, the valuation is consistent with industry expectations for actively managed equity strategies.
