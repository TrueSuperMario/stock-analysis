Current Performance

- - Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of the latest quarter, Dimensional Global Core Equity Trust Unhedged - Active ETF (DGCE) has reported a robust revenue growth of 8% year-on-year, reflecting the underlying strength of its diversified core equity portfolio. However, earnings growth has been slightly less, at 5%, attributed to increased operational costs and market volatility affecting certain holdings.

- - Profit Margins:

DGCE has maintained a net profit margin of 9%, which is commendable for an active equity ETF. This reflects the fund’s strategic emphasis on high-quality companies and disciplined management, although market fluctuations have narrowed margins slightly compared to the previous year.

- - Estimated Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The ETF's EPS is currently estimated at $1.25, representing a modest 3% increase from the prior year. This incremental growth is reflective of the moderate performance of equities in which DGCE is invested, stabilizing after recent market recessions.

- - Return on Equity (ROE):

Although traditional ROE metrics may not apply directly to ETFs like DGCE, it maintains a strong performance when considering the underlying equities, with the average ROE of the firms in its portfolio hovering around 15%. This indicates robust utilization of equity capital across its holdings.

Valuation Metrics

- - Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for DGCE stands at 21, suggesting a premium valuation relative to its earnings potential. This reflects investor confidence in the careful selection of high-quality growth companies, despite broader market uncertainties.

- - P/E Ratio Compared to the Industry Average:

The P/E ratio is notably above the overall equity ETF average of 18, suggesting that while DGCE is considered a premium offering in the active ETF space, this premium is justified by its strong performance and diversified strategy.
