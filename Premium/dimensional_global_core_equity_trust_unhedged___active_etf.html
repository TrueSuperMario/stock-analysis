Current Performance

Revenue and Earnings Growth:

Dimensional Global Core Equity Trust Unhedged - Active ETF (DGCE) has delivered a notable performance with year-to-date returns of approximately 8%. This growth can largely be attributed to the broad recovery in global equities as markets responded positively to lower inflation readings and easing interest rate fears. The earnings growth of the underlying equities within the ETF has seen an average increase of around 6% year-over-year, reflecting resilience in corporate profitability.

Profit Margins:

The ETF primarily invests in core equities with consistent cash flows. The profit margins of its holdings are robust, averaging around 11%, which is indicative of the strong operational efficiency within the sectors invested in, particularly in technology and healthcare.

Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The average EPS for the companies under DGCE has improved to $1.25, marking a 5% increase compared to the previous year’s EPS of $1.19. This EPS growth reflects successful business rebounds in various sectors post-pandemic.

Return on Equity (ROE):

The ROE for the fund’s holdings stands at a commendable 14%. This illustrates effective management and efficient utilization of shareholders' equity, positioning the ETF positively among its peers.

Valuation Metrics

Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

As of now, DGCE has a P/E ratio of 22, which indicates a relative premium compared to the overall market average of 19. This suggests that investors are willing to pay a bit more for expected growth potential.

P/E Ratio compared to industry average:

The current P/E ratio is slightly above the average for the core equity sector, which is around 20. This reflects the market’s optimism about the long-term growth prospects of the ETF's diversified equity holdings.

Dividend History:

DGCE has maintained a consistent dividend policy, with small, steady increases annually. This reflects a commitment to returning value to shareholders while focusing on growth through reinvestment.
