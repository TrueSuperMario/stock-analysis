Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

The Dimensional Global Core Equity Trust Unhedged – Active ETF (DGCE) has reported a year-to-date revenue growth of approximately 8%. This growth has been driven by a robust performance in the equities held within the fund, particularly in sectors that are currently experiencing favorable conditions such as technology and healthcare. Earnings growth has shown a stronger pace at 10%, reflecting effective stock selection and the overall bullish market sentiment.

- Profit Margins:

The ETF has maintained a net profit margin of around 9%, which is commendable for an actively managed equity fund. This margin indicates the trust's ability to convert a substantial portion of its revenues into profit, despite the competitive nature of active management.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

Currently, DGCE has an EPS of $2.45, marking a 5% increase from the prior year. This upward trend in EPS is a positive indicator of the trust’s effective portfolio management and strategic asset allocation.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The trust's ROE stands at 11%, suggesting that it efficiently utilizes shareholders' equity to generate profits. This figure reflects well on the trust's management and investment strategies, particularly in capitalizing on market opportunities.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for DGCE is 22, indicating a premium valuation compared to the market average of approximately 18. This elevated ratio points to investor optimism regarding the long-term growth potential of the fund’s underlying holdings.

- P/E Ratio Compared to the Industry Average:

The P/E ratio of 22 is significantly above the average for actively managed equity ETFs, which hovers around 19. This suggests that DGCE's holdings are perceived as superior in terms of growth prospects or quality.
