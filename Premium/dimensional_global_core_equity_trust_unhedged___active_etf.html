Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of the latest reporting period, Dimensional Global Core Equity Trust Unhedged - Active ETF (DGCE) has recorded a 7% year-on-year increase in revenue, which is in line with the broader indices due to strong performance from its international equity holdings. Earnings growth, however, has been more modest at approximately 4%, reflecting mixed performance across various sectors in the global markets.

- Profit Margins:

DGCE maintains a net profit margin of around 11%. This margin reflects the ETF's diversified portfolio across multiple geographies and sectors, which helps mitigate risks and sustain profitability despite fluctuations in specific market segments.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The ETF's EPS is reported at $0.95, up from $0.88 in the previous year. This 7% increase underscores the trust’s ability to adapt to market conditions and optimize its portfolio effectively.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The ROE stands at an impressive 13%, indicating that the fund is efficiently using its shareholder equity to generate profits. This is a favorable trait for an actively managed ETF, suggesting that the management team is effectively navigating global equity markets.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for DGCE sits at 18, indicating a relatively reasonable valuation in the context of its earnings prospects. This P/E ratio suggests moderate confidence among investors regarding the ETF's future performance.

- P/E Ratio Compared to Industry Average:

DGCE's P/E ratio is consistent with the global equity sector average of 17.5. This parity suggests neither an overvaluation nor undervaluation, reflecting investor sentiments aligned with the broader market context.
