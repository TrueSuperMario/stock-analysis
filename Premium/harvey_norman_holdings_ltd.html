Current Performance

Revenue and Earnings Growth:

Harvey Norman Holdings Ltd has reported a 4% decline in year-on-year revenue, primarily due to weakened consumer spending and increased competition in the retail sector. The company's earnings before interest and tax (EBIT) decreased by approximately 6%, reflecting the challenging market conditions and the impact of inflation on consumer behavior.

Profit Margins:

The net profit margin for Harvey Norman currently stands at about 8%, which shows a minor contraction compared to the previous year. This reduction in margins can be attributed to rising costs associated with logistics and staffing, along with increased promotional activities to drive sales amidst declining demand.

Earnings Per Share (EPS):

EPS has dropped to $0.62, down from $0.66 in the prior year. This 6% decline indicates the pressures facing the retail sector and suggests that Harvey Norman is struggling to maintain profitability in a challenging environment.

Return on Equity (ROE):

The current ROE for Harvey Norman is reported at 15%, which remains solid despite the headwinds. This suggests efficient use of shareholder equity in generating profits, although it has dipped slightly from previous levels.
