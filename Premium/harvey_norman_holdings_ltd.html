Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

Harvey Norman has reported a revenue growth of 7% year-on-year, driven by expanding online sales and strong performance in home improvement segments. Earnings growth has been slightly lower at 5%, attributed to increased operational expenses and supply chain challenges.

- Profit Margins:

The company’s net profit margin currently stands at around 8%, which indicates healthy profitability despite the pressures from inflation and fluctuating commodity prices. Operational efficiencies and a strong brand presence have helped maintain these margins.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

EPS for Harvey Norman has increased to AUD 0.95, reflecting a 3% growth compared to the previous year. This growth aligns with the company's strategic focus on e-commerce and diversification of product offerings.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The ROE for Harvey Norman is 15%, showcasing effective management of shareholders' funds to generate profits. This figure is competitive within the retail sector and highlights the company’s strong operational performance and brand loyalty.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for Harvey Norman is 16, indicating that the stock is reasonably valued relative to its earnings potential. This valuation appears attractive compared to historical averages and indicates market confidence in the company's business model.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

Harvey Norman’s P/E ratio is slightly below the retail industry average of 18, suggesting that the stock may be undervalued in relation to its peers, offering potential upside for investors.

Analyst Recommendations

- Consensus Rating:

- Price Targets:

- Dividend History:

Harvey Norman has a strong dividend history with consistent payments over the years. Recent increases in dividends reflect the company’s capability to generate solid returns and its commitment to returning value to shareholders.
