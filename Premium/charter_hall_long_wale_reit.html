Current Performance

- - Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of Q3 2024, Charter Hall Long Wale REIT (CLW) has reported a revenue growth of approximately 4% year-on-year, driven by steady demand for long-term leased properties. Earnings growth has been modest at around 3%, reflecting consistent rental income and effective management of operational expenses.

- - Profit Margins:

The net profit margin for CLW sits at approximately 45%, which is solid for a real estate investment trust (REIT). The company benefits from stable rental contracts and low vacancy rates, helping to maintain high profit margins in a competitive market.

- - Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The current EPS for CLW is $0.85, marking an increase of 5% from the same period last year. This growth indicates the REIT’s effective asset management and commitment to providing returns to investors.

- - Return on Equity (ROE):

The ROE for CLW is at 10%, signaling efficient use of equity to generate profits. This performance level is considered reasonable within the REIT sector, reflecting healthy financial management.

Valuation Metrics

- - Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

CLW has a current P/E ratio of 18, indicating a moderate valuation relative to its earnings. This valuation suggests that investors are willing to pay a premium for the REIT’s stable income characteristics.

- - P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

The P/E ratio for CLW is slightly above the average for the REIT sector, which stands at around 16. This discrepancy reflects investor confidence in CLW's long-term rental contracts and its reputation as a reliable income-generating asset.
