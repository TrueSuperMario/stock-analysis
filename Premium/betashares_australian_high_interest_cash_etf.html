Current Performance

- - Fund Size and Assets Under Management (AUM):

As of October 1, 2024, Betashares Australian High Interest Cash ETF (AAA) has an AUM of approximately AUD 2.1 billion, reflecting significant investor interest in high-yielding cash alternatives.

- - Performance Relative to Benchmark:

The ETF has delivered a year-to-date return of approximately 3.5%, aligning closely with interest rate trends and the Australian cash rate, which has seen a recent uptick due to the Reserve Bank of Australia’s tightening monetary policy.

- - Risk Metrics:

The ETF is classified as low-risk, with a very low standard deviation of returns, highlighting its focus on capital preservation rather than capital gains. The fund primarily invests in cash and cash-like instruments, which are known for their stability.

Valuation Metrics

- - Expense Ratio:

AAA has a low expense ratio of 0.18%, making it a cost-effective option for investors looking to gain exposure to cash-like securities without incurring significant fees.

- - Yield Comparison:

The current distribution yield of AAA is around 4.1%, which is competitive compared to traditional savings accounts and term deposits, indicating that the ETF is effectively capturing high interest rates available in the market.
