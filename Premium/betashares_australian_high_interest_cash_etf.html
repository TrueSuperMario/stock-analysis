Current Performance

- Fund Performance:

As of October 22, 2024, the Betashares Australian High Interest Cash ETF (AAA) has experienced a year-to-date return of approximately 3.5%, driven by an upward trend in interest rates set by the Reserve Bank of Australia (RBA). The fund has effectively capitalized on these rising rates, allowing for enhanced yield generation through its investments in high-interest cash accounts and term deposits.

- Fund Size and Growth:

The AAA fund currently manages assets totaling around AUD 800 million, indicative of strong investor interest in cash-like investments amidst market volatility. The fund's size allows it to maintain competitive yields while managing liquidity effectively.

- Net Asset Value (NAV):

The current NAV per unit stands at AUD 51.25, reflecting a slight increase from previous months as interest rates have trended upwards. This highlights the fund’s ability to provide capital stability and income generation.

Valuation Metrics

- Expense Ratio:

The expense ratio for AAA is at a low 0.18%, making it an attractive option for cost-conscious investors. This efficiency allows a more significant portion of the fund's yield to be passed on to its investors.

- Yield Metrics:

The fund currently offers an annualized yield of approximately 4.2%, which is competitive relative to other low-risk investment options. This yield reflects the higher interest rates available in the market.

- Comparison to Cash Rate:

With the current RBA cash rate at 4.0%, the fund offers a slight premium over traditional cash holdings, appealing to investors seeking better returns without significant risk.
