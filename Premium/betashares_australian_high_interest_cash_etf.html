Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of October 2024, the Betashares Australian High Interest Cash ETF (AAA) has generated an annualized return of approximately 3.75%, which reflects a favorable interest rate environment. The growth in revenue is largely tied to rising cash deposit rates in the Australian banking sector, providing a consistent yield for investors.

- Profit Margins:

Given the structure of AAA, it operates with a unique profitability profile. The ETF's income stems exclusively from interest on cash holdings, resulting in minimal operational expenses. While traditional profit margins do not apply, it effectively captures interest income with very low cost implications, maintaining high yields relative to the benchmarks.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

EPS is not a relevant metric for AAA, as it operates primarily as a cash management vehicle rather than a traditional corporation that generates earnings. Instead, it's essential to monitor the yield generated by the ETF, which is more indicative of its performance.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

ROE is also an inapplicable measure for AAA; its performance should be evaluated based on yield relative to risk-adjusted benchmarks rather than equity utilization, which is not a primary concern for cash ETFs.
