Current Performance

- Fund Performance Metrics:

As of February 10, 2025, the BetaShares Australian High Interest Cash ETF (AAA) has delivered a year-to-date return of approximately 4.5%. This performance aligns closely with the current high-interest rate environment in Australia, which has driven increased yields for cash and cash-equivalent instruments.

- Net Asset Value (NAV) Growth:

The NAV of AAA has been relatively stable amidst fluctuating economic conditions, with a slight increase of 1.2% over the past three months, indicating investor confidence in high-interest cash investments amid uncertainty in equity markets.

- Expense Ratio:

The ETF has a low expense ratio of 0.18%, which is favorable and contributes positively to net returns for investors. This efficiency makes AAA an attractive option for those seeking to preserve capital while earning interest.

Valuation Metrics

- Yield Metrics:

AAA currently offers an annualized yield of around 4.3%, which is competitive compared to traditional savings accounts. This yield represents a key reason why investors are leaning towards cash ETFs in the current economic climate.

- Comparison to Benchmarks:

The yield offered by AAA is significantly higher than the average savings account interest rate in Australia, which hovers around 2%. This premium yield enhances the ETF’s attractiveness during a period of economic volatility.
