Current Performance

- - Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of September 30, 2024, Vanguard US Total Market Shares INDEX ETF (VTS) has demonstrated robust performance, with a year-to-date revenue growth rate of approximately 8%. This is largely attributed to the overall strength of the U.S. equity market, which has benefited from positive corporate earnings reports and economic resilience. Earnings for the ETF have risen in line with the underlying index, reflecting the broader market's upward trend.

- - Profit Margins:

VTS mirrors the profit margins of its underlying index, showcasing a strong aggregate net profit margin of around 13%. This level is healthy for an index fund, demonstrating the stability and profitability across the diverse range of companies represented in the total market.

- - Earnings Per Share (EPS):

Although ETFs do not report EPS in the traditional sense, the underlying assets within VTS have seen an overall weighted EPS growth of about 6% year-on-year, showcasing the consistency in profit generation across its holdings.

- - Return on Equity (ROE):

The ROE for the underlying companies within the ETF averages 15%, indicating efficient utilization of shareholder equity to generate profits. This figure underscores the overall soundness of the companies in the index.

Valuation Metrics

- - Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for VTS stands at approximately 22, which is relatively aligned with historical averages. This suggests that the ETF is fairly valued relative to its earnings capacity in the context of the broader market.

- - P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

VTS's P/E ratio is slightly above the average of approximately 20 for the total U.S. market. This premium valuation reflects market confidence in the economic growth prospects and corporate profitability.
