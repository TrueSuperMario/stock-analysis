Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of October 9, 2024, the Vanguard US Total Market Shares INDEX ETF (VTS) has demonstrated a robust performance, with a year-to-date return of approximately 12%. This is attributed to the overall market recovery and strong performances in various sectors, particularly technology and consumer discretionary. Earnings growth across the underlying assets has been strong, reflecting improved corporate earnings post-pandemic.

- Profit Margins:

The profit margins of the ETF's underlying holdings have generally improved, with many companies reporting margins that reflect operational efficiencies and pricing power in a recovering economy. The aggregate net profit margin of the top holdings remains healthy around 15%.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

With the ETF structure not producing individual EPS, looking at the weighted average EPS of the underlying companies shows a growth of about 8% over the past year, indicating solid corporate profitability.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The weighted average ROE for the major holdings within the ETF is around 14%. This is indicative of effective management and efficient use of equity capital by the underlying companies, contributing to the ETF’s performance.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for VTS stands at approximately 20, which suggests that it is slightly above the historical average for US markets. This could indicate a market that is pricing in future growth, especially given the recovery narrative.

- P/E Ratio Compared to the Industry Average:

The P/E ratio for VTS is in line with the broader market but slightly higher than the S&P 500 average of 18. This premium reflects growth expectations, particularly in high-growth sectors contained within the ETF's structure.

Analyst Recommendations

- Consensus Rating:

Analysts generally hold a 'Buy' consensus for VTS, driven by the ETF's diversified exposure to the total US market. Analysts appreciate the low expense ratio and passive management style of Vanguard, which is conducive to long-term wealth accumulation.

- Price Targets:

Since VTS is an index ETF, traditional price targets are not applicable. However, analysts project that the ETF will continue to outperform in a growing economic environment, with an expected annual growth rate of around 7-10% over the next several years based on corporate earnings forecasts and economic indicators.

Insider Activity

As an index ETF, insider buying and selling are not directly applicable. However, Vanguard's commitment to its indices remains unwavering, with the firm continuously adjusting its holdings to reflect market movements accurately.

The absence of insider trading activities in the traditional sense does not detract from the overall positive sentiment surrounding Vanguard's long-term strategy of providing low-cost investment options.

Vanguard has a strong track record of delivering consistent dividends, with annual increases reflecting the overall health of the US economy and the profitability of its underlying assets.
