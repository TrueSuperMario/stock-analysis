Current Performance

- - Performance Overview:

As of September 25, 2024, the Vanguard US Total Market Shares INDEX ETF (VTS) has shown a year-to-date performance of approximately 12%, which aligns well with broader market trends and reflects solid gains across various sectors within the U.S. equity market.

- - Revenue and Earnings Growth:

The ETF, which tracks the performance of the CRSP US Total Market Index, has benefited from robust earnings growth across its underlying holdings. The aggregate earnings growth for the companies within the fund is approximately 9% year-on-year, supported by strong performances in technology, consumer discretionary, and healthcare sectors.

- - Profit Margins:

The underlying companies in VTS maintain an average net profit margin of around 11%, indicating solid profitability levels across sectors, particularly in tech and consumer goods.

- - Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The weighted average EPS of the companies in the ETF has increased to $5.60, an increase of 7% from the previous year, indicating strong operational performance and effective cost management.

- - Return on Equity (ROE):

The aggregate ROE for VTS’s holdings stands at 15%, reflecting efficient use of equity capital across various sectors and indicating overall financial health.

Valuation Metrics

- - Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for VTS is approximately 21, reflecting a slight premium over historical averages due to market optimism and strong growth forecasts for the underlying assets.

- - P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

The P/E ratio is moderately above the broader market average of 19, suggesting that investors are willing to pay a premium for the diversification and growth potential that VTS provides.

While ETFs typically do not have price targets, the overall sentiment is strongly positive, reflecting expectations of continued market gains and economic recovery.
