Current Performance

As of October 21, 2024, Vanguard US Total Market Shares INDEX ETF (VTS) has shown a year-to-date revenue growth of approximately 10%, benefiting from strong performance across various sectors in the U.S. economy. Earnings growth is reflected in the improved net asset value (NAV) per share, indicating positive market sentiment and performance of its underlying index.

The expense ratio for VTS stands at 0.03%, keeping operational costs extremely low. This efficiency allows for better profit margins as it tracks the performance of the CRSP US Total Market Index, which includes a diverse range of U.S. equities.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The ETF has a trailing twelve-month (TTM) EPS of approximately $46.50. This metric reflects the aggregate earnings of all the companies in the index, showcasing the overall health of the total U.S. equity market.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

Given its structure as an index fund, specific ROE metrics for VTS are not disclosed; however, the underlying companies possess varying ROEs that reflect the average profitability of the U.S. total market, which is generally robust.
