As of September 30, 2024, Vanguard US Total Market Shares Index ETF (VTS) has witnessed a revenue growth rate of approximately 8% year-on-year, driven by the overall recovery in the U.S. equity markets. The ETF’s expense ratio remains low, at around 0.03%, which contributes positively to its overall net gains for investors. By minimizing costs, VTS has maintained effective profit distribution to its shareholders.

- - Earnings Per Share (EPS):

EPS is not typically applicable to an ETF like VTS, but the growth in NAV (Net Asset Value) per share indicates healthy performance. The NAV has increased by roughly 8% over the past year, reflecting a robust recovery of the underlying U.S. equity market.

- - Return on Equity (ROE):

While ROE is not directly applicable to ETFs, the underlying holdings in VTS—comprised of U.S. stocks—are showing an impressive average ROE of around 15%, which indicates sound management performance across the companies held within the ETF.
