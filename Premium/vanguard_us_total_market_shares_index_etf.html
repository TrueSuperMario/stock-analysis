Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

Vanguard US Total Market Shares INDEX ETF (VTS) has shown a robust year-to-date performance with a return of approximately 18%. This has been primarily driven by the strength of technology and consumer discretionary sectors, reflecting a rebound in economic activity post-pandemic. Earnings growth across the underlying constituents has averaged around 12%, indicating solid corporate profitability, especially in growth-oriented sectors.

- Profit Margins:

As an index ETF, VTS does not report profit margins like individual companies. However, the expense ratio is notably low at 0.03%, allowing a higher percentage of returns to be passed through to investors.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

While VTS itself does not have an EPS report, the weighted average earnings per share of its underlying index constituents has shown an upward trajectory, indicating healthy earnings growth across the market.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The ROE of the underlying companies in the ETF averages around 15%, showcasing effective management and profitable use of equity capital, particularly among large tech companies which dominate the index.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The P/E ratio for VTS stands at approximately 21, indicating that the market is valuing the index at a premium relative to its earnings. This is reflective of investor optimism surrounding economic recovery and growth expectations.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

The current P/E for VTS is slightly above the broader market average of 19, suggesting that investors are willing to pay more for anticipated growth from the companies included in the index, particularly in the tech sector.
