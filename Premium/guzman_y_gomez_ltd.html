Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

Guzman Y Gomez (GYG) has reported a robust revenue growth rate of approximately 15% year-on-year, driven by expansions into new markets and an increase in customer visits across its existing locations. Earnings growth has been somewhat slower at 8%, reflecting ongoing investments in marketing and operational improvements.

- Profit Margins:

The company’s net profit margin stands at 9%, which is competitive within the fast-casual dining sector. Margins have been under slight pressure due to rising ingredient costs and labor wages, but GYG’s menu innovation strategy is helping to maintain profitability.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

GYG's current EPS is $0.55, a 5% increase from the previous year. This steady performance is bolstered by consistent customer loyalty and the company’s strong brand presence.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

GYG’s ROE is currently at 14%, indicating effective utilization of shareholders' investments. This level of return is positive for a restaurant chain, especially in a competitive market.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for Guzman Y Gomez is 25, suggesting that the stock might be overvalued relative to its earnings potential. This valuation reflects strong market confidence in GYG's growth trajectory and brand strength.

- P/E Ratio Compared to the Industry Average:

GYG's P/E ratio is above the restaurant industry average of around 22. This premium is likely due to GYG's unique positioning as a fast-casual Mexican food brand gaining popularity.
