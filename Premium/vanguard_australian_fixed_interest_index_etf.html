Current Performance

- Price Movement:

As of March 10, 2025, the Vanguard Australian Fixed Interest INDEX ETF (VAF) is trading at AUD 53.25, reflecting a 2% increase year-to-date, indicating solid performance in the bond market due to recent interest rate adjustments and economic stability.

- Net Asset Value (NAV):

The ETF's NAV has remained relatively stable, reporting values between AUD 52.50 and AUD 54.00 over the past month, which aligns with expected returns from the Australian fixed interest environment.

- Total Return:

The total return for VAF over the past year has been approximately 5.2%, incorporating income from yields and price appreciation. This performance mirrors a strengthened bond market due to lower interest rates and a more stable economic outlook.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

VAF does not have a traditional P/E ratio since it is a bond ETF; instead, focus is on yield and expense ratios. The fund's yield currently sits at 4.1%, which offers a competitive return in the current low-interest rate environment.

- Yield Comparison:

The yield on VAF is above the average yield on similar bond ETFs, which sit around 3.7%. This reflects the ETF's strategy of investing in higher-quality Australian fixed interests amid changing interest rates.
