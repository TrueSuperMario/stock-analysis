Current Performance

- Performance Overview:

As of October 26, 2024, the Vanguard Australian Fixed Interest INDEX ETF (VAF) has shown a year-to-date return of approximately 3.5%. This performance is in line with expectations for fixed interest assets amidst slight interest rate fluctuations. The fund has maintained a stable NAV, exhibiting resilience in a dynamic interest rate environment.

- Interest Rate Sensitivity:

The ETF's performance remains sensitive to changes in interest rates, particularly as the Reserve Bank of Australia (RBA) navigates monetary policy amidst inflationary pressures. The current average duration of the underlying securities is about 5 years, which may expose the ETF to moderate interest rate risk.

- Market Fluctuations:

The bond market has experienced some volatility due to geopolitical events and varying economic indicators. However, VAF has held steady, supported by quality holdings in both government and corporate bonds.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Book (P/B) Ratio:

VAF's P/B ratio is currently around 1.02, indicating that the ETF is fairly valued relative to the net asset value of its underlying securities. This is typical for fixed interest ETFs where bond market prices generally reflect the value of the underlying bonds.

- Yield Metrics:

The current running yield for VAF stands at approximately 4.1%, which is competitive in the current low-interest-rate environment. This yield reflects the fund's diverse holdings, with a mix of government and corporate bonds that provide reliable income.
