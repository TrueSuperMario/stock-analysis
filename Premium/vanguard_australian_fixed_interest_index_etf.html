Current Performance

- Net Asset Value (NAV) and Price Movement:

As of October 11, 2024, Vanguard Australian Fixed Interest INDEX ETF (VAF) has a NAV of approximately AUD 51.20, reflecting a modest increase of 2.5% year-to-date. The ETF has shown resilience amid fluctuating interest rates, which have impacted global bond markets.

- Total Return:

The total return of VAF over the past year stands at 4.8%, driven primarily by coupon payments and capital appreciation in the underlying bond portfolio. The stability of returns can be attributed to the ETF's exposure to high-quality Australian government and corporate bonds.

- Tracking Error:

VAF exhibits a low tracking error, typically under 0.5%, demonstrating its effectiveness in closely following the Bloomberg AusBond Composite Index. This efficiency underlines the ETF's role as an effective investment vehicle for fixed interest exposure.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Book (P/B) Ratio:

VAF's P/B ratio is currently around 1.03, suggesting that the market values the ETF's assets at a slight premium. This reflects the overall demand for fixed interest assets amid a low-interest environment.

- Yield to Maturity (YTM):

The ETF's yield to maturity is approximately 3.2%, offering a competitive return compared to traditional savings and term deposits, making it appealing for income-focused investors.
