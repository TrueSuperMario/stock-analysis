Current Performance

- Yield and Returns:

The Vanguard Australian Fixed Interest INDEX ETF (VAF) currently offers a yield of approximately 4.5%, which reflects the performance of Australian fixed interest securities. The ETF has experienced a price return of about 2% year-to-date, with total returns, including reinvested dividends, around 6.5%, underlining a stable performance amidst fluctuating interest rates.

- Volatility and Risk:

VAF’s standard deviation over the past year stands at 2.4%, indicating relatively low volatility compared to the equity market, making it an attractive option for conservative investors looking for stable returns in fixed income.

- Drawdown:

The maximum drawdown over the past year is around 4.2%, highlighting that while the ETF has seen fluctuations, its risk profile is manageable for fixed income investors.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Book (P/B) Ratio:

VAF’s P/B ratio is currently at 1.05, indicating that the ETF is fairly valued based on the book value of the underlying securities. This suggests that the market is optimistic about the future performance of Australian fixed interest assets.

- Effective Duration:

The effective duration of VAF is approximately 5.3 years, which measures its sensitivity to changes in interest rates. As interest rates rise, the value of the ETF's holdings may decrease, but the current duration suggests it can better withstand interest rate volatility than longer-dated securities.
