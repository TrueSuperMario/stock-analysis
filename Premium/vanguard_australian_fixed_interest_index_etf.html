The Vanguard Australian Fixed Interest INDEX ETF (VAF) has posted a year-to-date return of approximately 3.5% as of October 4, 2024. The return primarily reflects the performance of the underlying Australian government and corporate bonds, influenced by movements in interest rates and market conditions.

- Price Movement:

VAF's share price has been relatively stable, fluctuating within a narrow range in recent months. The ETF currently trades at around AUD 51.20, reflecting increased investor demand for fixed-income securities amid fluctuations in equity markets.

- Net Asset Value (NAV):

The latest NAV for VAF is approximately AUD 51.50, indicating that the ETF is trading at a slight discount compared to its NAV.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Book (P/B) Ratio:

The P/B ratio for VAF stands at 1.05, suggesting that the ETF is trading close to its book value, which is typical for fixed-income funds that hold stable, income-generating assets.

- Expense Ratio:

VAF has a low expense ratio of 0.20%, which is competitive compared to other fixed-income ETFs. This allows investors to retain more of their returns, further enhancing the attractiveness of this ETF.
