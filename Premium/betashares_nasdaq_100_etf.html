Current Performance

- - Funds Under Management (FUM):

The Betashares Nasdaq 100 ETF (NDQ) currently manages approximately $2 billion in assets, benefiting from substantial inflows this quarter as investors continue to embrace technology stocks, reflecting the growth trajectory of the Nasdaq index.

- - Fund Performance:

As of September 30, 2024, NDQ has delivered a year-to-date return of approximately 20%, significantly outperforming broader market indices like the S&P/ASX 200. The fund's performance has been primarily driven by strong earnings from key tech holdings such as Apple, Microsoft, and Tesla.

- - Net Asset Value (NAV):

The ETF’s NAV is $80.50 per share, showcasing a strong relative performance compared to its previous valuation of $67.00, indicating robust investor confidence in tech-centric investments within the Nasdaq.

- - Expense Ratio:

The ETF maintains a low expense ratio of 0.25%, making it an attractive option for cost-conscious investors seeking exposure to the U.S. technology sector.

Valuation Metrics

- - Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for NDQ is approximately 30, which is higher than the historical average for the Nasdaq 100 but reflects the premium investors are willing to pay for growth in the technology sector.

- - Comparison to Historical Averages:

The P/E ratio is notably above the long-term average of around 24, indicative of heightened expectations for growth and potential volatility in the sector as interest rates evolve.

Summary

The Betashares Nasdaq 100 ETF (NDQ) demonstrates a strong year-to-date performance, underpinned by the rapid growth of its technology-focused holdings. With a favorable analyst consensus, solid performance metrics, and an attractive expense ratio, NDQ remains a compelling option for investors seeking exposure to the U.S. tech scene. Although the ETF trades at a higher-than-average P/E ratio and might face volatility due to macroeconomic factors, the overall sentiment remains positive. NDQ is well-positioned to capitalize on ongoing trends in technology and innovation, making it a potentially valuable addition to an investment portfolio focused on growth.
