Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of October 2024, the Betashares Nasdaq 100 ETF (NDQ) has demonstrated robust performance, with a year-to-date return of approximately 22%. This growth is primarily driven by the strong performance of technology stocks within the Nasdaq 100 index, notably in areas such as artificial intelligence, cloud computing, and e-commerce.

- Profit Margins:

The ETF reflects the underlying companies' profit margins, which are generally high for the tech sector. As a result, the fund has benefitted from the high-margin business models prevalent among its top holdings, such as Apple, Microsoft, and Amazon.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

As an ETF, NDQ does not have an EPS metric in the traditional sense, but the weighted average EPS of its constituent stocks has seen solid growth, with many reporting significant increases relative to previous periods.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

While the ETF itself does not have a direct ROE, many of the companies it holds are characterized by high ROE, generally exceeding 25%. This reflects effective management and strong profitability, particularly in the technology sector.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The P/E ratio of NDQ is currently around 28, indicating investors are willing to pay a premium for growth in the tech sector. This is higher than the long-term average P/E ratio for the S&P 500, reflecting investor optimism and growth expectations from the technology sector.

- P/E Ratio compared to industry average:

Comparatively, the Nasdaq 100's P/E ratio is significantly above the broader market index, suggesting confidence in the continued growth of technology and innovation. The industry average P/E ratio for tech is around 23, indicating that NDQ is priced at a premium relative to many tech-focused funds.
