Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

The Betashares Nasdaq 100 ETF (NDQ) has experienced a remarkable year-to-date return of approximately 32%, reflecting a strong rally in growth and technology stocks. This performance is primarily driven by robust earnings reports from major constituents such as Apple and Microsoft, alongside favorable macroeconomic indicators.

- Profit Margins:

As an ETF, NDQ itself does not report profit margins; however, the underlying companies in the Nasdaq 100 tend to exhibit high gross profit margins, averaging around 70%. This indicates strong pricing power and operational efficiency among technology firms, which typically benefits ETF performance.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

While NDQ does not generate EPS directly, the aggregate EPS impact of its underlying holdings suggests solid growth, particularly from tech giants that dominate the index. The expectation for continued earnings growth reflects positively on investor sentiment and supports the ETF’s valuation.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The companies within the Nasdaq 100 typically have a high ROE, often exceeding 20%. High ROE reflects effective management and productive investment of shareholder capital, bolstering investor confidence in the ETF.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for the Nasdaq 100 stands at roughly 28, considerably higher than historical averages. This elevated valuation indicates heightened market expectations for growth, especially in technology and innovative sectors.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

Compared to the overall market, the Nasdaq 100 P/E ratio is above the S&P 500 average of about 22. This suggests a premium valuation, driven by expectations of superior growth rates from technology companies compared to more traditional sectors.

Summary
