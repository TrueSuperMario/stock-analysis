Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of October 2024, the Betashares Nasdaq 100 ETF (NDQ) has demonstrated notable performance, with a year-to-date increase of approximately 15%. This growth has primarily been fueled by a resurgence in technology stocks, particularly in the lead-up to earnings seasons for major constituents such as Apple, Amazon, and Nvidia. The ETF's earnings growth has been significantly outpacing broader market indices, reflecting the strength of the technology sector.

- Profit Margins:

The ETF's underlying assets, predominantly large-cap tech stocks, continue to show robust profit margins. On average, the constituents within the Nasdaq 100 have gross profit margins around 50%, elevating investor confidence despite macroeconomic headwinds.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The estimated EPS for the major holdings shows substantial growth compared to the previous fiscal year, suggesting that the companies within the NDQ fund are effectively translating revenue growth into bottom-line profit, indicative of operational efficiency.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The Return on Equity for leading firms in the Nasdaq 100 averages around 34%, a strong performance metric that suggests effective utilization of shareholder equity by these technology giants.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The NDQ’s current P/E ratio stands at 30, reflecting the premium investors are willing to pay for the earnings potential of its constituent companies. This is in line with expectations for high-growth companies in a recovering economic environment.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

Compared to the broader market’s P/E ratio of approximately 20, NDQ's elevated valuation indicates strong investor sentiment focused on technology, anticipating continued long-term growth despite potential short-term fluctuations.

Summary

Betashares Nasdaq 100 ETF (NDQ) is positioned strongly within the technology sector, showcasing vibrant performance metrics and promising growth expectations.
