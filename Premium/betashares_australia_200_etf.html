Current Performance

- - Market Price and NAV:

As of September 24, 2024, the Betashares Australia 200 ETF (A200) is trading at approximately AUD 102.24. The fund’s Net Asset Value (NAV) reflects a slight premium to its market price, indicative of strong market interest and demand for exposure to Australian equities.

- - Performance Metrics:

Over the last year, A200 has shown a total return of approximately 10%, driven by gains in core sectors like financials and materials, which constitute a significant portion of the underlying index. Year-to-date, the ETF has outperformed the broader ASX index, suggesting effective stock selection and positive momentum.

- - Volatility:

The ETF has displayed a beta of around 0.95, indicating that it is slightly less volatile than the broader market. This positions A200 as a relatively stable option for investors looking for exposure to the Australian equity market with reduced risk.

Valuation Metrics

- - Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The A200 ETF has a trailing P/E ratio of approximately 15, signaling a moderate valuation compared to historical averages. This indicates that the market is valuing Australian equities in line with expected growth, providing a balanced opportunity for investors.

- - P/E Ratio vs. Market Average:

The P/E ratio of A200 is slightly below the Australian market average of 16.5, suggesting that investors may find value in A200 relative to other Australian equities, especially in a rising interest rate environment.

- - Price-to-Book (P/B) Ratio:

The current P/B ratio for A200 stands around 2.1, which is typical for many growth-oriented equity ETFs and reflects a reasonable valuation given the performance of major constituents.
