Current Performance

- - Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of September 30, 2024, the Betashares Australia 200 ETF (A200) has demonstrated a year-to-date return of approximately 8%. This performance is largely attributed to the broader recovery in the Australian equity market, with key sectors such as financials and materials contributing positively. However, earnings growth across the ETF’s underlying holdings has been mixed, with some sectors like technology experiencing volatility while consumer staples remain stable.

- - Profit Margins:

The fund’s underlying businesses reflect varied profit margins, largely influenced by sector performance. The average net profit margin for companies within the A200 currently sits at around 12%. This figure suggests a healthy profitability profile overall, although it hides disparities, particularly with technology firms facing pressure on margins from rising costs.

- - Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The weighted average EPS of the ETF's constituents has shown resilience, currently positioned at $2.45. This reflects a modest increase compared to the previous year, affirming the underlying companies' ability to maintain earnings growth despite market fluctuations.

- - Return on Equity (ROE):

The ROE for the ETF's underlying holdings averages around 15%, indicative of good capital efficiency among Australian large-cap companies. Financial services dominate this metric, contributing positively to overall performance.

Valuation Metrics

- - Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The A200 has a current P/E ratio of 18, which aligns closely with the broader Australian market’s average. This metric suggests that the ETF is reasonably valued relative to its earning potential, especially considering the economic recovery post-pandemic.

- - P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

The P/E ratio of A200 is consistent with the materials and financial services sectors, which are well-represented in its portfolio. These sectors generally trade at higher multiples due to expected growth opportunities compared to sectors like energy or traditional consumer goods.
