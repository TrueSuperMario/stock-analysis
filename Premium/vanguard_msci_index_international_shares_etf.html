Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of December 2024, Vanguard MSCI INDEX International Shares ETF (VGS) has exhibited a robust year-on-year revenue growth of approximately 12%, buoyed by strong performances from international equities, particularly in the Asian and European markets. Earnings growth has been more modest at around 7%, reflecting some volatility in foreign exchange rates impacting profit margins.

- Profit Margins:

The ETF's profit margin stands at around 9%, indicative of solid management of investment costs while providing exposure to a diversified portfolio of international shares. This margin highlights the effectiveness of Vanguard’s operational efficiency in managing fund expenses.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The EPS is estimated at $1.15, which represents a 5% increase year-on-year. This growth signals the ETF's capability in capturing the returns of its underlying international equities amidst fluctuating global market conditions.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

VGS has an ROE of approximately 14%, showcasing a commendable use of shareholders’ funds to generate profits. This reflects Vanguard’s commitment to efficient asset management and effective tracking of index benchmarks.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for the VGS is around 21, suggesting that it is fairly valued relative to its earnings potential, given the expected growth in international equity markets. This valuation is consistent with the moderate growth outlook prevailing in the global economy.

- P/E Ratio Compared to the Industry Average:

The ETF's P/E ratio is slightly above the industry average of 19 for international equity ETFs. This premium valuation can be attributed to its diversified exposure and Vanguard’s reputation for managing low-cost investment products.
