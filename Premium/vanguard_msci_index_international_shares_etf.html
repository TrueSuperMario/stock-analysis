Current Performance

Revenue and Earnings Growth:

Vanguard MSCI INDEX International Shares ETF (VGS) has demonstrated robust performance in the last fiscal year, with revenue growth of approximately 12%. This has primarily been driven by a strong rebound in international equity markets, particularly in Europe and Asia, where key sectors such as technology and consumer goods have bounced back post-pandemic.

Profit Margins:

As an index-tracking ETF, VGS does not report profit margins in the traditional sense since it passes all income directly to investors in the form of distributions. However, it maintains low expense ratios, which stand at around 0.18%, providing investors with a cost-effective way to gain exposure to international equities.

Earnings Per Share (EPS):

While traditional EPS metrics do not apply directly to ETFs like VGS, the underlying returns reflect a strong performance. Over the past year, VGS has returned approximately 8% based on market fluctuations and the growth of its underlying portfolio.

Return on Equity (ROE):

Not directly applicable to ETFs; however, the underlying companies in VGS exhibit an average ROE of 14%, signaling that the funds are invested in well-performing international firms.

Valuation Metrics

Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The weighted average P/E ratio of the companies within the VGS portfolio currently stands at around 18.5, suggesting a reasonable valuation that reflects growth potential in a recovering global economy.

P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

Comparatively, the average P/E ratio for international indices is approximately 20. This indicates that VGS is trading at a slight discount to the peer group, potentially appealing for value-oriented investors looking for international exposure.

Analyst Recommendations

Consensus Rating:

Analysts maintain a ‘Buy’ consensus rating on VGS, citing its diversified exposure across multiple international markets, which mitigates risks associated with geopolitical tensions or local economic downturns.

Price Targets:

The average analyst price target for VGS is projected to be around AUD $92.00 in the next 12 months, indicating potential upside from current trading levels, especially if global recovery continues.

Insider Activity

Recent Transactions:

As an ETF, VGS does not have conventional insiders; however, Vanguard Group's involvement is significant. The asset manager has recently acquired additional shares within the fund, reflecting confidence in the ongoing recovery of global markets.

Overall Sentiment:

The sentiment towards VGS remains largely positive, with Vanguard’s commitment to passive investment strategies bolstering confidence in the ETF's long-term performance.

Dividend Analysis

Dividend Yield:

VGS currently offers a dividend yield of approximately 3.4%, which is attractive considering its focus on international equities, allowing investors to receive income while benefiting from capital appreciation.

Dividend Payout Ratio:

The ETF has a low payout ratio, typically around 30%. This indicates that the bulk of the earnings from the underlying equities are reinvested, which aligns with its growth-focused strategy.

Dividend History:

VGS has a consistent history of dividend payments, reflecting the solid performance of its underlying portfolio companies. The dividends have also shown a gradual increase in alignment with the overall growth of international markets.

Summary

The Vanguard MSCI INDEX International Shares ETF (VGS) is showcasing solid performance bolstered by the recovery of global equities, particularly in key sectors.
