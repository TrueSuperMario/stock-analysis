Current Performance

- - Fund Performance:

As of September 24, 2024, the Vanguard MSCI International Shares ETF (VGS) has shown a solid year-to-date performance, increasing approximately 12%. This increase in value is attributed to a recovery in international equity markets and improved investor sentiment towards global equities.

- - Net Asset Value (NAV):

The current NAV of VGS stands at AUD 97.50, which is reflective of the underlying equities' strong performance, especially in developed markets such as Europe and Asia.

- - Volatility:

The ETF has experienced moderate volatility, with a standard deviation of 15% over the past year, indicating a balanced risk-return profile suitable for long-term investors.

- - Tracking Error:

VGS has maintained a low tracking error of around 0.3%, demonstrating its effective replication of the MSCI All Country World ex USA Index, which includes equities from various international markets.

Valuation Metrics

- - Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for VGS is 18.5, positioned slightly above the global average of 17. This valuation suggests that the market is willing to pay a premium for exposure to international equities, reflecting confidence in global economic recovery.

- - Price-to-Book (P/B) Ratio:

VGS has a P/B ratio of 1.8, which signals a reasonable valuation level compared to historical averages. This indicates that the underlying assets are being valued fairly relative to their book value.

- - Dividend Discount Model:

The intrinsic value calculated using a dividend discount model (DDM) indicates that VGS could be undervalued relative to its historical performance, supporting the appeal for long-term investors looking for quality international exposure.
