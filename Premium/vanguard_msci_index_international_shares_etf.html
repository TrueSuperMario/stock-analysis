Current Performance

- - Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of September 2024, Vanguard MSCI International Shares ETF (VGS) has exhibited a solid performance with a year-to-date return of approximately 8%. The ETF has benefited from a general rebound in global equities, particularly in the technology and healthcare sectors. Revenue generation through net asset inflows has been robust, indicating strong investor interest and market confidence.

- - Profit Margins:

VGS mirrors the performance of the MSCI All Country World Index (ex-U.S. and Canada), and as such, the profit margins of the underlying businesses in the ETF vary across industries. Top performing sectors such as technology have shown healthy margins, while cyclical sectors such as materials have experienced fluctuations due to global economic conditions.

- - Earnings Per Share (EPS):

While traditional EPS metrics don’t apply directly to ETFs, the underlying index constituents have shown a collective improvement, reflecting an aggregate earnings increase of around 5% year-on-year among major international stocks held within the ETF.

- - Return on Equity (ROE):

The average ROE of the equities comprising VGS is approximately 13%, which reflects a healthy utilization of equity capital among these international firms, especially those in developed markets.

Valuation Metrics

- - Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

VGS has a current P/E ratio of approximately 18.5, which is in line with the global average valuation for international equities. This suggests the ETF is fairly valued given the current earnings environment.

- - P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

The P/E ratio of VGS is slightly above the average for its international peer group, which stands around 17. This indicates that investors are willing to pay a premium for exposure to diversified international equities, possibly due to their perceived growth prospects.
