Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of October 2024, Vanguard MSCI INDEX International Shares ETF (VGS) has shown a revenue growth rate of approximately 7% over the past year. This growth is primarily attributed to the strong performance of its underlying international equity holdings, particularly in sectors like technology and healthcare, which have rebounded following a downturn in earlier market cycles. Earnings growth has been more robust, reported at around 10%, showcasing the ETF's ability to participate in the recovery of global markets.

- Profit Margins:

Profit margins in the ETF context are represented by the overall performance of the underlying companies it holds. The aggregate net profit margin of the top holdings in VGS is approximately 15%, reflecting strong operational efficiency and profitability within the international equities space.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The aggregated EPS for VGS's top constituents averages around $3.00, indicating a healthy increase of 8% from the previous year. This uptick highlights the resilience of international markets despite macroeconomic headwinds.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The average ROE of the major companies within VGS is approximately 14%, demonstrating effective capital use to generate profits, which is particularly commendable given the ongoing geopolitical tensions and market volatility.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for the Vanguard MSCI INDEX International Shares ETF is around 18, suggesting that investors are willing to pay a moderate premium for its earnings potential. This reflects the overall positive sentiment in global markets, despite economic uncertainties.

- P/E Ratio Compared to Industry Average:

VGS’s P/E ratio is slightly above the average for international equity ETFs, which stands at about 16.5. This premium valuation suggests that the market has confidence in the ETF's diversified portfolio and its exposure to high-growth markets, particularly in Asia and Europe.
