Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of October 2024, the Vanguard MSCI INDEX International Shares ETF (VGS) has shown a year-on-year revenue growth of 8%, benefiting from significant gains in various international equity markets. Earnings growth has been robust at 7%, attributed to the diversified exposure across multiple regions and sectors which buffer against localized downturns.

- Profit Margins:

VGS, being an index fund, inherently has tighter profit margins compared to actively managed funds, but it maintains a net profit margin of around 0.3%. This is typical for index funds, reflecting low management fees and operational costs, ensuring that more investor capital is put to work in the markets.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The ETF’s EPS, reflective of its underlying holdings, has reached $2.80, marking a 5% increase from the previous year. This is commendable given the fund's extensive international exposure, showcasing resilience amidst global market fluctuations.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The ROE for VGS stands at approximately 15%, indicating that the fund is effectively utilizing investors' equity to drive returns. This is a favorable figure in the context of index funds, suggesting solid performance in line with its benchmark indices.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

VGS currently has a P/E ratio of 20, indicating a slightly higher valuation relative to historical averages. This suggests investor confidence in future growth prospects across its international holdings despite the moderate premium.

- P/E Ratio Compared to the Industry Average:

The P/E ratio for VGS is in line with the broader index fund category, where the average P/E is around 18. This alignment suggests that VGS is priced fairly within the index fund space, taking into account its international exposure which traditionally carries more variability.
