Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of October 2024, Vanguard MSCI International Shares ETF (VGS) has recorded a year-to-date return of approximately 11%, benefiting from a recovery in international equity markets. Revenue for VGS is closely tied to the performance of the underlying index, which reflects significant recovery in sectors such as technology and consumer discretionary.

- Profit Margins:

While ETFs do not report profit margins in the traditional sense, VGS has a very low expense ratio of approximately 0.18%, contributing to an efficient cost structure that maximizes returns for investors. This is competitive within the ETF space and helps maintain a solid passive investment strategy.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

VGS does not operate with EPS like individual companies; rather, it tracks the performance of dividends and capital gains derived from the underlying stocks. Overall returns have been steady, aligning with global market trends.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

ROE is not applicable as VGS functions as an investment fund. However, it aims to generate returns that reflect the performance of the underlying companies in the MSCI All Country World Index (ACWI) ex Australia.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for VGS is approximately 20, indicating that investors are willing to pay a moderate premium based on the expected growth in international markets relative to domestic returns.

- P/E Ratio Compared to Industry Average:

The P/E ratio of VGS is somewhat in line with the international equity ETF benchmarks, signaling that investors have confident expectations about earnings recovery outside Australia, which may be influenced by global economic trends.

- Price Targets:

Though ETFs do not have specific price targets, analysts expect continued positive momentum for VGS based on the anticipated recovery of key global markets, particularly in Europe and Asia.

- Dividend Payout Ratio:

The payout ratio is not explicitly stated, but VGS aims to distribute most of its income to investors, aligning with standard practices for open-ended ETFs.

VGS has a consistent history of distributing dividends quarterly, with a gradual increase in distributions reflecting growth in underlying international equities.
