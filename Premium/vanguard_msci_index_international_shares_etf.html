Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

Vanguard MSCI INDEX International Shares ETF (VGS) has demonstrated solid performance, experiencing a year-on-year revenue increase driven by the robust growth of its underlying international equities. As of the latest reports, VGS has recorded a 10% increase in revenue, attributed to rising valuations in the global equity markets, particularly in technology and consumer discretionary sectors.

- Profit Margins:

The fund's effective management of operational costs and passive investment strategy allows it to maintain low expense ratios, which have a direct positive impact on profit margins. The current total expense ratio (TER) stands at 0.18%, ensuring that investors retain a significant portion of their returns.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

As an ETF, VGS does not have a traditional EPS like individual stocks; however, the fund's annualized return over the last year is approximately 12%, indicating strong performance driven by quality international holdings, particularly from countries like the United States, Japan, and the United Kingdom.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

Although ROE is not a standard metric for ETFs, the underlying companies within VGS boast average ROEs of around 15%, reflecting strong financial health and efficient use of equity in generating profits.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for the underlying assets of VGS is approximately 20.5, reflecting a moderate valuation relative to historical averages. This suggests that while the fund is not undervalued, its exposure to high-growth sectors may justify the premium.

- P/E Ratio Compared to the Industry Average:

The P/E ratio of VGS aligns closely with the global equity market average, which is around 21. This positions VGS competitively, suggesting that it is on par with similar international equity funds in terms of valuation.
