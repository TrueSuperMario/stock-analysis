Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of Q3 2024, Vanguard MSCI International Shares ETF (VGS) has shown strong performance, with a revenue growth rate of approximately 12% year-on-year, attributed to the overall recovery in global equities and a favorable market sentiment following recent economic stabilizations. However, earnings growth has lagged slightly at around 8%, impacted by fluctuations in currency exchange rates and certain sector performance discrepancies.

- Profit Margins:

The net profit margin for VGS, which reflects the overall profitability of its holdings, stands at approximately 14%. This figure is indicative of the underlying management efficiency in both maintaining costs low and capitalizing on high-margin operational segments within the international investments.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The ETF reports an EPS of around $1.45, which is a 4% increase from the previous year. This steady growth is encouraging, given the diverse range of international equities it comprises.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

VGS has an impressive ROE of 15%, signifying efficient use of equity capital in generating profits. This is a strong performance indicator relative to many peers in the international investing landscape.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio of VGS is recorded at 20, indicating a moderate valuation level when compared to historical averages for international ETFs. This suggests that while the market remains optimistic, it is also cautious about overly aggressive valuations.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

Compared to the international equity average P/E of 18, VGS's 20 indicates a slight premium, driven primarily by its diversified holdings and relatively strong performance outlook. This premium reflects investor confidence in the ETF's management and strategy.
