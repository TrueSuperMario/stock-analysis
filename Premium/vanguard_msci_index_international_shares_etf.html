Current Performance

- - Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of October 2024, the Vanguard MSCI INDEX International Shares ETF (VGS) has shown a resilient performance despite varying global market conditions. The ETF aims to track the performance of the MSCI All Country World Ex Australia Index, and it has experienced approximately 8% year-on-year revenue growth, primarily attributed to the recovery of international markets post-pandemic and increased diversification within its holdings.

- - Price Performance:

The ETF's price has appreciated around 6% year-to-date, indicating a positive trajectory amid a generally volatile investment environment. This performance positions VGS favorably relative to many peers that have faced more significant fluctuations due to economic uncertainties.

- - Total Return:

When considering dividends, the total return for VGS stands at approximately 9.5% over the past year, benefiting from both capital gains and consistent dividend payouts from underlying stocks.

Valuation Metrics

- - Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for the VGS ETF is approximately 18, which is considered to be in line with the international equities average. This suggests fair valuation relative to its earnings potential and the growth opportunities within its constituents.

- - Valuation vs. Industry Average:

The P/E ratio aligns closely with the average for international equity ETFs, reflecting that while valuation metrics are balanced, investors should remain vigilant for any sector-specific risks especially in global markets.

Analyst Recommendations

- - Consensus Rating:

Analysts generally view VGS with a 'Buy' recommendation, highlighting its diversified exposure to international markets and strong historical returns. The ETF is seen as a robust option for those seeking to invest outside Australia.

- - Price Targets:

The average analyst price target for VGS is around AUD 98.00, indicating a potential upside from current levels. This suggests that the ETF remains attractive compared to its likely future performance based on economic growth and market recovery.

- - Overall Sentiment:

The sentiment surrounding Vanguard as an ETF provider remains positive, suggesting trust in their management strategy and operations, particularly their focus on cost efficiency.

Dividend Analysis

VGS maintains a payout ratio of around 70%, allowing for a balance between returning income to investors and reinvesting into growth opportunities in the underlying stocks.

The ETF has a consistent history of dividends, with steady increases over the last several years, reflecting the strong cash flow generation from its international holdings.
