Current Performance

- - Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of September 30, 2024, the iShares Core S&P/ASX 200 ETF (IOZ) has seen steady performance, reflecting a revenue growth of approximately 6% year-over-year attributed to rebound trends in the Australian equity markets. Earnings growth reflects broader market dynamics, with underlying companies benefiting from improved consumer sentiment and economic resilience.

- - Profit Margins:

The profit margins of the holdings within IOZ remain robust, averaging around 14%. This level of profitability is sustained by factors such as effective cost management and the lucrative positioning of key sectors like financials and materials.

- - Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The ETF's weighted average EPS has improved to $1.80 over the past year, which is a 4% increase compared to previous periods. This growth is driven by the strong performance of top holdings in the index, particularly in the banking and mining sectors.

- - Return on Equity (ROE):

The underlying companies within IOZ boast an average ROE of approximately 15%, indicating solid efficiency in utilizing shareholders' equity to generate profits. This is particularly noteworthy in a recovering economic landscape post-COVID-19.

Valuation Metrics

- - Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for IOZ is around 18, suggesting that while the ETF is moderately valued, it is slightly above the historical average for the Australian market, reflecting investor confidence in future growth and resilience.

- - P/E Ratio Compared to the Industry Average:

IOZ’s P/E ratio is marginally higher than the broader Australian market average of 17, indicating that investors may be pricing in higher expected growth rates for the index constituents.
