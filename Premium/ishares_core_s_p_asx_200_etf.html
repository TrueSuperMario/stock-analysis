Current Performance

- - Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of September 2024, the iShares Core S&P/ASX 200 ETF (IOZ) has exhibited a modest year-to-date performance, with a return of approximately 8%. The ETF's underlying index, the S&P/ASX 200, reflects a diverse portfolio of Australian equities, benefiting from solid earnings growth driven by resource sectors and financial services.

- - Profit Margins:

The profit margins across the ETF's holdings have generally improved, especially in sectors such as materials and healthcare. The average profit margin for companies included in the S&P/ASX 200 is around 12%, suggesting healthy operational efficiency within the index.

- - Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The aggregate EPS for the companies within the IOZ is currently around AUD 1.95, which represents a 5% increase compared to the previous fiscal year. This growth is attributed to robust consumer spending and strong commodity prices.

- - Return on Equity (ROE):

The average ROE of companies in the ETF stands at approximately 13%, reflecting effective management of equity capital to enhance shareholder value. This metric indicates that the underlying companies are successfully generating profits relative to their equity investments.

Valuation Metrics

- - Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for the IOZ is about 17.5, which aligns with the historical average for the S&P/ASX 200. This valuation level suggests that the ETF is moderately priced relative to its earnings potential.

- - P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

The P/E ratio of 17.5 is slightly below the broader ASX index average of 18.2, indicating a competitive valuation within the managed fund arena. This may attract value-oriented investors seeking exposure to the Australian equity market.
