Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

The iShares Core S&P/ASX 200 ETF (IOZ) has provided a year-on-year revenue growth of approximately 8% as of January 2025, driven largely by the recovery in the Australian share market post-pandemic and the strong performance of Australian blue-chip companies. However, earnings growth has been more modest at around 4%, reflecting some sector-specific challenges, such as increased costs and economic uncertainty moderating profits for some underlying companies in the ETF.

- Profit Margins:

The profit margins for the ETF's underlying holdings vary, but on average, net profit margins are around 11%. Many of the larger constituents, such as banks and miners, have sustained strong margins, although recent economic pressures have begun to influence this figure slightly.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The weighted average EPS for companies within the IOZ is approximately $1.67, which is a 5% increase over the past year. This reflects a solid performance from key sectors such as financials and materials, despite some volatility.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The average ROE across the ETF's top holdings is about 14%, indicating effective management and profitability from shareholder investments. This performance suggests that the underlying companies are efficiently using their equity to generate profits.

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for IOZ is approximately 18, which indicates a slightly higher valuation compared to the broader Australian market, which sits around a P/E of 16. This suggests that investors are paying a premium due to the quality and reliability of the underlying blue-chip assets.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

When comparing IOZ's P/E ratio to its underlying sectors, it remains competitive within the financials sector but slightly elevated in the materials sector. This indicates market optimism, particularly towards financial stability and growth in economic recovery.
