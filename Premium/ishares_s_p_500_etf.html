The profit margins of the underlying companies in the S&P 500 have improved as companies have navigated supply chain issues effectively and managed costs. The average profit margin for the index now hovers around 11%, reflecting enhanced operational efficiency post-pandemic.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The trailing twelve months' (TTM) EPS for the S&P 500 constituents represented in IVV stands at around $225, which marks a 9% increase when compared to the same period last year, indicating robust earnings growth and positive corporate outlooks.

The ROE for S&P 500 companies has stabilized around 16%, showcasing efficient use of equity to generate profits, a sentiment echoed across many sectors, including technology and healthcare.

With the broader market P/E ratio sitting slightly higher than the historical averages, a comparison with the average P/E of major sectors indicates that while technology stocks have higher valuations, sectors such as energy and financials remain more attractively valued.
