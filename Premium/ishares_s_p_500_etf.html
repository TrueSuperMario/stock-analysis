Current Performance

- - Revenue and Earnings Growth:

The iShares S&P 500 ETF (IVV) has demonstrated a steady performance in 2024, with year-to-date returns around 15%. The underlying S&P 500 index has seen revenue growth of approximately 6% year-on-year, driven primarily by robust earnings in the technology and consumer discretionary sectors. Earnings growth has been slightly higher at around 8%, reflecting strong profitability across major S&P 500 constituents.

- - Profit Margins:

The ETF tracks an index of large-cap U.S. equities, which generally have healthy profit margins. The average operating margin for the S&P 500 firms currently stands at 20%, indicative of strong price power and cost management strategies amongst its constituents.

- - Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The EPS for the S&P 500 is estimated at $208 for the fiscal year 2024, which marks a 6% increase from the previous year. This growth has fueled investor confidence and contributed to the ETF's overall performance.

- - Return on Equity (ROE):

The underlying companies in the S&P 500 have an average ROE of about 15%, signaling efficient utilization of equity capital. This strong performance metric is a key driver for long-term investors in equity ETFs like IVV.

Valuation Metrics

- - Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for the S&P 500 index is approximately 23, suggesting that the market is pricing in higher future growth expectations. This valuation reflects the ongoing economic recovery post-pandemic, as well as significant momentum in technology and healthcare sectors.

- - P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

Compared to the historical average P/E ratio of around 18 for the S&P 500, the current figure indicates a premium valuation. Investor sentiment and demand for growth have pushed these metrics higher.
