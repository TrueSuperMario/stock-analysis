The underlying S&P 500 index has averaged approximately 8% annual growth in earnings across its constituent companies.

- Profit Margins:

The fund’s profit margin, while not directly applicable, can be inferred from the operational efficiency of its holdings. IVV typically tracks the performances of high-margin, large-cap companies, contributing positively to its overall expense ratio, which remains low at around 0.03%.

Although IVV itself does not report EPS as a traditional stock would, the S&P 500's constituent companies have exhibited an average EPS growth rate of about 6% since the beginning of 2025, further solidifying the ETF's performance during this time.

For the companies within the S&P 500, the aggregate ROE is healthy, sitting around 16%. This strong utilization of shareholder equity among its constituents reflects positively on IVV as a representative of these companies.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

While the average P/E for IVV is slightly elevated compared to historical trends, it remains in line with the broader theme of strong growth potential among large-cap stocks. Analysts note this premium valuation reflects confidence in recovery and growth post-pandemic.
