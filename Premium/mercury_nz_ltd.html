Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of December 2024, Mercury NZ Ltd has achieved a revenue growth of 7% year-on-year, primarily driven by increased demand for electricity and a robust expansion in renewable energy capacity. Earnings have risen by 4%, reflecting ongoing investments in renewable infrastructure and a commitment to operational efficiency.

- Profit Margins:

Mercury’s net profit margin stands at 11%, indicating a slight dip from last year due to increased maintenance costs and supply chain disruptions. However, the company's ongoing emphasis on renewable energy helps maintain solid margins amid these challenges.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The current EPS is $0.75, representing a 4% increase compared to the previous year. This growth is attributed to Mercury's effective cost management practices and steady revenue streams from its renewable generation assets.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

Mercury’s ROE is at 13%, demonstrating effective use of equity to generate profits. This is particularly impressive within the utility industry, where stable cash flows are essential for investor confidence.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for Mercury NZ is 21, suggesting that investors are willing to pay a premium for the company’s earning potential, reflective of growing confidence in its renewable energy future.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

Mercury’s P/E ratio is above the utilities industry average of 18, indicating a strong market position and favorable growth outlook in the renewable segment, despite the higher valuation curve.
