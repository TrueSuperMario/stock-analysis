Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of October 2024, the VanEck MSCI International Quality ETF (QUAL) has demonstrated a year-to-date performance of approximately 15%, reflecting a recovery in international equity markets. The ETF has benefitted from its focus on high-quality companies, which have shown stable earnings growth in the current economic environment. Earnings growth for the underlying assets within the ETF has been robust, averaging around 8% year-on-year.

- Profit Margins:

The profit margins of the companies within QUAL average around 12% to 15%, indicating a strong ability to convert revenue into profit. This is a commendable feature, especially during periods of economic uncertainty, showcasing the quality focus of the ETF's management.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The EPS for the top holdings within QUAL has seen an increase, averaging $4.50, an improvement compared to $4.10 in the previous year. This growth reflects the underlying companies' ability to generate solid earnings despite global economic challenges.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

QUAL’s portfolio companies boast an average ROE of 14%, which signifies effective management in utilizing equity to generate profit. This strong figure is reflective of the ETF’s focus on quality and financially sound companies.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for the VanEck MSCI International Quality ETF is approximately 22, indicating that it is somewhat elevated compared to historical averages for the international equity markets. This higher ratio reflects the premium investors may be willing to pay for quality investments, especially in uncertain times.

- P/E Ratio Compared to the Industry Average:

QUAL’s P/E ratio exceeds the international equity average of 19. This suggests that investors are placing a higher value on the quality and stability of the earnings generated by the companies held within the ETF, driven by their resilience in the market.
