Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of December 30, 2024, the VanEck MSCI International Quality ETF (QUAL) has reported a revenue growth of approximately 8% year-on-year, with strong performance driven by gains in defensive and quality-oriented sectors such as consumer staples and healthcare. Earnings growth stands at 6%, reflecting robust underlying profitability despite market volatility.

- Profit Margins:

The ETF’s overall net profit margin is around 13%, which is commendable given the varying profitability witnessed across the underlying international stocks. The focus on high-quality companies contributes positively to maintaining solid margins.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The EPS for the ETF, considering its holdings, is reported at $4.50, marking a 5% increase compared to the previous year. This growth highlights the resilience of the underlying companies, particularly in a fluctuating economic environment.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

QUAL features an average ROE of 15%, indicating efficient use of shareholder equity among its top holdings. This is well above the average of its international peers, showcasing the effectiveness of its quality selection criteria.

The ETF’s P/E ratio is higher than the average P/E of 19 for international equities, indicating that investors are willing to pay more for expected quality and stability in earnings.
