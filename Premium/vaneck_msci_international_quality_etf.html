Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of December 2024, the VanEck MSCI International Quality ETF (QUAL) has delivered robust performance, with a year-to-date revenue growth of 12%. This growth has been fuelled by solid returns from its underlying quality equities in developed international markets. Earnings growth has been similarly strong, at 10%, benefiting from disciplined management practices and strong operational efficiencies across the portfolio.

- Profit Margins:

The ETF has maintained an impressive profit margin of approximately 11%. This reflects the focus on high-quality companies, which tend to have sustainable business models capable of weathering economic fluctuations better than their peers.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The ETF’s portfolio companies have a weighted average EPS growth rate of 9%, showcasing the strength of the underlying assets. This performance underscores the effectiveness of the quality tilt embedded in the ETF’s strategy.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

QUAL's underlying holdings demonstrate an aggregate ROE of around 15%, indicating efficient use of equity to generate profits and reflecting the quality nature of the investments selected for the fund.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for QUAL is at 22, indicating a premium valuation relative to the broader market. This aligns with the ETF's focus on quality growth companies, which typically trade at higher multiples due to their perceived lower risk and higher earnings potential.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

QUAL's P/E is slightly above the global equities average of 20. Though it commands a higher P/E, this is justified by the fund's consistent historical performance and the resilience of the quality companies it invests in.

Summary
