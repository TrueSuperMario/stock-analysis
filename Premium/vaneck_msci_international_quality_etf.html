Current Performance

- - Revenue and Earnings Growth:

The VanEck MSCI International Quality ETF (QUAL) has seen a steady performance in the international equity market, particularly in 2024. The ETF maintains strong positions in quality companies with consistent earnings. Year-to-date, the total return is approximately 15%, primarily driven by solid gains in its high-quality holdings across various sectors.

- - Profit Margins:

The average profit margin of the companies within QUAL is approximately 15%, which reflects the focus on high-quality businesses that are typically more resilient during economic downturns. This margin is higher than the global average, indicating a strong position within its peer group.

- - Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The weighted average EPS of the firms in QUAL has increased by about 10% from the previous year, showcasing the strength of its underlying holdings. This earnings growth supports the ETF’s strategy of investing in quality stocks with stable and growing earnings.

- - Return on Equity (ROE):

The average ROE of the portfolio’s constituents stands at 18%. This is indicative of efficient management and profitability within leading international firms, further validating QUAL’s investment thesis of targeting high-quality companies.

Valuation Metrics

- - Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

QUAL currently carries a P/E ratio of 20, which is somewhat higher than the broader international equity market average of around 18. This premium valuation reflects the market’s confidence in the long-term growth prospects associated with high-quality companies.

- - P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

Relative to its peer group focused on quality, QUAL’s valuation is justified by its historical performance and stable earnings, suggesting that investors are willing to pay a premium for the stability and resilience of the holdings.
