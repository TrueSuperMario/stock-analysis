Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of February 2025, VanEck MSCI International Quality ETF (QUAL) has seen a revenue growth rate of approximately 8% year-over-year. This growth is supported by strong performance in developed international markets, particularly in sectors like consumer staples and healthcare. Earnings growth has been robust, with the ETF reflecting a 7% increase due to capitalizing on high-quality companies that typically provide steady earnings.

- Profit Margins:

The net profit margin for the underlying companies represented in QUAL averages about 15%. This is considered solid, especially as the ETF focuses on high-quality firms that tend to maintain higher margins through operational efficiencies and competitive advantages.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The weighted average EPS for the ETF's constituents stands at $3.25, marking a 5% increase from the previous year. This improvement highlights the resilience of the quality stocks within the ETF amidst fluctuating market conditions.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The ROE for the companies in QUAL is approximately 18%, demonstrating strong profit generation relative to shareholder equity. This level of ROE is indicative of the ETF's focus on quality equities, often linked with effective management and shareholder value creation.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for QUAL is 22, suggesting that the ETF is trading at a slightly elevated valuation compared to historical averages. This premium reflects investor confidence in the quality of the underlying assets and their potential for long-term growth.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

QUAL’s P/E is higher than the broader international equity average of around 18. This valuation indicates that investors are willing to pay a premium for exposure to high-quality, low-volatility stocks typically found in QUAL.
