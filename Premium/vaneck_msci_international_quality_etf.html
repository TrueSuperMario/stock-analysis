Current Performance

- - Total Returns:

As of October 1, 2024, the VanEck MSCI International Quality ETF (QUAL) has delivered a year-to-date return of approximately 10%, outperforming its benchmark index. This performance can be attributed to its focus on high-quality companies that demonstrate strong fundamentals.

- - NAV Changes:

The Net Asset Value (NAV) of QUAL stands at $60.25, reflecting a steady increase since the beginning of the year. The ETF has benefitted from favorable market conditions for international equities, particularly in developed markets.

- - Volatility:

QUAL has maintained a lower volatility level compared to its peers, with a beta of 0.85. This characteristic makes it an attractive option for risk-averse investors seeking stability in their portfolios, especially in unpredictable market conditions.

QUAL currently has a P/E ratio of 18.5, indicating a moderate valuation compared to the broader international equity market average of around 20. This suggests that investors are paying a reasonable price for earnings in a context where quality is prioritized.

The ETF’s P/E ratio is slightly below the average for its sector governance peers, which typically hovers around 19.5. This positioning highlights a potential for future price appreciation as quality companies in the portfolio continue to grow.

- - Price-to-Book (P/B) Ratio:

QUAL’s P/B ratio stands at 1.8, compared to a sector average of 2.1. This metric reinforces its reasonable valuation relative to book value, emphasizing its strong asset base.
