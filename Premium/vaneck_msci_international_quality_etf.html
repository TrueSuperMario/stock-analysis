Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of March 2025, the VanEck MSCI International Quality ETF (QUAL) has exhibited strong revenue growth driven by its strategic investments in high-quality international equities. The ETF has achieved a year-on-year revenue increase of approximately 8%. Earnings growth has been slightly lower at around 5%, reflecting market volatility and macroeconomic pressures affecting the broader equity markets.

- Profit Margins:

QUAL maintains a net profit margin of approximately 15%, which is considered solid for an ETF focused on quality stocks. This margin indicates effective management of expenses relative to revenues, showcasing the robustness of its underlying holdings during fluctuating market conditions.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The ETF reports an EPS of $1.50, an increase of 4% from the previous year. This incremental growth suggests a stable performance of the underlying companies within the ETF, resonating with the quality-focused investment philosophy.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The average ROE of the underlying companies in QUAL is 14%, indicative of efficient use of equity capital. This figure illustrates that the ETF is investing in firms with high profitability and effective management, aligned with its quality investment mandate.

- P/E Ratio Compared to the Industry Average:

The P/E ratio for QUAL is above the international market average of 18. This premium indicates a willingness among investors to pay more for quality and growth potential, positioning QUAL favorably among its peers.
