Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

The VanEck MSCI International Quality ETF (QUAL) has demonstrated solid performance in 2023, with year-to-date returns of approximately 12.5%. This growth is largely attributed to the strength of its high-quality international holdings, which have outperformed many of their peers amid market volatility, benefiting from strong revenue growth in sectors like technology and healthcare.

- Profit Margins:

The ETF's underlying holdings have showcased robust profit margins, averaging around 15.8%. This reflects the quality focus of the ETF, as companies selected within QUAL typically exhibit strong operational efficiencies and cost management.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The weighted average EPS for companies within the ETF has increased by about 7% compared to the previous year, indicating resilient earnings performance across its core holdings.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The portfolio's ROE stands at an impressive 18%, demonstrating strong profitability and effective utilization of shareholder equity, characteristic of high-quality companies that prioritize returns.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current average P/E ratio for the ETF is approximately 22x earnings, indicating a premium valuation compared to the broader international equity market average of around 18x. This higher P/E reflects investor confidence in the quality of the underlying stocks.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

QUAL's P/E ratio is higher than the average for international quality-focused ETFs, suggesting that investors are willing to pay a premium for exposure to companies with resilient business models and strong financials.
