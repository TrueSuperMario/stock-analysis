Current Performance

- - Revenue and Earnings Growth:

The VanEck MSCI International Quality ETF (QUAL) has shown a robust performance in 2024, with year-to-date growth in net asset value of approximately 12%. This growth is attributed to the strong showing of quality international equities within its portfolio, particularly in sectors like consumer discretionary and technology, which have rebounded significantly in the current economic climate.

- - Profit Margins:

QUAL’s weighted average profit margin stands at approximately 15%, reflecting the high-quality focus of its underlying holdings. These companies typically demonstrate stronger operational efficiencies compared to their peers, contributing positively to the ETF's overall performance.

- - Earnings Per Share (EPS):

Since QUAL tracks companies with high return on equity and solid earnings, the average EPS growth for its holdings over the past year has been around 8%. This growth indicates that the companies within the ETF are successfully navigating current market challenges while capitalizing on growth opportunities.

- - Return on Equity (ROE):

The combined ROE for QUAL’s constituents is around 14%, underscoring the ETF’s focus on high-quality companies that effectively utilize shareholder equity to enhance profitability.

Valuation Metrics

- - Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

QUAL currently has a P/E ratio of 20, which suggests a relatively rich valuation compared to historical averages. This high valuation reflects the market's confidence in quality growth prospects amid a recovering global economy.

- - P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

The average P/E ratio for the broader international equity market is approximately 18. QUAL's higher P/E indicates that investors are willing to pay a premium for quality stocks that are expected to deliver consistent performance.
