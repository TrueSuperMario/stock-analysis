Current Performance

- - Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of September 30, 2024, the VanEck MSCI International Quality ETF (QUAL) has experienced a notable uptick in revenue growth, reaching approximately 8% year-on-year. This increase can be attributed to strong contributions from its high-quality international equity holdings, particularly in technology and consumer discretionary sectors, indicative of a rebound in global economic conditions post-pandemic.

- - Profit Margins:

The ETF maintains a healthy average profit margin of around 15% across its holdings. This solid profitability reflects better operational efficiency and cost management among its constituents, which predominantly consists of high-quality firms known for their robust competitive moats.

- - Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The weighted EPS of the constituent companies within QUAL is reported at $3.15, marking a 5% increase from the previous year. This growth, albeit moderate, aligns with the ETF's strategic focus on quality stocks known for delivering consistent earnings.

- - Return on Equity (ROE):

QUAL reflects an average ROE of about 14%, showcasing strong management effectiveness in converting equity investments into profit. This figure places the ETF above the global average ROE for the stock market, highlighting the quality of its underlying assets.

Valuation Metrics

- - Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for QUAL stands at 22, indicating a slightly elevated valuation compared to its historical average of 18. This premium suggests that the market is pricing in higher growth expectations from the quality stocks encompassed in the ETF.

- - P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

Compared to a broader international equity market average P/E of 20, QUAL's current P/E reflects the market's confidence in its high-quality selection and potential for sustainable growth amid fluctuations.
