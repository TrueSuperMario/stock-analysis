Current Performance

- Net Asset Value (NAV) Performance:

As of March 10, 2025, the Vanguard Diversified High Growth INDEX ETF (VDHG) has demonstrated solid performance, with a year-to-date return of approximately 8.5%. This is consistent with its objective of long-term capital growth through exposure to a diversified portfolio of growth-oriented assets.

- Performance Relative to Benchmark:

VDHG has outperformed its benchmark, the MSCI World Growth Index, which has recorded a year-to-date return of approximately 6.7%. This outperformance highlights VDHG's strategic asset allocation and effective management in capturing market upside.

- Risk Metrics:

The ETF exhibits a beta of 1.1, indicating a higher volatility compared to the broader market. Despite this, the risk-adjusted returns have been favorable, highlighting effective risk management strategies within its portfolio.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

VDHG has a current P/E ratio of 22, reflecting a premium valuation compared to the broader ETF market P/E of 19. This premium can be attributed to the ETF's focus on high-growth assets.

- Price-to-Book (P/B) Ratio:

The P/B ratio stands at 3.5, suggesting that investors are willing to pay a premium for the growth potential of underlying assets, particularly in technology and healthcare, which make up a significant portion of the ETF's holdings.
