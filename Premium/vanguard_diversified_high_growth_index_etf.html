Current Performance

- Performance Overview:

As of October 21, 2024, the Vanguard Diversified High Growth INDEX ETF (VDHG) has demonstrated strong performance in the high-growth investment sector. The ETF has increased approximately 12% year-to-date, benefiting from a recovery in equity markets and robust performances in technology and healthcare sectors.

- Asset Allocation:

VDHG comprises a diverse range of assets, predominantly favoring stocks (around 88%). The remainder is allocated to fixed income and cash, reflecting its high-growth strategy aimed at capital appreciation over income generation.

- Volatility Metrics:

The ETF has a beta of 1.2, indicating a higher volatility compared to the overall market. This aligns with its growth-focused strategy, as higher risk often accompanies potential for greater reward.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for VDHG stands at 23, suggesting that investors are willing to pay a premium for expected future earnings growth. This valuation is in line with other high-growth funds.

- Price-to-Book (P/B) Ratio:

VDHG has a P/B ratio of 3.5, which indicates that the ETF is trading above its book value. This is typical for growth-oriented funds that invest in companies with strong future growth potential.
