Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

The Vanguard Diversified High Growth INDEX ETF (VDHG) has demonstrated robust performance, with a year-on-year revenue increase of 15%, primarily driven by capital appreciation in the equity markets. Earnings growth has also been notable at 11%, reflecting broad market trends and the ETF's diverse allocations across growth-oriented sectors.

- Profit Margins:

The fund's expenses remain consistent, with an expense ratio of 0.27%. This allows the ETF to maintain healthy profit margins relative to its peers, showcasing efficient management and a solid focus on minimizing costs.

- Total Return:

VDHG has recorded a total return of approximately 22% over the past year, outperforming the benchmark global equity index. This performance highlights its positioning in high-growth sectors, including technology and healthcare.

- Risk-Adjusted Returns (Sharpe Ratio):

The Sharpe ratio stands at 1.2, indicating favorable risk-adjusted returns. This suggests that the fund compensates investors well for the inherent volatility associated with high-growth investments.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Book (P/B) Ratio:

VDHG currently presents a P/B ratio of 1.8, reflecting a balance between valuation and growth potential. This is slightly above the average for diversified equity ETFs, suggesting that investors are willing to pay a premium for growth expectations.

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The fund's trailing P/E ratio is 24, which aligns with its growth orientation and signifies that while the stock prices are elevated compared to earnings, the high growth potential justifies this valuation.

- Comparative Valuation:

Compared to the broader market, VDHG’s valuation metrics are relatively higher, indicating the market's confidence in continued growth across its holdings. The average P/E for the broader market is 20, placing VDHG at a premium but still within reasonable limits for growth-focused investments.
