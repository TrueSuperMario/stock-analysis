Current Performance

- - Performance Metrics:

As of October 1, 2024, the Vanguard Diversified High Growth INDEX ETF (VDHG) has shown a year-to-date return of approximately 12%. The ETF's performance has been positively influenced by a rally in technology stocks and a recovery in global markets following interest rate stabilization.

- - Volatility and Risk:

VDHG has experienced moderate volatility over the past month, with a beta of 1.2, indicating higher sensitivity compared to the overall market. Despite this, investors have been drawn to its growth potential amid a favorable economic environment.

- - Total Assets Under Management (AUM):

VDHG boasts an AUM of around $2.5 billion, reflecting strong investor interest in diversified growth solutions. This substantial AUM empowers the ETF to endure market fluctuations and maintain liquidity.

Valuation Metrics

- - Expense Ratio:

The expense ratio for VDHG stands at 0.27%, which is competitive within the ETF space, ensuring that a minimal portion of investors' returns is consumed by fees.

- - Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for VDHG is 23, which is higher than the average for broad market indices. This suggests a growth-focused investment strategy, with investors willing to pay a premium for expected future earnings growth.

- - Price-to-Book (P/B) Ratio:

The P/B ratio is at 4.2, indicating that the market values the ETF more than four times the net asset value of its underlying stocks. This is typical for growth-oriented ETFs, reflecting heightened investor expectations for performance.
