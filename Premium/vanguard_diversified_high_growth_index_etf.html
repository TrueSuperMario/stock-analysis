Current Performance

- - Revenue and Earnings Growth:

Vanguard Diversified High Growth INDEX ETF (VDHG) has demonstrated robust performance over the last year, with annualized returns averaging around 10%. This performance is largely driven by significant gains in the equity markets, particularly in technology and healthcare sectors, aligning with VDHG’s high-growth investment strategy.

- - Profit Margins:

While ETFs do not have profit margins in the traditional sense, the underlying assets in VDHG have seen healthy operating margins. The fund's focus on growth-oriented sectors tends to result in higher margins for owned companies, particularly in the tech industry, where many investee companies operate.

- - Earnings Per Share (EPS):

As an ETF, VDHG does not report EPS akin to individual companies; however, the underlying holdings collectively exhibit strong earnings growth trends. The earnings growth supported by high-growth companies suggests positive long-term performance potential for the fund.

- - Return on Equity (ROE):

Similar to EPS, ROE is not directly applicable to ETFs. However, the underlying equity investments in high-growth firms reflect strong ROE metrics, typically exceeding 15%, indicative of effective management and profitable operations within the sectors VDHG targets.

Valuation Metrics

- - Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for VDHG’s underlying investments is approximately 24, suggesting that the fund is currently trading at a premium compared to the broader market average of around 20. This reflects investor confidence in the high-growth strategy despite elevated valuations.

- - P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

When comparing to similarly focused funds, VDHG's P/E is on the higher side, reflecting its tilt towards rapid growth sectors, which tend to command higher valuations due to expected future earnings.
