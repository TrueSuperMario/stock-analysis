Current Performance

Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of September 2024, Vanguard Diversified High Growth INDEX ETF (VDHG) has shown a robust revenue growth of approximately 12% year-on-year. This performance has been driven by strong gains in global technology and healthcare sectors, which constitute significant portions of the ETF's holdings. However, due to market volatility, earnings growth has been more conservative, at around 7%.

Profit Margins:

While VDHG does not directly report profit margins (as it is an index fund), its underlying assets have exhibited solid operating margins, averaging around 15% across its major holdings. This indicates a healthy capacity to generate profits, even amid market fluctuations.

Earnings Per Share (EPS):

VDHG, being an ETF, doesn't have traditional EPS figures. However, the earnings growth of its underlying securities implies that investors could expect a rise in the ETF's projected earnings per unit, albeit in a volatile market environment.

Return on Equity (ROE):

The ROE of the major holdings within VDHG showcases a robust average of about 14%, reflecting effective utilization of shareholder equity in generating profits, which is significant given the growth-focused nature of the ETF.

Valuation Metrics

Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

VDHG currently has an estimated P/E ratio of 22, suggesting it is moderately valued considering its growth orientation. This is indicative of strong market confidence in the growth potential of the ETF's underlying holdings.

P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

The ETF's P/E ratio is above the broad market average of approximately 19. This premium valuation is reflective of the high growth potential anticipated in the technology and healthcare sectors, which dominate the index.

VDHG has a consistent dividend payment history, with slight increases annually, reflecting the underlying strength of the companies within the ETF and their ability to generate sustainable earnings.
