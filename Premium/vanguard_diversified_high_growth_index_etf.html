Current Performance

- Net Asset Value (NAV) Growth:

As of January 27, 2025, the Vanguard Diversified High Growth INDEX ETF (VDHG) has shown considerable strength with a year-to-date NAV increase of approximately 8%. This growth is buoyed by strong performance in equity markets, especially in technology and healthcare sectors, which are prominent within the ETF’s holdings.

- Performance Compared to Benchmarks:

VDHG has outperformed its benchmark index, showing a 2% higher return than the average performance of its underlying index over the last quarter. This is indicative of effective management of the fund and favorable allocation strategies that have enhanced its performance relative to market movements.

- Total Return:

The total return for the ETF, including price appreciation and reinvested dividends, has reached around 12% over the past 12 months. This strong performance underscores the ETF’s focus on growth-oriented sectors and its diversified approach, which has mitigated risks associated with market volatility.

Valuation Metrics

- Expense Ratio:

VDHG boasts a low expense ratio of 0.27%, which is competitive compared to other growth-focused ETFs. This low cost structure is advantageous for long-term investors, as it allows more of the returns to be passed onto shareholders.

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The fund’s weighted average P/E ratio stands at 22, suggesting that, while the ETF may be priced slightly higher than some traditional benchmarks, it reflects the growth prospects of the underlying companies. This is consistent with the high-growth strategy of VDHG.
