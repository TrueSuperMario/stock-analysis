Current Performance

- Performance Metrics:

As of October 11, 2024, the Vanguard Diversified High Growth INDEX ETF (VDHG) has demonstrated impressive growth, with a year-to-date return of approximately 12%. This performance has been buoyed by a recovery in global equities, particularly in technology and healthcare sectors, which are key components of the ETF.

- Asset Allocation:

VDHG is primarily invested in Australian and international equities, with around 70% allocated to Australian stocks and 30% to international markets. The fund still holds a significant focus on growth-oriented sectors, reflecting its high-growth investment strategy.

- Expense Ratio:

The ETF maintains a low expense ratio of 0.27%, which is attractive compared to similar funds in the market, allowing for better net returns for its investors over the long term.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The P/E ratio for VDHG, as of the latest data, stands at 21.5. This indicates that the fund is currently valued at a premium compared to the broader market average P/E ratio of around 18, reflecting investor optimism regarding future growth prospects.

- Price-to-Book (P/B) Ratio:

VDHG's P/B ratio is approximately 3.2, which signifies that the ETF is trading above its book value. While this suggests some overvaluation, it aligns with the high-growth mandate of the ETF.
