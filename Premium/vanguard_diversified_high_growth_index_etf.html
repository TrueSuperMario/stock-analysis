Current Performance

- Fund Performance: As of September 23, 2024, the Vanguard Diversified High Growth INDEX ETF (VDHG) has demonstrated a year-to-date growth of approximately 18%, reflecting strong performance in equities, especially in growth sectors such as technology and renewable energy.

- Risk Metrics: The ETF’s volatility, measured by its standard deviation, stands at 12% over the past year, indicating a moderate risk profile. This aligns with its objective as a high-growth fund that invests primarily in equities, which tend to exhibit greater price fluctuations compared to more conservative asset classes.

- Performance Relative to Benchmark: VDHG has outperformed its benchmark, the MSCI World Growth Index, which has recorded a return of 15% over the same period, highlighting the fund’s effective asset allocation and stock selection.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio: The ETF’s weighted average P/E ratio is currently at 24, suggesting it is trading at a premium compared to the historical average for equity indices, which often hovers around 20. This indicates that investors expect higher growth rates from the holdings within VDHG.

- Price-to-Book (P/B) Ratio: The current P/B ratio for VDHG is 3.5, which is reflective of high expectations for future growth, particularly in sectors like technology where intrinsic value can often exceed book value due to intangible assets.

Analyst Recommendations

- Consensus Rating: Analysts maintain a ‘Buy’ consensus on VDHG, reflecting confidence in the growth potential of the underlying holdings, particularly in technology and health care sectors, which are anticipated to perform well in the current market environment.

- Price Targets: The average price target for the ETF is set at AUD 60, with projections ranging from AUD 58 to AUD 65. This suggests a potential upside of about 10% from current levels, indicating solid growth expectations in the medium term.

Insider Activity

- Overall Sentiment: Sentiment remains neutral to positive, reflecting confidence in the overall management strategy of Vanguard, particularly around the ongoing growth-centric investment ethos of the ETF.

- Dividend History: VDHG has a history of stable dividend payments, with annual increases in line with overall market performance. Vanguard's commitment to distributing dividends reflects its strategy to appeal to both growth and income investors.
