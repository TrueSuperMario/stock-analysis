Current Performance

- - Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of September 30, 2024, the Vanguard Australian Property Securities Index ETF (VAP) has demonstrated a revenue increase of approximately 4% year-on-year, largely driven by a resurgence in the Australian property market and increased investor interest in real estate securities. Earnings have similarly improved, with a modest 3% growth reflecting enhanced operational efficiency among the underlying property assets.

- - Profit Margins:

VAP's net profit margin is currently estimated at around 12%. This is considered healthy for a real estate ETF, given the competitive landscape. The margins have been supported by effective management strategies executed by the underlying property companies, which have been able to optimize their operational costs.

- - Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The ETF currently shows an EPS of $0.85, indicating an increase from the previous reporting period. This improvement is tied to the strong performance of its constituent REITs (Real Estate Investment Trusts), which have benefited from rising rental income and increased occupancy rates.

- - Return on Equity (ROE):

The average ROE for the underlying assets within VAP is about 10%. This figure signifies a robust utilization of equity among the listed property trusts, indicating effective management in generating returns for shareholders.

Valuation Metrics

- - Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

VAP is currently trading at a P/E ratio of 22, highlighting a moderately high valuation relative to its earnings capacity. This reflects optimism in the recovery of the Australian property market and the expected growth of the underlying securities.

- - P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

The P/E ratio of VAP is significantly above the Australian property sector average of approximately 18. This premium valuation suggests that investors are willing to pay more for VAP due to its diversified exposure to potentially high-growth property securities.
