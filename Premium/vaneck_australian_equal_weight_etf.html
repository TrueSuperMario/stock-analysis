Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of October 16, 2024, the VanEck Australian Equal Weight ETF (MVW) has showcased a revenue growth rate of approximately 7% year-on-year, primarily driven by the strong performance of the sectors it is weighted in, which include financials, materials, and healthcare. Earnings growth has followed suit, coming in at around 6%, reflecting solid operational discipline among the ETF's constituent companies.

- Profit Margins:

The ETF's average profit margin stands at 12%, which is commendable given Australia’s competitive market landscape. This level of profitability is driven by the robust performance of key sectors, coupled with a strategic focus on larger, more stable companies within the equal-weighted framework.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The trailing twelve months EPS for MVW is around $1.85, showing a 5% increase compared to the previous fiscal year. This rise indicates consistent performance across its diversified holdings.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The weighted average ROE of the companies included in MVW is approximately 13%, suggesting effective management and a solid ability to generate returns on shareholder equity, particularly in the financial and materials sectors that dominate the ETF’s holdings.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for MVW is approximately 18, which aligns closely with the broader Australian equity market average. This valuation reflects a healthy balance between earnings potential and investor expectations.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

MVW's P/E ratio is slightly above the industry average of 16 for Australian ETFs, indicating a moderate premium. This premium can be attributed to its equal-weight strategy, which enhances exposure to smaller companies and those with higher growth potential.
