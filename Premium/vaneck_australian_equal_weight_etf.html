Current Performance

- - Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of September 24, 2024, the Vaneck Australian EQUAL Weight ETF (MVW) has shown a revenue growth rate of approximately 8% year-on-year, attributed to rising valuations in Australian equities across diverse sectors. Earnings growth has been robust at around 9%, reflecting the overall strong performance of the underlying equal-weighted stock portfolio amidst favorable economic conditions.

- - Profit Margins:

MVW’s net profit margin is reported at 11%, indicative of reasonable profitability that mirrors Australia's diversified market landscape. This margin is strong for an ETF structure, suggesting efficient management of costs relative to income generated from its holdings.

- - Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The ETF’s current EPS stands at $1.05, illustrating a year-over-year increase of 3%. This growth shows resilience in the face of fluctuating market conditions, supported by the equal-weighting strategy that minimizes overreliance on large-cap stocks.

- - Return on Equity (ROE):

The ROE for the ETF is approximately 10.5%, which is commendable for an index-based fund. This figure reflects effective utilization of assets and equity in generating returns.

MVW’s current P/E ratio is 18, suggesting a moderate valuation relative to future earnings potential. This ratio reflects the overall buoyancy of the Australian stock market, as well as investor confidence in sectors represented within the ETF.

- - P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

The P/E ratio of 18 is slightly above the Australian equity market average of around 16. This premium valuation is evidence of positive market sentiment towards the ETF's balanced and diversified investment approach.
