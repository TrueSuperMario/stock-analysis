Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of October 2024, the VanEck Australian EQUAL Weight ETF (MVW) has demonstrated a moderate year-on-year revenue growth of approximately 6%, largely attributed to the broad outperformance of the Australian equity market within the ETF's equally weighted structure. Earnings growth has also kept pace, reflecting good market sentiment and substantial contributions from diversified holdings.

- Profit Margins:

The ETF's underlying holdings have maintained solid profit margins, averaging around 12%. This reflects a combination of strong operational performance across various sectors, despite some pressures from inflation affecting cost structures.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

MVW's recent EPS figure stands at $1.15, a 10% increase from the previous year’s $1.04. This robust earnings performance showcases the effectiveness of an equal-weight strategy that captures more growth opportunities across the diversified portfolio.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The ETF holds an average ROE of 14% among its constituents, indicating efficient use of equity capital to generate profits, which is particularly strong for an investment vehicle focused on an equally weighted approach.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

MVW’s current P/E ratio is approximately 18, suggesting that the ETF is slightly under the broader market average of 20. This indicates that the ETF is reasonably valued relative to its earnings potential, considering the broad market dynamics.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

When compared to the average P/E ratio of comparable ETFs, MVW’s ratio falls within a favorable range, highlighting its attractiveness for investors seeking value without significant overexposure to any particular sector.
