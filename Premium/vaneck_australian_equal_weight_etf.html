As of September 2024, VanEck Australian EQUAL Weight ETF (MVW) has reported a year-to-date return of approximately 9%. Earnings growth for the underlying companies within the ETF has been around 6%, indicative of stable corporate profits across the sectors represented in the fund.

- - Profit Margins:

The collective net profit margin of the underlying portfolio holdings within MVW currently averages around 11%. This level of profitability is reassuring, particularly in the context of slight inflationary pressures that have affected costs across the market.

- - Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The ETF's underlying constituents reported an average EPS of $1.20, reflecting a healthy increase of 4% compared to the previous year. This modest growth signifies consistent profitability and effective management practices across the portfolio.

- - Return on Equity (ROE):

The weighted average ROE of the companies within MVW stands at 15%, suggesting strong utilization of equity capital to generate profits. This figure is well above the broader market average and is a positive indicator of management effectiveness across the diversified holdings.
