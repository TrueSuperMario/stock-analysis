Current Performance

- Fund Performance:

The VanEck Australian EQUAL Weight ETF (MVW) has delivered a return of approximately 10% year-to-date as of October 26, 2024. This performance has been supported by a diverse allocation across sectors, reducing risks associated with individual stock volatility, and reflecting the broader Australian equity market trends.

- Net Asset Value (NAV):

As of the latest report, the NAV of MVW stands at around AUD 16.50 per share, showing a slight premium over its market price due to buyers actively pursuing this diversified fund amidst a positive market environment.

- Volatility:

MVW has demonstrated a standard deviation of returns lower than the ASX 200 index, which indicates a lower volatility profile. With the recent stabilization in the Australian equity market, controlled by interest rate movements and commodity prices, MVW's strategy has effectively cushioned against significant downturns.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for MVW is about 18, which is marginally higher than the ASX 200 average of 16. This suggests the fund may be trading at a premium, possibly due to its equal-weighting strategy that focuses on smaller and mid-cap companies that have potential for growth.

- Price-to-Book (P/B) Ratio:

MVW's P/B ratio currently stands at 1.9, indicating a belief in stronger future earnings prospects compared to its book value. This ratio highlights the market's expectations for performance from its underlying holdings.
