Current Performance

- Fund Performance:

As of October 29, 2024, the VanEck Australian Equal Weight ETF (MVW) has delivered a year-to-date total return of approximately 10%. This performance has been buoyed by strong gains in the Australian equities market, particularly in the Financials and Consumer Discretionary sectors.

- Relative Strength:

MVW has outperformed the broader Australian market index (S&P/ASX 200), which has reported a year-to-date return of around 7%. The equal-weighting strategy allows MVW to avoid overexposure to larger corporations, resulting in a more balanced performance across different sectors.

- Volatility:

The ETF has exhibited relatively moderate volatility, with a standard deviation of returns of approximately 12% over the past year, indicating a lower risk profile compared to other equity-focused index funds.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

MVW's current P/E ratio stands at 17, suggesting a competitive valuation compared to the broader market average of 18. This lower P/E reflects the equal-weight strategy's focus on mid-sized companies, which can often be undervalued compared to mega caps.

- Price-to-Book (P/B) Ratio:

The price-to-book ratio for MVW is around 1.5, which is consistent with historical values for Australian equities, indicating fair valuation relative to book value as compared to industry peers.
