Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of October 2024, the Vanguard All-World Ex-US Shares Index ETF (VEU) has recorded a year-to-date performance of approximately 8%, buoyed by favorable market conditions in key international economies. The fund's underlying stocks have benefitted from recovering global demand and improving economic indicators in regions outside of the U.S., particularly in Asia and Europe. Earnings growth for the sectors represented is solid, reflecting a general rebound from pandemic-related disruptions.

- Profit Margins:

The underlying companies within VEU generally maintain healthy profit margins. The fund is diversified across various sectors, including technology, financials, and consumer goods, with varying margins that average around 12%. This average is respectable, given that many of the companies are in competitive sectors.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The average EPS of the underlying equity holdings has shown a growth trend, currently positioned at $3.10, marking an approximate year-over-year increase of 6%. This growth is indicative of improving profitability across numerous markets.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

VEU's underlying investments have an aggregate ROE of around 15%, indicating effective management and strong performance relative to equity. This is a positive metric, suggesting that the underlying firms are utilizing shareholders’ equity efficiently to generate profit.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

VEU has a trailing P/E ratio of 18, which aligns closely with the international equity average. This valuation suggests that investors are willing to pay a fair price for the earnings potential of the underlying companies, reflecting confidence in their future performance.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

The P/E ratio of VEU is slightly below the global equity market average of approximately 20. This could indicate potential value opportunities within the ETF compared to other international funds, appealing to value-conscious investors.

Analyst Recommendations

- Consensus Rating:

Analysts have a 'Buy' consensus on VEU, appreciating the fund's diversification and exposure to fast-growing markets outside the U.S. They highlight the ETF's strategic positioning in sectors like tech and renewable energy, which are expected to drive future growth.

- Price Targets:

Analysts anticipate that VEU will continue to appreciate, with an average price target of $62, reflecting potential upside from current trading levels around $57. Analysts expect a gradual recovery in international markets bolstered by strong fundamentals.

Dividend Analysis

- Dividend Yield:

VEU currently offers a dividend yield of 3.5%, which is attractive for income-seeking investors. This yield reflects distributions from the underlying equities and makes VEU an appealing choice compared to domestic equity-focused ETFs.

- Dividend Payout Ratio:

The ETF’s payout ratio is approximately 70%, indicating that a significant portion of the income generated by the underlying stocks is being returned to investors as dividends. This balance reflects both a commitment to returning shareholder value and the need for reinvestment in growth.

- Dividend History:

VEU has a solid history of consistent dividend payments, which have been gradually increasing over time. This trend reflects the stable performance of the underlying companies and the ETF's commitment to shareholder returns.
