Current Performance

- Fund Performance:

Vanguard All-World Ex-US Shares INDEX ETF (VEU) has shown a 7% year-to-date return as of October 29, 2024. This performance reflects a recovery in global markets post-pandemic, although it has lagged behind some major U.S. indices, partly due to geopolitical tensions and currency fluctuations affecting non-U.S. investments.

- Tracking Error:

VEU has maintained a low tracking error of approximately 0.2%, indicating that it closely follows the performance of its benchmark index, the FTSE All-World ex US Index. This efficiency highlights the fund’s strong operational management.

- Net Asset Value (NAV):

The current NAV for VEU is approximately $52.50, which reflects fluctuations in the global equity markets and the various currencies in which it operates.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

As of the latest report, VEU has a P/E ratio of 15, suggesting it is relatively attractively priced compared to historical measures and global averages for equities.

- Price-to-Book (P/B) Ratio:

The P/B ratio stands at 1.8, indicating that the ETF is slightly above the global average but within a reasonable range considering the higher potential for growth in developing markets.

Summary

The Vanguard All-World Ex-US Shares INDEX ETF (VEU) is a compelling investment option for those looking to diversify their portfolios internationally.
