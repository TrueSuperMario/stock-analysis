Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of the latest update, the Vanguard All-World Ex-US Shares INDEX ETF (VEU) has seen a year-to-date performance increase of approximately 12%. This reflects a recovery in international markets, particularly in emerging economies, which have been buoyed by improving economic indicators and easing geopolitical tensions.

- Profit Margins:

Since VEU is an ETF, traditional profit margins don’t apply, but the fund effectively tracks the performance of its underlying assets. The expense ratio stands at 0.08%, which is low compared to many competitors, ensuring that a larger portion of the investment return is passed on to shareholders.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

As an index fund, VEU does not report EPS in the traditional sense. Instead, its performance is indicative of market trends in global equity markets outside the U.S., reflecting a blend of large-, mid-, and small-cap growth and value stocks.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

VEU consists of a diversified portfolio that includes various equity holdings. Therefore, specific ROE metrics do not apply at the ETF level but are reflective of constituent company performances, many of which report ROE values in the 10%-20% range.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current weighted average P/E ratio for the underlying holdings in VEU is approximately 15.6, which is seen as fairly valued relative to historical averages and suggests moderate growth expectations from international markets.

- P/E Ratio Compared to the Industry Average:

VEU’s valuation is in line with its peers in the international equity ETF space, which typically hover around a P/E ratio of 15-16. This indicates that VEU offers reasonably priced exposure to global markets.
