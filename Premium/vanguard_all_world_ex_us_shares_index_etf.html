Current Performance

- Price Movement:

As of October 18, 2024, the Vanguard All-World Ex-US Shares INDEX ETF (VEU) is trading at approximately $59.50 per share, reflecting a year-to-date performance of about 12%. This performance is underpinned by a strong recovery in international markets, particularly in Asia and Europe.

- Return Metrics:

VEU has delivered a trailing twelve-month return of approximately 15%, outperforming many of its peers in the international equity space. This strong return has been supported by a combination of robust corporate earnings from non-U.S. companies and macroeconomic stability in regions outside of the United States.

- Expense Ratio:

The fund maintains a low expense ratio of 0.08%, which is favorable compared to the average for international equity ETFs. This efficient cost structure contributes positively to net returns for investors.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for VEU stands at around 18.5, which is slightly above the historical average of 17. This suggests that while the fund is trading at a premium, it reflects market confidence in the growth potential of non-U.S. equities.

- P/B Ratio:

The price-to-book (P/B) ratio is approximately 1.5, signaling that the ETF is valuing its holdings in line with long-term growth prospects in the international market, particularly within emerging markets where growth rates are typically higher.
