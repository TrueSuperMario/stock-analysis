Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of January 26, 2025, the Vanguard All-World Ex-US Shares INDEX ETF (VEU) has achieved a revenue growth rate of approximately 7% year-over-year. This growth reflects a rebound in global equities as markets stabilize post-pandemic, with emerging markets contributing significantly to revenue increases. Earnings growth is at 5%, slightly lagging behind revenue due to increased operational expenses and some volatility in currency exchange rates.

- Profit Margins:

The ETF’s profit margins are estimated to be around 12%, with variances across the different regions and sectors it encompasses. Margins are impacted by strong performances in technology and consumer discretionary sectors outside the U.S., balanced by pressures from energy and materials sectors.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The current EPS for VEU stands at approximately $1.40, which shows a 6% increase compared to last year. This increase is attributed to a recovering global economy and improved corporate earnings in non-U.S. markets.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

VEU reports an ROE of 11%, indicating a solid return relative to its equity base. This metric is reflective of the diversified holdings in the ETF, capturing performance across many high-growth regions and sectors.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for VEU is 18, which suggests that the ETF is moderately valued based on its earnings potential. This valuation is reasonable considering the varied growth rates across its international constituents.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

VEU’s P/E ratio is slightly above the broader average for international equity ETFs, which generally hover around 16. This premium valuation could be justified by the ETF's geographical diversification and growth potential in emerging markets.
