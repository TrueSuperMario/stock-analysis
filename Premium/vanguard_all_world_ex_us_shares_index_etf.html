Current Performance

- Recent Returns:

The Vanguard All-World Ex-US Shares INDEX ETF (VEU) has experienced a year-to-date return of approximately 8.3%, showcasing resilience amid fluctuating global markets. The fund's focus on developed and emerging markets outside the U.S. has provided a buffer against localized downturns.

- Underlying Index Performance:

VEU tracks the FTSE All-World ex US Index, which has benefited from strong performance in certain emerging markets, particularly in Asia. This has counterbalanced weaker performance in Europe amid geopolitical tensions and economic challenges.

- Annualized Returns:

Over a three-year period, VEU has delivered annualized returns of 9.1%, slightly lower than its five-year annualized return of 10.5%, indicating a slight slowdown in growth momentum but still reflecting solid long-term performance.

Valuation Metrics

- Expense Ratio:

VEU has an expense ratio of 0.08%, which is very competitive compared to other international equity ETFs. This low fee structure augurs well for cost-conscious investors seeking exposure to global equity markets.

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The fund has a trailing P/E ratio of 15.6, which is relatively low compared to the global average of 18. This suggests potential undervaluation, especially in relation to anticipated earnings growth in several emerging markets.

- Price-to-Book (P/B) Ratio:

VEU's P/B ratio stands at 1.4, indicating that investors are paying a reasonable price relative to the underlying book value of the companies within the ETF.
