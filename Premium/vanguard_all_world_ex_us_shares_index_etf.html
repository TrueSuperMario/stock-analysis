Current Performance

- - Revenue and Earnings Growth:

The Vanguard All-World Ex-US Shares INDEX ETF (VEU) has shown resilience in its revenue generation, fueled by the performance of underlying international equities. As of October 2024, the fund has reported a year-to-date return of approximately 8%, which reflects a recovery in many non-U.S. markets post-pandemic. Earnings growth for the underlying companies in the fund has been steady, driven by recovering global demand and improved market conditions.

- - Profit Margins:

VEU's holdings primarily comprise large-cap international companies with robust profit margins that average around 15%. These profit margins are considered healthy for international investments, allowing for reinvestment in growth opportunities across various sectors.

- - Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The aggregate EPS for the underlying holdings in VEU has increased by about 5% year-on-year, as companies adapt to inflationary pressures and increased operational efficiencies. This growth indicates a positive trend in profitability and reflects improving economic conditions in many non-U.S. regions.

- - Return on Equity (ROE):

The average ROE for VEU’s constituents stands at 12%, showcasing an ability to generate profits effectively from shareholder equity. This performance metric suggests that VEU maintains a diversified and well-selected portfolio of international companies.

Valuation Metrics

- - Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for VEU is approximately 18, indicating moderate valuation compared to historical averages. This ratio reflects the market's optimistic outlook on the recovery of international markets.

The P/E ratio for VEU is slightly higher than the global equity average of 17, hinting at a premium valuation driven by the fund’s exposure to high-growth markets that are expected to outperform the developed markets in the long run.
