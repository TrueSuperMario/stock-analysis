Current Performance

- Asset Allocation:

The iShares Global 100 ETF (IOO) primarily invests in 100 of the largest multinational companies, providing a significant exposure to large-cap stocks across various sectors. As of March 17, 2025, the ETF has demonstrated a robust performance, with a year-to-date return of approximately 8%, reflecting a broad rebound in global equities.

- Performance Relative to Benchmark:

IOO has outperformed its benchmark index, the MSCI All Country World Index, which has posted a return of 6% during the same period. This performance is attributed to the strong recovery of tech stocks and a favorable global economic outlook.

- Volatility:

The ETF has exhibited a low volatility profile, with a beta of around 0.85, indicating that it is less volatile than the broader market. This makes it an attractive option for conservative investors looking to gain exposure to global equities.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio of IOO stands at 24, which suggests that the ETF is slightly above the historical average for large-cap stocks (approximately 20). This indicates that investors are willing to pay a premium for anticipated future growth.

- Price-to-Book (P/B) Ratio:

The P/B ratio is around 3.5, which shows that the market values the underlying assets at a premium, reflecting confidence in the earnings growth potential of the constituent companies.

- Comparison with Peers:

When compared to similar ETFs within the large-cap growth category, IOO's valuation metrics are competitive, albeit on the higher side, indicating a stronger growth outlook among its holdings.

Analyst Recommendations

- Consensus Rating:

The consensus rating among analysts for IOO is "Buy," reflecting optimism about the continued economic recovery and strong fundamentals of the underlying companies within the ETF.

- Price Targets:

Analysts project an average price target of $95 per share over the next 12 months, suggesting an upside potential of approximately 15% from its current price of $82.

- Dividend Growth:

The ETF has a record of gradually increasing its dividend payments, although the growth has been conservative, consistent with its investment in large-cap growth stocks.

The iShares Global 100 ETF (IOO) presents a robust investment opportunity with a strong performance year-to-date, outperforming its benchmark.
