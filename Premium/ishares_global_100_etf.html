Current Performance

- - Performance Overview:

As of September 30, 2024, the iShares Global 100 ETF (IOO) has seen a year-to-date return of approximately 15%, reflecting strong performance driven by key global companies, particularly in tech and consumer discretionary sectors. The ETF continues to benefit from its focus on large-cap companies with stable growth prospects.

- - Top Holdings Contribution:

The top holdings, including well-known names like Apple, Microsoft, and Amazon, have significantly contributed to IOO's performance. The tech sector remains a key driver, with these companies benefiting from ongoing digital transformation trends that have heightened demand for technology services.

- - Expense Ratio:

The ETF boasts a low expense ratio of 0.40%, favorably positioning it for long-term investors as it minimizes the cost of investing while delivering diversified exposure to global equity markets.

Valuation Metrics

- - Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

IOO’s current P/E ratio is approximately 25, indicating that investors are willing to pay a premium for the earnings of the companies within the ETF, reflecting confidence in their growth potential.

- - P/E Ratio Comparison:

Compared to the broader market average P/E of around 20, IOO’s elevated ratio underscores the growth expectations surrounding its holdings, particularly in technology and healthcare sectors.

- - Price-to-Book (P/B) Ratio:

The P/B ratio stands at 4.5, suggesting that the market has high expectations for the future performance of the companies in the ETF relative to their book value.
