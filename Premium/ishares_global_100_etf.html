Current Performance

- - Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of October 2024, the Ishares Global 100 ETF (IOO) has seen a robust recovery with a year-to-date revenue growth of approximately 8%. This growth is primarily driven by the rebound in global markets and a recovery in key sectors like technology, consumer goods, and healthcare, which make up a significant portion of IOO's holdings. Earnings growth for the underlying companies within the ETF is estimated to be around 5%, reflecting improved economic conditions and solid corporate performance.

- - Profit Margins:

The effective profit margins of the companies within IOO vary depending on the sector, but they generally remain strong, averaging around 15%. This is particularly noteworthy given the ongoing inflationary pressures that have impacted many industries globally.

- - Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The weighted average EPS for the underlying entities in IOO is reported at $4.15, which is a 6% increase from the previous year. This growth reflects the positive momentum in corporate earnings across the ETF's diversified holdings.

- - Return on Equity (ROE):

The ROE for the major companies within IOO is around 14%, suggesting that the ETF continues to benefit from high levels of efficiency in generating profits from shareholder equity investments.

Valuation Metrics

- - Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for IOO is approximately 22, indicating that the ETF is moderately priced considering its earnings generation. This is reflective of a premium valuation often associated with global leaders in their respective industries.

- - P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

The P/E ratio of 22 is above the average of 19 for global equity ETFs, indicating a slight overvaluation but justified by the high quality and growth potential of the underlying companies, which include major players with strong market positions.
