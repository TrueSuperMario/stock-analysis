Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of October 2024, the iShares Global 100 ETF (IOO) has shown a year-to-date return of approximately 15%, driven by strong performance in technology and healthcare sectors. The ETF's asset growth is bolstered by a rebound in global markets following soft economic conditions last year, and earnings growth for its underlying holdings is estimated around 10% for the current financial year.

- Profit Margins:

The profitability of companies within the iShares Global 100 ETF remains robust, with an aggregate net profit margin of around 12% across its top holdings. This stability reflects strong brand positioning and pricing power in key segments, such as technology and consumer staples.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The weighted average EPS of the ETF’s constituents is approximately $8.50, reflecting a 6% increase from the previous year. This growth is primarily attributed to the recovery in consumer spending and increased demand for tech products post-pandemic.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The ETF shows a strong median ROE of 15%, illustrating effective management and strong profitability for the best-in-class companies featured in the fund's portfolio.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for the iShares Global 100 ETF is 23, which is slightly above the broader market average of 21. This premium reflects the high growth expectations for the top companies within the portfolio, particularly in technology and consumer discretionary sectors.

- Price-to-Book (P/B) Ratio:

The P/B ratio stands at 3.5, indicating that the ETF is trading at a significant premium compared to the book value of its underlying assets. This suggests investors are willing to pay more for the quality and growth potential of the companies it represents.

- Comparison to Indexes:

Compared to the S&P 500, which has a P/E of around 20, IOO’s higher valuation metrics indicate a preference for growth-oriented companies, particularly in mega-cap sectors that dominate its holdings.
