Current Performance

- - Revenue and Earnings Growth:

The iShares Global 100 ETF (IOO) has shown a resilient performance over the year, with a reported year-to-date return of approximately 12%. This growth is primarily driven by strong performances from major global companies within its portfolio, particularly in technology and healthcare sectors. Earnings growth per underlying company has averaged around 10%, supported by robust consumer demand and recovery from pandemic-related disruptions.

- - Profit Margins:

The ETF's profit margins are indirectly reflected through its diverse holdings, with multiple companies exhibiting healthy net profit margins averaging around 15%. Notable contributors include large market players in technology like Apple and Microsoft, which consistently report high margins due to their dominant market positions and brand loyalty.

- - Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The underlying companies within IOO have reported an average EPS growth of about 8% year-over-year, showcasing a sustainable upward trajectory. This consistent EPS growth underscores the solid fundamentals of these leading global companies.

- - Return on Equity (ROE):

The ETF's holdings exhibit impressive ROE figures, averaging around 20%, indicating effective management of shareholder equity among the constituent companies. This level of ROE is particularly notable given the capital-intensive nature of some sectors represented within the fund.

Valuation Metrics

- - Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

IOO's current P/E ratio stands at approximately 24, suggesting that the fund is trading at a premium relative to its earnings potential. This reflects the market's confidence in the growth capabilities of its high-quality constituents.

- - P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

The P/E ratio of 24 is higher than the global equity market average of about 21. This premium valuation is indicative of the growth expectations investors have for the leading companies within the ETF, particularly in technology and consumer discretionary sectors.
