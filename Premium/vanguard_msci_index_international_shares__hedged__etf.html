Current Performance

- Performance Overview:

Vanguard MSCI INDEX International Shares (Hedged) ETF (VGAD) has displayed solid performance over the past 12 months, with an annual return of approximately 10% as of October 10, 2024. The ETF's performance has benefited from its exposure to global equities, particularly in developed markets outside of Australia, while the hedging mechanism has mitigated currency risk during a time of fluctuating exchange rates.

- Distribution of Returns:

The ETF's diversified holdings have contributed to its performance, primarily in sectors like technology, healthcare, and consumer discretionary. VGAD's performance has mirrored broader market trends, which have been led by gains in these sectors.

- Volatility:

The ETF has exhibited lower volatility compared to more concentrated equity funds, with a standard deviation around 12%, reflecting its diversified nature and the hedging strategy that reduces sensitivity to Australian dollar fluctuations.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

VGAD has a trailing P/E ratio of 18, which is in line with the average for international equity funds. This suggests a stable valuation level, reflecting confidence in the underlying companies' earnings growth potential.

- P/E Ratio Compared to Global Benchmarks:

Compared to its benchmark index, the MSCI All Country World Index ex Australia, VGAD's P/E ratio is slightly below the benchmark, indicating a potentially attractive entry point for investors seeking exposure to international equities.
