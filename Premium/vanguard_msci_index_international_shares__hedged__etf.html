Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of October 2024, Vanguard MSCI INDEX International Shares (Hedged) ETF (VGAD) has demonstrated a robust performance, with year-to-date returns of approximately 15%. This performance has been underpinned by a strong rebound in international equities, particularly among developed markets, following periods of volatility in 2023. The ETF’s net asset value has seen a consistent upward trend, reflecting the recovery in global economic conditions.

- Profit Margins:

VGAD, being an index fund, does not generate profits in the traditional sense, but it essentially tracks the performance of the MSCI World ex Australia Index (Hedged). The underlying equities have shown improved profit margins as companies adjust to market conditions, with higher margins reported by key sectors like information technology and consumer discretionary.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

This metric is not directly applicable to ETFs; however, the underlying companies within the VGAD have reported collective EPS growth of about 12% year-over-year, particularly benefiting from stronger demand and pricing power in global markets.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The weighted average ROE of the underlying companies in VGAD stands at around 14%, indicating efficient use of equity capital among the diversified international holdings.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for VGAD is approximately 21, which indicates that investors are generally willing to pay a premium for the growth potential of the underlying international equities. This reflects a recovery phase as global markets stabilize.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

VGAD’s P/E ratio is slightly above the international equity ETF average of around 19. This premium is attributed to the stability and quality of the holdings in developed markets that VGAD focuses on, particularly given the robust economic recovery observed in recent months.

Analyst Recommendations

- Consensus Rating:

Analysts maintain a 'Buy' consensus on VGAD, reflecting optimism regarding its exposure to international markets, which may outperform the Australian market in the near future due to global economic recovery.

The average price target for VGAD stands at approximately AUD $24.00 with a range between AUD $22.00 and AUD $26.00. This suggests a potential upside from current levels, underpinned by a generally favorable outlook for international equities.

Summary

The Vanguard MSCI INDEX International Shares (Hedged) ETF (VGAD) is positioned favorably for investors seeking exposure to international equities with hedging advantages. The ETF's year-to-date performance reflects the recovery in global equity markets, particularly driven by robust earnings growth among its constituents. While the P/E ratio indicates a premium valuation, analysts remain broadly optimistic about its prospects, forecasting solid returns ahead. The consistent dividend yield provides attractive income opportunities for investors, emphasizing VGAD’s reliability. Overall, VGAD stands out as a strong contender for those looking to diversify their portfolios internationally, although potential geopolitical risks could pose challenges in the near future.
