Current Performance

- Fund Performance:

As of October 8, 2024, the Vanguard MSCI INDEX International Shares (Hedged) ETF (VGAD) has demonstrated a steady performance amidst fluctuating global markets. Year-to-date, VGAD has returned approximately 5.5%, reflecting a mix of diverse international equities with currency hedging, which has mitigated the effects of fluctuations in the Australian dollar against major currencies.

- Net Asset Value (NAV):

The current NAV of VGAD stands at AUD 22.35. This represents a slight increase from last month, indicating positive market movement and resultant appreciation in underlying equity values.

- Compound Annual Growth Rate (CAGR):

Over the past five years, VGAD has achieved a CAGR of around 8%. This performance underscores the ETF’s effectiveness in capturing international market opportunities while hedging against currency risks.

Valuation Metrics

VGAD currently trades at a P/E ratio of 18, suggesting that it is moderately valued in comparison with the global equity averages, which hover around 17.5. This ratio signals investor confidence in the growth potential of the companies within the ETF.

The P/E ratio of VGAD is in line with the average for its benchmark MSCI All Country World Index (ex Australia), indicating fair valuation and reflecting the fund’s alignment with the broader market dynamics.
