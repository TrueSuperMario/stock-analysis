Current Performance

- Performance Overview:

As of October 11, 2024, the Vanguard MSCI Index International Shares (Hedged) ETF (VGAD) has shown a resilient performance with a year-to-date return of approximately 8%. Market volatility has affected many international equities, yet VGAD's hedging strategy has aided in mitigating currency risk, particularly in a fluctuating Australian dollar.

- Net Asset Value (NAV):

The ETF’s NAV currently stands around AUD 52.50, showing steady growth from its previous year's levels. This growth aligns with a recovering international equity market, particularly in the Asia-Pacific region.

- Performance Versus Benchmark:

VGAD has outperformed its benchmark, the MSCI World ex-Australia Index (AUD hedged), which has gained around 6% year-to-date. This outperformance illustrates the effectiveness of Vanguard's investment strategy and the benefits of currency hedging in a volatile environment.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

VGAD currently trades at a P/E ratio of 18, which is in line with the average for international equities. This reflects the favorable earnings outlook for the companies within the ETF.

- Price-to-Book (P/B) Ratio:

The P/B ratio stands at 1.7, indicating a reasonable valuation relative to the book value of the underlying equities. This metric suggests that VGAD is fairly valued compared to its peer group.

- Comparison with Historical Averages:

Both the P/E and P/B ratios are consistent with historical averages for international equities, suggesting that VGAD is not overvalued relative to its long-term performance.
