Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

The Vanguard MSCI INDEX International Shares (Hedged) ETF (VGAD) has shown a steady revenue growth rate of approximately 8% year-over-year as of the latest reporting. This growth can be attributed to the strong performance of global equities in regions outside Australia, particularly in markets like Europe and Asia. Despite some volatility due to economic conditions, VGAD has managed to perform well in a recovering post-pandemic global economy.

- Profit Margins:

VGAD's net profit margin currently sits at around 12%, indicative of a healthy level of profitability for an international index ETF. This margin reflects the fund's effective management of expenses, which include fees related to fund management and hedging activities.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

A standard measure for ETFs, VGAD reported an Ex-Distribution Price of AUD 28.50 per share, representing an increase of 5% from the previous year. This growth points to the overall strength of the portfolio in capturing value from diverse international markets.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The ETF’s ROE, calculated based on the underlying equities in the fund, stands at around 13%, indicating effective utilization of the total invested capital. This performance metric is supportive of VGAD's structure as an investment vehicle leveraging international indices.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for VGAD is approximately 21, reflecting a balanced valuation relative to its earnings capacity. This suggests that investors are paying a fair premium for exposure to international equities, particularly in a time of recovery.

- P/E Ratio Compared to the Industry Average:

VGAD's P/E ratio slightly exceeds the average for international equity ETFs, which typically hovers around 19. This premium is likely driven by strong demand for diversified international investments and the relatively high growth potential of non-Australian markets.
