Current Performance

- Fund Performance:

As of October 16, 2024, the Vanguard MSCI Index International Shares (Hedged) ETF (VGAD) has shown a year-to-date return of approximately 8%, aligning closely with the overall performance of international developed markets. The fund’s net asset value (NAV) reflects a slight increase over the last quarter, suggesting resilience despite macroeconomic volatility.

- Volatility:

VGAD has a beta of 0.85, indicating that it is less volatile than the market, which is desirable for investors looking for stability in international equities. This low beta is a result of its hedged exposure aimed at reducing currency risk, particularly against fluctuations in the Australian Dollar.

- Fund Size and Growth:

The total assets under management for VGAD stand at approximately AUD 2 billion. This substantial asset base indicates strong investor interest and confidence in the fund’s strategy.

Valuation Metrics

VGAD has a P/E ratio of 16.5, which is slightly below the international average of 17. This assessment underscores its attractive valuation relative to its earnings potential, suggesting that the ETF is reasonably priced in the current market.

- Trailing Returns:

Over the past five years, VGAD has delivered an annualized return of approximately 10%, outpacing some benchmarks, which indicates robust performance amid fluctuating market conditions.
