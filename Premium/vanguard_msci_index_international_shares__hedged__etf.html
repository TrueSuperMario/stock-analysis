Current Performance

Revenue and Earnings Growth:

Vanguard MSCI Index International Shares (Hedged) ETF (VGAD) has shown stable revenue growth, benefiting from a diversified international portfolio. Year-to-date, the ETF has delivered approximately 10% in total returns, driven by strong performance in various sectors, particularly technology and consumer discretionary. Earnings growth reflects positive performance of underlying assets, with many international companies recovering from pandemic-related disruptions.

Profit Margins:

VGAD’s profit margins are indirectly affected by the performance of the underlying international equities it tracks. The ETF maintains a hedged position against currency fluctuations, thus stabilizing its returns. Overall, the fund benefits from favorable profit margins in tech-heavy markets where many of its holdings operate.

Earnings Per Share (EPS):

EPS growth is representative of overall market trends, given VGAD's reliance on the performance of its constituents. The effective EPS growth for underlying companies has averaged about 6% over the last year, reflecting a recovery trend in global markets.

Return on Equity (ROE):

While VGAD itself does not have a direct ROE measure, it derives this information from the performance of the companies in its portfolio, primarily those listed in developed markets. The average ROE for top holdings is above 15%, indicating efficient profit generation relative to equity.

Valuation Metrics

Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

VGAD currently has a trailing P/E ratio of 18, suggesting that investors are willing to pay a premium for international exposure, particularly as economies recover post-pandemic. This valuation reflects confidence in growth from foreign markets.

P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

Compared to its benchmarks, VGAD’s P/E is in line with the broader international equity market, slightly above the average P/E ratio of 16 for developed international markets. This indicates a balanced valuation amidst expectations of moderate growth.

Analysts generally hold a 'Buy' consensus on VGAD due to its diversified exposure and hedged position, which is attractive for investors seeking international equity exposure without currency risk. Analysts emphasize the ETF’s ability to capture growth from international markets.
