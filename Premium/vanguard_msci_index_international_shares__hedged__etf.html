Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of October 15, 2024, Vanguard MSCI INDEX International Shares (Hedged) ETF (VGAD) has shown robust performance driven by strong international equity markets. The ETF has experienced a year-to-date return of approximately 12%, reflecting a recovery post-pandemic and increased investor confidence in global markets. The underlying asset performance across developed international equities has contributed to consistent returns.

- Profit Margins:

VGAD's expense ratio currently stands at 0.18%, which remains competitive within the ETF space. Though the ETF does not generate direct profits or losses akin to a traditional stock, its operational efficiency is an essential factor for investors as it impacts net returns.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

Given the nature of ETFs, EPS is not directly applicable. However, VGAD’s underlying assets have generated healthy earnings growth, averaging around 10% year-over-year, reflecting solid corporate earnings from its constituents in developed international markets.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

As an index fund, VGAD does not report ROE in the traditional sense; however, the return on investments in its underlying indices reflects the overall health of the international equity markets it tracks, showing signs of strengthening investment returns.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

VGAD has a trailing P/E ratio of approximately 15.5, which is favorable compared to the global equity market average of around 18. This indicates that VGAD’s holdings are currently trading at a more attractive valuation relative to their earnings potential.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

While VGAD’s P/E ratio is slightly below the developed market average, this is indicative of a valuation that calls for attention, particularly as international markets show potential for growth as they recover from recent downturns.
