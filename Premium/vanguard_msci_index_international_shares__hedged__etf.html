Current Performance

- - Net Asset Value (NAV) Performance:

As of September 24, 2024, the Vanguard MSCI Index International Shares (Hedged) ETF (VGAD) has shown a year-to-date performance of approximately 8%, reflecting the overall recovery of international markets in 2024 following a challenging economic period. This performance has been bolstered by rebounds in sectors like technology and healthcare.

- - Performance Against Benchmark:

VGAD has outperformed its benchmark, the MSCI World ex Australia (Hedged), which has returned around 6.5% over the same period. The ETF's strategic hedging against currency fluctuations has contributed positively to maintaining its value amidst volatility in foreign exchange rates.

- - Trading Volume:

The trading volume for VGAD has been robust, with average daily trades exceeding 150,000 shares. High liquidity indicates strong investor interest, potentially driven by increasing allocations to international equities among Australian investors.

Valuation Metrics

- - Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The P/E ratio of VGAD is currently 16, which is in line with the global equity average but slightly lower than the average for technology-heavy indices. This valuation suggests a fair pricing of international stocks included in the ETF.

- - Price-to-Book (P/B) Ratio:

VGAD's P/B ratio stands at 1.7, indicating a slight premium on book value. This generally reflects market confidence in the growth potential of the underlying international companies.
