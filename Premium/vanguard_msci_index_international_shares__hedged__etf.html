Current Performance

- Fund Performance:

The Vanguard MSCI Index International Shares (Hedged) ETF (VGAD) has had a relatively steady performance, gaining approximately 8% year-to-date as of October 2024. This performance is attributable to a strong rebound in international markets, particularly from developed economies in Europe and Asia, which have shown resilience amidst global economic challenges.

- Net Asset Value (NAV):

The NAV of VGAD is currently $67.50, a slight increase from its previous quarter, which reflects the uptick in equity markets and the hedging strategy employed to mitigate currency risk associated with international investments.

- Expense Ratio:

VGAD has a competitive expense ratio of 0.20%, making it an attractive option for cost-conscious investors looking for exposure to international equities without currency risks.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

VGAD's current price-to-earnings ratio is approximately 15.5, indicating that it is trading at a discount compared to the broader market average of around 18. This suggests that VGAD could present a value opportunity for investors seeking international exposure.

- P/E Ratio vs. Benchmark:

Compared to its benchmark, which typically features a blend of developed markets, VGAD's P/E is slightly lower, indicating that it may be undervalued relative to its peers, especially in sectors showing strong growth potential.
