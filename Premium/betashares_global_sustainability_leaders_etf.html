Current Performance

- - Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of October 2024, the Betashares Global Sustainability Leaders ETF (ETHI) has experienced a revenue increase of approximately 8% year-on-year, fueled by heightened global interest in sustainability and environmental, social, and governance (ESG) investing. This increase reflects the growing market share of the underlying companies in ETHI's portfolio, which are predominantly leaders in sustainability.

- - Profit Margins:

The ETF’s profit margins have remained stable, with an average net margin of around 15%. This is commendable considering the varied performance among sectors represented in the fund, suggesting the underlying companies are effectively managing their costs despite macroeconomic challenges.

- - Earnings Per Share (EPS):

While ETFs do not report EPS in the same manner as individual stocks, the earnings derived from the constituent companies indicate a solid average increase of roughly 6% over the past year.

- - Return on Equity (ROE):

The underlying companies in the ETF have an average ROE of 14%, which is robust for a sustainability-focused investment vehicle, indicating effective use of shareholder capital to generate profits.

Valuation Metrics

- - Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for ETHI stands at 24, which indicates that the fund is valued at a premium compared to traditional market indices. This premium is justified by the emphasis on sustainable companies anticipated to benefit from the shift towards a greener economy.

- - P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

The ETF’s P/E ratio is notably higher than the average P/E ratio of the broader market, which is around 20. This reflects a strong market confidence in the growth potential of the sustainability sector.
