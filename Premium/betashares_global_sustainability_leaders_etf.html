Current Performance

- Fund Performance:

As of October 29, 2024, the Betashares Global Sustainability Leaders ETF (ETHI) has delivered a total return of approximately 15% year-to-date, benefiting from a rebound in global equities focused on sustainability. This is a notable recovery compared to a slight decline of around 2% in the previous year amidst market volatility.

- Portfolio Allocation:

ETHI currently holds over 100 companies, primarily from sectors such as technology, consumer discretionary, and renewable energy. The largest allocations include major firms like Tesla, Microsoft, and NextEra Energy, highlighting a focus on innovations that promote sustainability.

- Expense Ratio:

The ETF carries an expense ratio of 0.59%, which is competitive compared to similar sustainable funds and reflects effective management in a rapidly evolving market.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for ETHI stands at 24, indicating a premium valuation compared to the broader market P/E of approximately 18. This reflects the high market expectations for the growth potential of sustainable companies.

- Price-to-Book (P/B) Ratio:

The P/B ratio is at 3.2, again suggesting that investors are willing to pay a premium for sustainability-focused investments due to their perceived long-term growth potential.

Summary

The Betashares Global Sustainability Leaders ETF (ETHI) is performing well in a conducive market environment characterized by a strong trend towards sustainable investing. Boasting a healthy year-to-date return and solid management practices, ETHI is well-positioned in terms of performance metrics, analyst recommendations, and market sentiment. Though the dividend yield is modest, the ETF remains an attractive option for investors focused on capital appreciation through sustainable companies.
