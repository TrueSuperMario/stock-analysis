Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

The Betashares Global Sustainability Leaders ETF (ETHI) has exhibited a robust performance over the past year, with an approximate 12% increase in revenue driven largely by a rising interest in sustainable investments. Earnings growth, however, has been modest at around 5%, reflecting the ETF's diversified holdings across various sectors which may not all be experiencing explosive growth simultaneously.

- Profit Margins:

As an ETF, ETHI does not report traditional profit margins like a corporation. However, the fund boasts a relatively low expense ratio of 0.59%, allowing more of its revenue from its investments to be funneled back to investors. This profitability strategy mirrors the growing trend toward cost-efficient passive fund management.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

EPS for ETHI is not typically applicable as it is an ETF, but tracking the net asset value (NAV) shows that it has risen about 8% on a per-share basis compared to the same period last year. This increase reflects positive price appreciation among its underlying securities.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

ROE isn't directly applicable for ETHI as a fund structure. However, the returns generated from its underlying equity holdings have averaged about 11% over the past year, a solid figure indicative of the performance of the companies included within the ETF.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for ETHI is 23, which is slightly higher than traditional broad market averages. This reflects the premium investors are willing to pay for exposure to sustainability-focused companies, which are viewed as having higher growth prospects.

The P/E ratio of ETHI is above that of traditional indices like the S&P 500 (approx. 19).
