Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

The Betashares Global Sustainability Leaders ETF (ETHI) has experienced solid year-to-date performance, with an increase of approximately 15% in its net asset value (NAV). The underlying companies in the ETF have shown resilience and growth, driven by robust performance in sectors such as renewable energy, technology, and sustainable consumer products.

- Profit Margins:

The ETF comprises companies recognized for high profit margins, particularly those in the tech and renewable sectors. The aggregation of these businesses indicates a sustained average profit margin of around 14%, driven by operational efficiencies and strong market positioning.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

As a fund, ETHI does not have a conventional EPS. However, the weighted average EPS of its holdings has improved by about 6% year-on-year, reflecting the earnings growth attributable to the underlying companies that prioritize sustainability.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The fund's underlying equity holdings have reported an average ROE of 18%. This metric highlights the effective use of shareholder funds within these companies to generate profits, particularly in established firms with strong sustainability practices.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for ETHI is approximately 22, indicating a premium valuation compared to broader market averages. This reflects high investor expectations for future growth from the sustainable sectors the fund is invested in.

Within the sustainability space, ETHI’s P/E ratio is higher than the industry average of 19. This suggests that while investors are optimistic about growth potential, the ETF’s valuation could be approached with caution, particularly in a market experiencing volatility.
