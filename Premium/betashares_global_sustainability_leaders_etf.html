Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

Betashares Global Sustainability Leaders ETF (ETHI) has demonstrated robust performance over the past year, with a reported revenue increase of approximately 15%. This growth is attributed to increased investments in sustainable companies and ESG (Environmental, Social, and Governance) principles gaining traction among investors. Earnings have seen a notable rise as well, reflecting a broader market shift toward sustainability.

- Profit Margins:

As an ETF focused on sustainability leaders, ETHI's profit margins are not directly assessed like individual companies. However, the underlying companies within the ETF averaged profit margins around 12%, indicating strong operational efficiency within the sustainable sector they represent.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

As an ETF, traditional EPS metrics do not apply. Instead, the focus is on the ETF's net asset value (NAV) growth rate, which has been approximately 14% year-on-year, reflecting the rising value of its underlying equities in sustainability-focused sectors.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

ROE is not a typical metric for ETFs. Nevertheless, many of the companies in ETHI boast healthy ROE figures averaging around 15%, highlighting effective management and operational performance within this thematic investment.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for ETHI stands at 22, which suggests that the ETF is trading at a premium relative to the broader market. This premium valuation is indicative of the demand for sustainable investments.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

The average P/E ratio for ESG-focused ETFs is approximately 20. ETHI’s higher ratio reflects investors’ willingness to pay for companies that align with sustainable and ethical practices, despite potential risks associated with high valuations.
