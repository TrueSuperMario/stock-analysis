Current Performance

- - Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of September 2024, the Betashares Global Sustainability Leaders ETF (ETHI) has seen an annual return of approximately 14%, driven by increased investor interest in sustainable investments and strong performance from its underlying holdings in global companies focused on sustainability. Earnings growth for the fund is reflective of the positive performance of its core holdings, which are largely in tech, healthcare, and renewable energy sectors.

- - Profit Margins:

The ETF does not generate its own profit margins, as it mainly invests in stocks of companies with varying margins. However, many of its holdings feature high gross profit margins due to their positions in growth industries, contributing to the ETF's overall performance.

- - Earnings Per Share (EPS):

While ETHI does not have a conventional EPS like individual stocks, the underlying holdings contribute to perceived value growth. The average earnings growth of top holdings has averaged around 15% year-on-year, indicating healthy business performance.

- - Return on Equity (ROE):

The ETF's underlying holdings exhibit a broad range of ROEs, averaging around 18% across leading companies involved in sustainable practices. This strong ROE reflects effective utilization of equity to fuel growth in profitable sectors.

Valuation Metrics

- - Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

ETHI carries a weighted average P/E ratio of approximately 25, indicating a premium valuation compared to the broader market. This reflects the elevated expectations for future earnings from its sustainably-focused holdings.

- - P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

The average P/E ratio for sustainability-themed ETFs tends to hover around 20-22. Consequently, ETHI's higher P/E suggests strong market confidence in the future growth prospects associated with sustainability investing.

Analyst Recommendations

- - Consensus Rating:

Analysts generally regard ETHI positively with a 'Buy' consensus. The ETF is seen as a pioneering fund in sustainable investments, attracting interest due to the growing trend towards environmental, social, and governance (ESG) considerations.

- - Price Targets:

Average analyst price targets suggest upside potential of around 10-15% from current levels, based on anticipated continued growth in sustainable-focused sectors, with some expectations of volatility linked to market conditions.

Due to the nature of ETFs, there are generally no significant insider transactions in the traditional sense, as they are managed funds. However, institutional investor interest has been notable, with increasing allocations to ETHI as sustainable investing gains traction.

- - Overall Sentiment:

The sentiment surrounding ETHI remains positive among institutional investors, who see it as a long-term play consistent with global sustainability goals.

ETHI has a growing history of dividend payments since its inception, indicative of the sustainability and profitability of its underlying assets.

The global demand for sustainable investment opportunities is robust, with increasing regulations pushing companies towards sustainable practices. This trend is reflected in ETHI’s portfolio, which is heavily weighted towards sectors such as technology, healthcare, and renewable energy.

Summary

The Betashares Global Sustainability Leaders ETF (ETHI) is successfully positioned within the growing landscape of sustainable investing, featuring robust performance metrics such as strong earnings growth from underlying holdings and a healthy dividend yield. With a high P/E ratio reflecting confidence in its future growth, and prevailing positive sentiment from both analysts and investors, ETHI stands as an attractive option for those committed to sustainability. While it faces potential risks associated with market volatility and economic fluctuations, the ETF’s strong performance and commitment to sustainability provide a compelling case for long-term investment. Overall, ETHI represents a solid choice for investors looking to align their portfolios with sustainable practices while pursuing capital appreciation.
