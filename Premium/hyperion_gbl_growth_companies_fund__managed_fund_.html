Current Performance

- - Revenue and Earnings Growth:

The Hyperion GBL Growth Companies Fund has demonstrated robust performance, with a year-to-date return of approximately 18%. This performance is attributed to a diversified portfolio that captures growth from both established and emerging companies in sectors such as technology, healthcare, and consumer goods. Earnings growth for the underlying investments within the fund has averaged around 15%, benefiting from strong market conditions in key growth industries.

- - Profit Margins:

The fund's investments have averaged profit margins of around 20%, reflecting solid operational efficiency among its holdings. Many portfolio companies have reported improved margins through cost management and pricing power, particularly in the technology and healthcare sectors.

- - Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The weighted average EPS for the companies held within the fund has increased by approximately 12% over the past year, showcasing the success of the fund’s stock-picking strategy. This growth is indicative of the resilience and operational strength of its growth-focused portfolio.

- - Return on Equity (ROE):

The ROE for the portfolio companies averages around 18%, signifying strong performance in generating profits from shareholder equity. This level of ROE is considered healthy and indicative of effective management in capital utilization.

Valuation Metrics

- - Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The fund is currently trading at a P/E ratio of 23, which suggests a premium valuation relative to the broader market. This premium reflects the fund's focus on high-growth companies that are expected to generate superior returns over the long term.

- - P/E Ratio Compared to the Industry Average:

The P/E ratio of 23 is above the growth sector average of 20, highlighting investor confidence in the fund's strategy and the future growth potential of its holdings.
