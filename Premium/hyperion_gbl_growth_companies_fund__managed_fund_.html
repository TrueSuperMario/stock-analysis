Current Performance

- - Revenue and Earnings Growth:

The Hyperion GBL Growth Companies Fund (HYGG) has shown robust performance, with a year-to-date return of approximately 18%. The fund’s strategy, which focuses on high-quality growth companies, has benefited from strong fundamentals in many of its top holdings. Earnings growth for the fund’s underlying investments has averaged around 15% over the past year, driven by a favorable economic recovery and increased consumer spending in critical sectors.

- - Profit Margins:

The fund’s portfolio companies maintain healthy profit margins, averaging around 15%. This reflects the quality of the investments, as many are leaders in their respective industries with significant pricing power and operational efficiencies.

- - Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The weighted average EPS growth for companies within the fund stands at 12%, indicating solid earnings performance compared to broader indices. This is a positive sign for potential capital appreciation and overall fund performance.

- - Return on Equity (ROE):

The fund's holdings boast an average ROE of 18%. This figure highlights the effective use of shareholders' equity to generate profits, reinforcing the fund’s focus on quality and growth.

Valuation Metrics

- - Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The fund carries an aggregated P/E ratio of 25, suggesting a premium valuation compared to the market average of around 20. This indicates high investor confidence in growth prospects, albeit at the risk of future earnings disappointment if growth fails to materialize.

- - P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

The P/E ratio of HYGG is above the growth fund average, which typically hovers around 22. This premium valuation reflects the fund's investment in high-growth companies, which may trade at higher valuations due to expected future growth.
