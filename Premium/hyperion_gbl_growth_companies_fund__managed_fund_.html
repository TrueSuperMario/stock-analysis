The Hyperion GBL Growth Companies Fund (HYGG) has delivered a commendable performance with a year-to-date return of approximately 12%, reflecting strong underlying portfolio performance. The fund achieved a quarterly earnings growth of 8%, spearheaded by several technology and healthcare holdings that benefitted from ongoing market trends and demand growth.

The fund’s average profit margin from its top holdings is around 15%, indicating a healthy capability to convert revenues into profits. This is particularly relevant in a challenging economic landscape, showcasing operational efficiency and effective cost management within its investment approach.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The EPS for the fund sits at $1.20, which translates to a 5% increase compared to the previous year. This growth reflects performance improvements across various sectors represented in the portfolio, notably in tech and consumer discretionary.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The fund reports an ROE of 10%, indicating the calibre of the underlying companies’ ability to generate profits relative to shareholder equity. This performance metric is competitive within the growth fund category, showcasing the ability to leverage capital effectively.
