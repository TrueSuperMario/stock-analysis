Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

The Vanguard Australian Shares High Yield ETF (VHY) has demonstrated a solid revenue growth trajectory over the past year, facilitated by its focus on high-yielding stocks across various sectors. As of October 2024, it reported a year-on-year revenue increase of approximately 6%. However, earnings growth has been slightly lower at around 4% due to increased operational costs affecting some portfolio companies.

- Profit Margins:

VHY’s underlying portfolio companies display an average net profit margin of about 12%, indicative of robust profitability despite challenges such as rising input costs and inflationary pressures. This average reflects the ETF's focus on established companies known for their strong cash flow generation.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The trailing twelve-month (TTM) EPS for VHY stands at approximately $1.88, which marks a 3% increase compared to the previous year, showcasing its resilience in maintaining earnings even in a varied market landscape.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The portfolio exhibits an average ROE of around 14%, suggesting effective utilization of equity by the companies within the ETF. This level is favorable and indicates that VHY's holdings are generating substantial returns for shareholders.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for VHY is around 17, which positions the ETF as slightly undervalued relative to its earnings potential, especially considering its focus on high-yield assets.

- P/E Ratio Compared to Industry Average:

Compared to the broader Australian equities market, where the average P/E hovers around 19, VHY’s lower valuation may present an intriguing opportunity for investors seeking value within high-yield stocks.
