Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

The Vanguard Australian Shares High Yield ETF (VHY) has shown robust performance over the past year, with a revenue growth of approximately 8%, fueled by strong distributions from its underlying holdings. However, earnings growth for the ETF, reflective of its yield-driven structure, has remained more stable, showing a modest increase of 4% as market conditions stabilized.

- Profit Margins:

VHY's profit margins are effectively defined by the performance of its underlying equities; the net yield from the ETF currently stands at around 6%. This exceeds several peers, showcasing that VHY effectively captures high-yield opportunities in the Australian market.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

EPS is not directly applicable as VHY is an ETF, but the average distribution per unit reflects approximately $0.90 over the last twelve months, providing a consistent income stream for investors.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

For ETFs like VHY, ROE isn’t typically calculated; however, the effective allocation among high-yield stocks signifies a good utilization of the capital invested within the portfolio, providing high returns through dividends distributed to shareholders.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio of VHY is approximately 15, which indicates a reasonable valuation based on its earnings potential. This metric aligns well with the Australian market's average P/E of 14.5, suggesting that VHY is priced fairly relative to its peers.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

Compared to the broader ASX 200, VHY's P/E ratio is slightly above average, reflecting the premium investors are willing to pay for high-yield equities, which are particularly appealing during low interest rate environments.

The ETF has a high payout ratio, approximately 90%, implying that it effectively returns most of its earnings to investors through dividends, which is typical for high-yield funds.

- Dividend History:

VHY has a history of consistent dividend payments, with annual increases reflecting the underlying companies' solid performance and financial health, which enhances its appeal to long-term investors.
