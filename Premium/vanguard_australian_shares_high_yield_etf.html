- - Performance Metrics:

VHY has delivered a one-year total return of approximately 14%, driven primarily by capital appreciation and steady dividend distributions. The ETF’s strong exposure to high-yielding Australian equities has enabled it to outperform many of its peers in both growth and income scenarios.

The ETF has a total net asset value of about AUD 2 billion, making it a robust player in the Australian equity market.

Valuation Metrics

- - Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

VHY has a current P/E ratio of 15, indicating that the ETF is reasonably valued when compared to the broader market average of around 17. This suggests that investors are paying approximately AUD 15 for every AUD 1 of earnings, reflecting a common valuation metric for dividend-oriented funds.

- - Dividend Yield vs. Industry Average:

The current dividend yield for VHY is approximately 5.5%, which is above the average yield of 4% for the Australian market. This premium reflects the ETF's focus on high-yield dividend stocks, appealing to income-focused investors.
