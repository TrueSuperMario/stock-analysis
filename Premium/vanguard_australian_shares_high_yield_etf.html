Current Performance

- - Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of September 30, 2024, the Vanguard Australian Shares High Yield ETF (VHY) has demonstrated stable performance, with an annual revenue growth of approximately 6%. Earnings have reflected a consistent trajectory, increasing by around 5%, driven largely by strong performances in component stocks from the financial and materials sectors.

- - Profit Margins:

VHY’s overall profit margin stands at roughly 9%, which is solid for an ETF primarily focused on high-yield Australian equities. This figure indicates efficient management of the underlying investments, although margin pressures from operational costs in some sectors could impact returns.

- - Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The current EPS for the ETF has improved to $3.45, reflecting a year-on-year increase of 4%. This increase is attributed to the strong dividend performance of the underlying holdings, along with capital gains realized over the past year.

- - Return on Equity (ROE):

ROE for VHY is approximately 10%, indicating a good utilization of equity in generating profits. This metric signifies that the underlying companies within the ETF are effectively converting equity investments into earnings.

Valuation Metrics

- - Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for VHY is 15.5, which is slightly above the broader ASX 200 average of around 14. This suggests the ETF is valued at a premium, likely due to its focus on high-yielding stocks that attract yield-seeking investors.

- - Dividend Yield:

VHY currently offers investors a dividend yield of 5.8%, which is attractive compared to other domestic equity funds. This yield reflects the ETF's focus on high-dividend-paying stocks within the Australian market.
