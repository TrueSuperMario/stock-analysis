Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of March 17, 2025, Global X Physical Gold (GOLD) has experienced significant interest amid fluctuating gold prices, largely driven by economic uncertainty and inflation concerns. The ETF has seen a year-on-year revenue increase of approximately 15%, directly correlating with a rise in gold prices over the same period, which have surged due to geopolitical tensions and a weakening dollar.

- Profit Margins:

GOLD's profit margins are closely tied to the performance of the underlying gold assets. The ETF’s expense ratio is relatively low at 0.60%, allowing it to maintain solid margins even amid market volatility.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The fund does not distribute earnings as traditional companies do, but the net asset value (NAV) per share is currently at $16.50, representing a 10% increase from the previous year, reflecting the appreciation in gold prices.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

Since GOLD operates as an ETF that primarily tracks the physical gold market, ROE as traditionally measured for companies is not applicable. However, the fund's performance can be measured via its tracking error and overall market performance against benchmarks for gold.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Assets (P/A) Ratio:

The current P/A ratio for GOLD stands at approximately 1.15, suggesting that the ETF is trading at a slight premium relative to its underlying gold holdings but reflects strong market confidence in gold as a safe-haven asset.

- NAV versus Market Price:

The significant divergence between NAV and market price has led to shares trading at a slight premium, indicating investor demand despite the higher costs of physical gold holdings.

The average analyst price target for GOLD is around $17.00, suggesting moderate upside potential from current levels, driven largely by projected increases in gold prices.

Dividend Analysis

- Dividend Yield:

Global X Physical Gold does not pay dividends because it is designed to track physical gold prices rather than generate income. However, the appreciation in NAV serves as the primary return for investors.

- Dividend History:

There are no dividends paid by GOLD, which is a common characteristic of gold ETFs. Investors are typically compensated through price appreciation of the underlying asset rather than income distributions.
