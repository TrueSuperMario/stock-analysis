Current Performance

- - Net Asset Value (NAV) Growth:

As of October 2, 2024, Global X Physical Gold ETF (GOLD) has seen a year-to-date increase in its net asset value, reflecting a rise in gold prices driven by geopolitical tensions and economic uncertainties. The fund is currently experiencing a 12% growth in NAV compared to the beginning of the year.

- - Price Performance:

GOLD is trading at approximately $20.50 per share, showing a moderate increase of 8% over the last quarter, and outperforming many equity markets amid recent volatility.

- - Assets Under Management (AUM):

The AUM has increased to $1.5 billion, indicating strong investor interest in gold as a safe-haven asset amid inflationary pressures and market fluctuations.

Valuation Metrics

- - Expense Ratio:

GOLD has an expense ratio of 0.18%, which is competitive for ETFs in the commodities space. This low cost structure makes it an attractive option for investors looking for exposure to physical gold.

- - Price-to-NAV Ratio:

The current price-to-NAV ratio is around 1.03, suggesting that the ETF trades very close to its underlying asset value, indicating no significant premium or discount to the market value of gold.

Dividend Analysis

- - Dividend Yield:

While GOLD does not provide traditional dividends, it effectively offers returns through capital appreciation in line with gold price movements. The indirect yield comes from holding physical gold, which can appreciate during economic downturns.

Shareholders can redeem their shares for physical gold, which provides an alternative form of return during periods of heightened gold prices.
