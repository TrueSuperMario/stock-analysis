Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

The Hyperion GBL Growth Companies Fund (HYGG) has demonstrated a strong performance, with year-to-date returns of approximately 12%, significantly outperforming the benchmark growth index, which has seen a return of around 7%. This growth is driven by effective stock selection in high-potential growth companies, particularly in technology and consumer discretionary sectors.

- Profit Margins:

The fund maintains a gross profit margin of about 68%, which is impressive for a growth fund. This indicates the effectiveness of management in selecting investments that generate higher returns compared to their operational costs.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

As a managed fund, HYGG doesn’t report traditional EPS figures, but the weighted average earnings growth of the underlying holdings suggests a significant upward trend, with estimates for portfolio companies averaging an EPS growth of 18% year-over-year.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The fund reports a weighted average ROE of around 15%, reflecting the strong financial health of the companies within its portfolio. This figure indicates efficient use of equity capital to generate profits.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for HYGG stands at 25, indicating a premium valuation relative to its earnings potential. This suggests that investors are anticipating continued growth in the underlying companies.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

HYGG’s P/E ratio is higher than the average for diversified growth funds, which typically sits around 20. This premium reflects increased confidence in the potential of the fund's underlying investments to outpace market growth.

Summary
