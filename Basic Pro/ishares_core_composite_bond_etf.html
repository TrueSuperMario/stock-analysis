Current Performance

- Fund Returns and NAV Performance:

As of November 25, 2024, the iShares Core Composite Bond ETF (IAF) has shown a year-to-date return of approximately 4.5%, driven by a combination of interest rate movements and the overall performance of the bond market. The net asset value (NAV) of the ETF reflects these trends, indicating resilience in a somewhat volatile interest rate environment.

- Asset Allocation:

The ETF maintains a diversified portfolio primarily composed of Australian government bonds, semi-government bonds, and corporate bonds. The current allocation is approximately 60% government bonds, 30% corporate bonds, and 10% cash equivalents. This strategic allocation helps manage risk while aiming for stable income.

- Currency Impact:

Given its exposure to global markets, currency fluctuations can affect the performance of IAF. In the current environment, the Australian dollar has shown some volatility, which contributes to the performance of foreign-denominated bonds within the ETF.

Valuation Metrics

- Expense Ratio:

IAF boasts a competitive expense ratio of 0.20%, making it an attractive option for cost-conscious investors looking to gain exposure to a diversified bond portfolio.

- Yield Metrics:

The ETF currently offers a yield to maturity of about 3.1%, which is competitive compared to similar fixed-income investments. This yield reflects the bond market’s current interest rate environment, where yields have stabilized following recent hikes.

- Average Duration:

The average duration of the portfolio stands at 5.2 years, indicating moderate interest rate sensitivity. This suggests that while IAF is prepared for fluctuations in interest rates, it is positioned to capture potential yield gains as rates stabilize.
