Earnings growth has followed a similar trend, with an increase of around 5% year-on-year, benefitting from strong demand for quality international equities and a focus on companies with stable earnings and return on equity.

- Profit Margins:

The ETF is focused on high-quality international stocks, which has contributed to a net profit margin of about 15%. This margin is solid, particularly for a fund that prioritizes quality and stability among its international holdings.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

QUAL currently exhibits an EPS of $3.80, marking a 4.5% increase compared to the previous year. This growth reflects the ETF's successful investment strategy and its ability to select quality companies that are resilient in various market conditions.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The underlying companies in QUAL maintain a robust average ROE of around 18%, indicating a strong capability to generate profits from shareholder equity. This highlights the fund’s investment in quality companies with effective management practices.

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

As of January 20, 2025, the P/E ratio for QUAL is approximately 20, reflecting a generally favorable valuation relative to its earnings. This indicates that investors are willing to pay a premium for the stability and growth potentials provided by high-quality international equities.

- P/E Ratio Compared to Industry Average:

QUAL's P/E ratio is slightly above the average for international equity funds, which stands at around 18. This premium valuation suggests that the market recognizes the value of quality stocks during uncertain times and is placing confidence in their long-term growth potential.
