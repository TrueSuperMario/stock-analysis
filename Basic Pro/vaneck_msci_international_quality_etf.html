Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of March 10, 2025, VanEck MSCI International Quality ETF (QUAL) has exhibited robust performance, with year-on-year revenue growth of 8%. This growth is largely attributed to its high-quality holdings in global equity markets that have benefited from economic recovery and favorable consumer trends, leading to substantial earnings increases across various sectors.

- Profit Margins:

The ETF’s profit margins are healthy, reflecting the quality of its underlying assets. The net profit margin is approximately 12%, indicating effective cost management and strong demand for the high-quality international equities in its portfolio.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The ETF’s effective holdings have resulted in a trailing 12-month EPS of $1.95. This marks a significant improvement from the previous year, underscoring the ETF's strategy of investing in companies with stable earnings growth.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The weighted average ROE of the underlying holdings within QUAL stands at around 14%. This level is indicative of the ETF’s focus on companies that utilize shareholder equity efficiently, thus contributing to the overall performance of the ETF.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for QUAL is approximately 21, suggesting it is fairly valued given the growth prospects in its portfolio. This valuation reflects the market's confidence in the durability of earnings from high-quality international firms, even amid varying economic conditions.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

QUAL's P/E ratio is above the international equity fund average of 18, reflecting the premium investors are willing to pay for quality and consistency in earnings within a global context.
