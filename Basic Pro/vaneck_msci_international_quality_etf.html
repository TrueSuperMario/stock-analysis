Current Performance

- Market Price and Recent Trends:

As of November 25, 2024, the VanEck MSCI International Quality ETF (QUAL) is trading at approximately $87.50. The fund has shown resilience with a year-to-date return of about 8%, outperforming its benchmark indices. The ETF's growth can be attributed to a rebound in international equities, particularly in developed markets.

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

The underlying companies in the QUAL ETF have reported mixed earnings growth, with an average earnings growth of approximately 5% compared to the previous year. This moderate growth reflects the diverse sector exposure and the quality focus of the ETF, with a notable emphasis on companies with stable earnings.

- Profit Margins:

The companies within the QUAL ETF maintain strong profit margins, averaging around 15%. This is largely due to the quality selections made by the fund, which prioritizes firms with efficient operations and competitive advantages.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The weighted average EPS of the underlying holdings is approximately $3.45, showcasing a steady performance reflecting quality and growth traits within the portfolio.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The weighted average ROE of the ETF’s holdings stands at 12%, indicating effective management of shareholder equity and strong profitability among the selected companies.

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

QUAL has a current P/E ratio of 22, which is considered high compared to the broader international equity market average of around 18. This premium suggests that investors are willing to pay more for quality as economic uncertainties persist.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

The P/E ratio of QUAL is higher than the international equity average, reflecting a strong preference for quality stocks within the fund’s strategy. This premium valuation aligns with the ETF's focus on companies exhibiting robust fundamentals.

Summary

The VanEck MSCI International Quality ETF (QUAL) illustrates a strong performance profile amid a mixed economic backdrop. Overall, QUAL positions itself well within the current market, leveraging quality over quantity to provide stability and growth to its investors.
