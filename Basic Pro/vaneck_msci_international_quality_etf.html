Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of November 2024, the VanEck MSCI International Quality ETF (QUAL) has shown robust performance with a revenue growth rate of approximately 8% year over year. This growth is attributed to the ETF's focus on high-quality international companies that exhibit strong fundamentals and growth potential despite a challenging global economic environment. Earnings growth has also been solid, with a reported increase of about 7%, reflecting efficiency in operations and strategic investments in various sectors.

- Profit Margins:

The ETF's underlying holdings maintain an average net profit margin of around 12%. This margin is indicative of the quality and efficiency of the companies in the portfolio, showcasing their ability to manage costs effectively while generating substantial profits.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The current EPS for the ETF's top holdings averages $2.15, which represents a 5% increase versus the prior year. This steady growth in EPS indicates that the companies included in the ETF are effectively leveraging their competitive advantages to drive profitability.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The average ROE for the underlying companies stands at 15%, signifying robust management performance in utilizing shareholders' equity to generate profits. This metric is particularly strong compared to the benchmark average for international equities.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for QUAL is 22, suggesting that the ETF is moderately valued in relation to its earnings potential. This valuation reflects both the high-quality nature of the holdings and investor confidence in their growth prospects.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

The 22 P/E ratio is slightly above the global equity average of 20 and indicates a premium valuation. This premium is justified by the ETF's focus on companies with sustainable growth rates and strong financial health.

Analyst Recommendations

The ETF has a strong history of dividend increases, having raised its distribution annually for the past five years. This trend demonstrates the commitment of its underlying companies to returning value to shareholders.
