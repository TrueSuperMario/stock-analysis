Current Performance

- Net Asset Value (NAV) Performance:

As of October 21, 2024, the VanEck MSCI International Quality ETF (QUAL) has seen a year-to-date (YTD) performance of approximately 10%. This is reflective of a broader recovery in international equities, particularly in developed markets, where the ETF is concentrated.

- Performance vs. Benchmark:

QUAL has outperformed its benchmark, the MSCI World ex USA Index, which has returned around 8% YTD. This outperformance is primarily driven by an emphasis on high-quality companies with strong fundamentals.

- Total Return:

The total return over the last 12 months stands at about 12%, closely aligned with improving sentiment towards international equities amidst stabilizing geopolitical risks and a more favorable economic outlook.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for QUAL is approximately 21. This indicates a premium valuation compared to the broader international market average, which typically hovers around 18-19 for similar quality-focused ETFs.

- Price-to-Book (P/B) Ratio:

QUAL's P/B ratio stands at 3.2, reflecting the higher-quality stocks it holds. This is slightly above the average for its peer group, highlighting the market's willingness to pay more for quality growth.

- Forward P/E Ratio:

The forward P/E ratio is estimated at 19, suggesting that analysts expect earnings growth to catch up with current prices, which supports a positive long-term outlook.
