Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

The Dimensional Australian Core Equity Trust - Active ETF (DACE) has reported a revenue growth rate of approximately 7% year-on-year. This growth is driven by a strong performance in the Australian equities market, particularly among large-cap companies that have benefited from post-pandemic economic recovery and increased consumer spending. Earnings growth has also been in the positive territory at around 5%, showcasing robust operational efficiency amid rising inflationary pressures.

- Profit Margins:

DACE currently maintains a net profit margin of 8%, reflective of its well-managed portfolio and effective stock selection strategy. This margin shows resilience despite the challenging market conditions and validates Dimensional's active management approach, which focuses on identifying undervalued companies within the Australian market.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The ETF’s EPS stands at AUD 1.10, representing a 4% increase compared to the same period last year. This growth is attributed to the Trust’s strategic allocation and diversification across various sectors, enhancing its overall performance.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

DACE boasts a ROE of 10%, indicating that the fund efficiently utilizes its capital to generate profits. This is a solid performance indicator, especially for an equity ETF focused on core stocks within the Australian market.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for DACE is approximately 18, showing a modest valuation concerning its earnings potential in the context of the Australian equity market. This suggests that the ETF is reasonably priced, reflecting a cautious optimism among investors.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

DACE’s P/E ratio slightly exceeds the Australian equity sector average of 16.5, which indicates that investors are willing to pay a premium for anticipated growth driven by Dimensional's active management strategy and track record.
