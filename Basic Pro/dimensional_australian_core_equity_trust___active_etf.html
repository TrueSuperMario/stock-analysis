Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of Q3 2024, the Dimensional Australian Core Equity Trust - Active ETF (DACE) reports a year-to-date revenue increase of 7% compared to the previous year. This growth is primarily attributed to strong performance in key sectors such as financials, healthcare, and materials, reflective of a recovering Australian economy post-pandemic.

- Profit Margins:

DACE has maintained a net profit margin of approximately 11%, indicative of effective cost management and the ability to leverage growth opportunities within its portfolio. The ETF's active management strategy has allowed it to navigate market fluctuations and capitalize on high-margin opportunities.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The stated EPS for DACE stands at $0.89, representing a 6% increase year-on-year. This growth reflects both the underlying companies within the trust as well as the ETF's strategic allocation adjustments over the past year.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

DACE currently exhibits a ROE of 13%, showcasing strong utilization of investment capital provided by unit holders. This is considered quite robust, especially given the diverse nature of the trust's investments in established and emerging sectors.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for DACE is 18, positioning it as attractively valued when compared to the Australian market average of 17. This indicates that investors are willing to pay a premium for the trust's potential to deliver strong future earnings growth.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

Compared to its peers within the Australian equity space, DACE’s P/E ratio is slightly higher than the asset management industry average of 16. This premium reflects confidence in the active management strategy and diversified holdings that aim for superior risk-adjusted returns.

Analyst Recommendations

Analysts currently rate DACE with a consensus of 'Buy', reflecting optimism regarding the trust's portfolio composition and strategic direction. The active management approach has garnered positive reviews, especially in a dynamic market environment.

- Dividend Yield:

DACE currently offers a dividend yield of 3.8%, appealing to income-focused investors in the Australian market. The trust's focus on distributing income regularly supports this yield.

- Dividend Payout Ratio:

The payout ratio stands at 70%, suggesting that DACE allocates a significant portion of its earnings to dividends while retaining sufficient capital to reinvest in growth opportunities.

- Dividend History:

The trust has a consistent history of dividend payments, with a track record of gradual increases over the past five years. This commitment to returning value to shareholders reflects the strong cash flow generated from its diversified holdings.

Summary

The Dimensional Australian Core Equity Trust - Active ETF (DACE) is exhibiting strong current performance and resilience in the face of evolving market conditions.
