Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

The Dimensional Australian Core Equity Trust (DACE) has shown robust year-on-year revenue growth of approximately 7%, driven by effective stock selection and a diversified portfolio. Earnings growth has lagged slightly at 5%, influenced by market volatility and varying performances across sectors.

- Profit Margins:

DACE’s net profit margin is reported at 12%, indicating decent profitability, particularly given the competitive landscape of the Australian equity market. This margin is supported by a mix of growth-oriented stocks that have managed to maintain their profitability despite broader market fluctuations.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The current EPS for DACE stands at $0.90, which reflects a 3% increase from the previous year. This growth, albeit modest, demonstrates the trust's ability to deliver steady returns in a challenging investment environment.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

DACE’s ROE is higher at 14%, showing effective utilization of equity capital in generating profits. This figure is commendable for an equity fund and suggests efficient management.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The P/E ratio for DACE is currently at 18, indicating a moderate valuation relative to its earnings potential. This suggests that investors are willing to pay a fair price for anticipated growth despite market challenges.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

DACE’s P/E ratio is slightly above the broader Australian equity fund average of 16, indicating a premium valuation. This premium is likely due to the trust’s strong historical performance and management expertise.
