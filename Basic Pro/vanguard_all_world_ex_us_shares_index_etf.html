Current Performance

- - Assets Under Management (AUM):

As of September 2024, the Vanguard All-World Ex-US Shares INDEX ETF (VEU) has approximately $30 billion in assets under management, indicating strong investor interest in globally diversified investment options excluding U.S. stocks.

- - Price Movement:

Over the past year, VEU has exhibited a moderate performance, with a price appreciation of about 8%. The fund has rebounded from previous lows due to a stabilizing global economic outlook and a recovery in non-U.S. markets.

- - Performance Relative to Benchmark:

VEU's performance has largely mirrored that of its benchmark, the FTSE All-World Ex-US Index, showcasing effective tracking of its target index. Year-to-date, VEU has outperformed the benchmark by about 1%, indicating strong fund management.

- - Volatility:

The ETF has shown a volatility measure (beta) of 0.95 relative to the global equities market, illustrating that it is slightly less volatile than the overall market, which is favorable for risk-averse investors.

Valuation Metrics

- - Expense Ratio:

VEU has a low expense ratio of 0.08%, making it a cost-effective option for investors seeking exposure to international stocks without high management fees.

- - Forward P/E Ratio:

The forward price-to-earnings (P/E) ratio for VEU is currently at 15.5, which is attractive compared to the global equity average of around 18. This suggests that investors may find VEU appealing based on its earnings potential.

- - Price-to-Book (P/B) Ratio:

The current P/B ratio for VEU is 1.4, indicating that the ETF is trading at a slight premium to the underlying net assets of the companies in the index.

Summary

The Vanguard All-World Ex-US Shares INDEX ETF (VEU) presents a solid opportunity for investors seeking diversified exposure to international equities, excluding U.S. markets. With its favorable performance metrics, low expense ratio, and attractive dividend yield, VEU is well-positioned to capitalize on the global economic recovery. Analysts maintain a positive outlook, supported by robust market conditions and strategic fund management by Vanguard. Despite some risks associated with geopolitical tensions and inflation, the fund's diversification and low-cost structure reinforce its value as a long-term investment choice for those looking to broaden their portfolio globally.
