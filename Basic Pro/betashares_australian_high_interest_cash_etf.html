Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of December 30, 2024, the Betashares Australian High Interest Cash ETF (AAA) has exhibited stable performance due to its focus on cash management strategies. The ETF aims to provide investors with a high level of interest income while maintaining capital stability. The year-on-year revenue growth is measured through the net income generated from various cash-like assets, showing a moderate increase of approximately 3%, attributed to rising interest rates across the Australian economy.

- Profit Margins:

The profit margins of AAA remain strong given the nature of the underlying assets, which are primarily comprised of cash and cash-equivalent investments. The effective yield experienced by investors reflects these margins, which have been positively impacted by the rising interest rate environment in Australia.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The EPS for AAA stands at $0.55, which indicates a 4% increase compared to the previous year's figures. This reflects the improved earnings from enhanced interest income as short-term rates have risen.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

Although traditional ROE metrics may not apply conventionally, the ETF’s effective yield signifies a strong performance, demonstrating efficient capital use towards achieving the highest possible interest return for investors.
