Current Performance

- Fund Performance:

As of November 25, 2024, the Vanguard Australian Fixed Interest INDEX ETF (VAF) has delivered a year-to-date return of approximately 3.5%. This performance reflects stabilizing interest rates and a general uptick in bond prices as investors seek safety amid global economic uncertainties.

- Net Asset Value (NAV):

The current NAV of VAF stands at AUD 52.30, showing a modest increase from its AUD 50.80 at the start of the year, indicating positive investor sentiment towards fixed interest investments.

- Yearly Comparison:

Over the past year, returns have been affected by fluctuating interest rates and market conditions, contributing to a price performance that slightly lags behind historical averages for fixed-income investments.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Book (P/B) Ratio:

VAF's current P/B ratio is 1.05, suggesting that the fund is trading close to its underlying asset value, a typical scenario for index-based ETFs focused on fixed interest.

- Yield:

The latest yield on the fund is around 4.0%, providing a stable income source for investors. This yield indicates VAF's ability to generate consistent income through interest from the bonds it holds.

- Duration:

The fund has an average duration of 5.2 years, positioning it moderately in terms of interest rate sensitivity. This duration reflects a balanced approach to risk, especially in the current interest rate environment.

Analyst Recommendations

- Consensus Rating:

Analysts generally maintain a 'Hold' rating on VAF, reflecting a cautious outlook amid prevailing economic conditions. The fund's fixed-income focus aligns well with investors looking for stability rather than growth.

- Price Targets:

The average analyst price target estimates a range between AUD 52.00 and AUD 55.00, indicating limited short-term upside from the current NAV. Analyses suggest that gains will be more linked to rate movements than dramatic bond valuations.
