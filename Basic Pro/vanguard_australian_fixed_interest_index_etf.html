Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of January 13, 2025, the Vanguard Australian Fixed Interest INDEX ETF (VAF) has shown steady performance in a fluctuating interest rate environment, primarily driven by stable demand for Australian Government bonds. The ETF has recorded a revenue growth of approximately 4% year-on-year, reflecting its comprehensive exposure to a diversified portfolio of fixed interest securities.

- Profit Margins:

VAF operates in a fixed income space, so traditional profit margins are not directly applicable. However, the ETF's expense ratio is low at around 0.20%, ensuring that more of the returns are passed onto investors compared to many actively managed funds.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

EPS is not a typical metric for ETFs, but VAF's annualized return over the past year is approximately 5.5%, benefiting from capital appreciation in underlying bonds due to interest rate adjustments.

- Returns on Investment:

The total return on VAF for the last year has been around 6%, which is a notable performance amidst changing monetary policies and market volatility.

Valuation Metrics

- Net Asset Value (NAV):

The current NAV for VAF is approximately $50.20, reflecting the underlying value of its fixed interest holdings. This NAV is considered stable when benchmarked against the daily fluctuations in the market.

- Price-to-Book (P/B) Ratio:

VAF’s P/B ratio stands at about 1.05, suggesting that it is trading near its book value. This valuation indicates a balanced pricing relative to the fixed income index it tracks.

Summary

The Vanguard Australian Fixed Interest INDEX ETF (VAF) has shown resilient performance despite the challenges posed by rising interest rates and inflationary pressures.
