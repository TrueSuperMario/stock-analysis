Current Performance

- Net Asset Value (NAV) Growth:

As of February 10, 2025, Vanguard Australian Fixed Interest INDEX ETF (VAF) has delivered a consistent annualized return of approximately 4.5% since inception. This performance is primarily driven by the prevailing interest rate environment and the ETF's exposure to high-quality government and corporate bonds, which have performed well in the current economic backdrop.

- Price Movement:

The ETF’s share price has seen a steady increase over the last year, rising around 3% from $53.00 a year ago to $54.50 today. This moderate increase reflects stability in fixed interest investments amid fluctuating equity markets.

- Volatility:

VAF has experienced lower volatility compared to equity markets, consistent with fixed interest securities. The ETF's beta stands at 0.2, indicating it is less volatile than the broader market, which is expected in bond investments.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Book (P/B) Ratio:

The current P/B ratio for VAF is 1.05, suggesting the ETF is trading slightly above its book value, reflecting a reasonable market valuation relative to its net assets.

- Yield Metrics:

The distribution yield of VAF is currently around 4.0%, offering a competitive return for fixed interest investors, especially in a low-interest-rate environment. This yield aligns well with the Australian fixed income market trends.
