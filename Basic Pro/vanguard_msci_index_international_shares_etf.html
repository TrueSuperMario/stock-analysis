Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of February 24, 2025, Vanguard MSCI INDEX International Shares ETF (VGS) has demonstrated impressive year-on-year revenue growth of approximately 8%. This is primarily attributed to a global economic recovery and rising stock prices in the international markets where the ETF is invested. Earnings growth has closely followed suit, with a reported increase of around 7%, reflecting positive performance across its diverse international holdings.

- Profit Margins:

The fund's profit margins are indicative of healthy underlying performance, with an effective management fee structure that keeps operational costs low. Currently, expense ratios remain competitive at 0.18%, allowing a significant portion of revenue to contribute to overall returns for investors.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The ETF does not publish EPS in a traditional sense, but based on net asset value (NAV) growth, it has recorded a significant uptick, mirroring the overall market performance. As of today, the NAV has reportedly increased by approximately 9% over the past year.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

Since VGS is an ETF that tracks an index, it doesn’t operate with traditional equity metrics like ROE. However, the effective exposure to high-quality international equities has led to strong total returns, estimated at around 12% annualized, signifying robust investor returns over the long term.
