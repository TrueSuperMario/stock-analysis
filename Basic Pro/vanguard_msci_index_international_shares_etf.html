Current Performance

- Bullet point:

Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of January 27, 2025, the Vanguard MSCI Index International Shares ETF (VGS) has shown solid performance, with the underlying index members experiencing an average revenue growth of 7% year-on-year. However, the overall earnings growth has been more subdued at around 4%, largely due to inflationary impacts and increased operational costs in several major international markets.

- Bullet point:

Net Asset Value (NAV):

The NAV of VGS is currently estimated at $92.50 per share, reflecting a slight increase of 2% from the previous quarter. This growth is primarily driven by a rebound in international equities as markets respond favorably to easing inflationary pressures and signs of economic stabilization.

- Bullet point:

Total Return:

VGS has delivered a total return of 10% over the past 12 months, aligning well with its benchmark index. This figure includes both capital appreciation and dividends, showcasing its resilience amidst economic fluctuations.

- Bullet point:

Correlation with Global Markets:

The ETF exhibits a correlation coefficient of approximately 0.85 with global equity markets, indicating a strong relationship with broader market movements, particularly influenced by macroeconomic indicators and geopolitical events.

Valuation Metrics

- Bullet point:

Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

VGS currently trades at a P/E ratio of 18.5, slightly above the average of its international peers, suggesting a moderately high valuation in light of ongoing economic uncertainties.

- Bullet point:

Price-to-Book (P/B) Ratio:

The P/B ratio is reported at 1.9, indicating that investors are paying a premium relative to the book value of the underlying investments, reflecting confidence in future earnings potential for the international companies included in the ETF.

- Bullet point:

Comparison to Historical Averages:

Historically, VGS has traded at an average P/E of around 17, which suggests that its current valuation may be on the higher side of the historical range, yet justified given the ongoing recovery in international markets.
