Current Performance

- - Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of September 2024, the Vanguard MSCI Index International Shares ETF (VGS) has demonstrated a year-to-date revenue growth of approximately 8%. This growth is attributed to strong performance in key international markets, particularly in Asia and Europe, where economic recovery and consumer spending have rebounded post-pandemic. Earnings growth is reported at around 6%, positively influenced by higher equity valuations in global markets.

- - Profit Margins:

The net profit margin for the ETF holdings averages around 15%, indicating healthy profitability across its diverse range of international equities. The margin has been slightly affected by fluctuations in foreign exchange rates and operational costs in some sectors, but overall remains robust.

- - Earnings Per Share (EPS):

Projected EPS for the ETF is currently estimated at $3.50, which reflects a solid 5% increase over the prior year. This positive trend showcases the underlying strength of the global equities included in VGS.

- - Return on Equity (ROE):

The weighted average ROE of VGS’s holdings stands at approximately 13%, which is respectable for international equities. This indicates that the companies within the ETF are efficiently using shareholder equity to generate profits.

Valuation Metrics

- - Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for VGS is approximately 21, signalling that the ETF is valued at a premium in comparison to the broader market average of 19. This reflects investor confidence in the growth prospects of international equities.

- - P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

The P/E ratio of VGS is in line with the global equity ETF average of around 20.5, suggesting that it is fairly valued relative to its peers while benefiting from exposure to foreign markets with higher growth potential.
