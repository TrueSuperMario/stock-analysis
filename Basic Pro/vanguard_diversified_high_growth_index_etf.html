Current Performance

- Investment Performance:

As of October 14, 2024, the Vanguard Diversified High Growth INDEX ETF (VDHG) has demonstrated a strong performance, posting a year-to-date return of approximately 18%. This is primarily attributed to favorable market conditions and solid performances from key holdings in the technology and healthcare sectors, which make up a substantial part of the ETF's portfolio.

- Risk Metrics:

VDHG displays a relatively high beta of 1.2, indicative of its volatility compared to the broader market. This higher beta implies that while the ETF offers the potential for significant returns, it also comes with increased risk, particularly in uncertain market conditions.

- Asset Allocation:

The ETF's strategy is diversified across various asset classes, with approximately 80% allocated to equities and 20% to fixed income. This allocation reflects its high-growth focus, aimed at long-term capital appreciation.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

VDHG's current P/E ratio is around 25, indicating that investors are willing to pay a premium for expected earnings growth. This is above the historical average for similar diversified funds, suggesting that the market anticipates strong future performance.

- P/E Ratio Compared to Benchmark:

Compared to its benchmark index, the Vanguard Total World Stock Index, which has a P/E of 22, VDHG's relatively higher P/E suggests a more aggressive growth outlook, particularly given its composition heavily weighted towards growth sectors.
