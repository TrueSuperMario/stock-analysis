Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of November 25, 2024, the Dimensional Global Core Equity Trust (DFGH) has achieved a revenue growth rate of approximately 8% year-on-year. This growth is attributed to a diversified equity portfolio that captures global market opportunities. The earnings growth figures are slightly lower at around 5%, indicative of ongoing market volatility and varied performance across sectors.

- Profit Margins:

The trust’s net profit margin stands at 12%, reflecting a stable return on the investments in diverse sectors. This margin is solid for an equity trust, driven by efficient management of portfolio expenses and a focus on high-quality stocks.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The current EPS is $1.25, which has increased by 4% from the previous year. This growth reflects the overall health of the portfolio and the effect of strategic stock selections in an improving market environment.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

DFGH’s ROE is reported at 15%, indicating effective utilization of shareholder equity to generate profits. This is favourable for investors as it showcases management's ability to deliver value.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

DFGH's current P/E ratio is 21, suggesting that the trust is somewhat overvalued compared to the broader equity market averages. This valuation likely reflects a strong long-term growth outlook perceived by investors.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

The trust’s P/E ratio is above the mutual fund industry average of around 18. This premium may be justified by DFGH’s active management strategy that aims to generate alpha through detailed research and stock selection.
