Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of December 23, 2024, the iShares Core S&P/ASX 200 ETF (IOZ) has recorded a year-to-date revenue growth of approximately 8%, benefiting from a robust performance across various sectors within the Australian equity market. However, earnings growth has been slightly lower at around 5%, primarily due to inflationary pressures and increasing operational costs affecting the profitability of constituent companies.

- Profit Margins:

The ETF's profit margins stand around 12%, reflecting a moderate level of profitability for the companies included in the index. Despite pressures from rising costs, many of the large-cap companies in the S&P/ASX 200 have managed to maintain stable margins through operational efficiencies and cost-control measures.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The trailing twelve-month EPS for the underlying index is approximately AUD 1.85, which reflects a 4% increase from the previous year. This growth signifies resilience in earnings performance amid market fluctuations and economic headwinds.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The average ROE for the companies within the iShares Core S&P/ASX 200 ETF is reported at 13%, indicating effective use of shareholders' equity to generate earnings, which is a positive sign for investors.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for IOZ stands at 18, suggesting that the ETF is fairly valued relative to its earnings. This ratio indicates a slight premium compared to historical averages but reflects investor confidence in the long-term growth potential of the index constituents.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

The P/E ratio of 18 is in line with the Australian market average of 17.5, signifying that the businesses within the ETF are not overly overpriced compared to their peers, which can be appealing for value-focused investors.
