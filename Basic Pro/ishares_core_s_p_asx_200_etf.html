Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

The iShares Core S&P/ASX 200 ETF (IOZ) has shown a moderate year-on-year revenue growth of approximately 6%, largely attributed to the overall performance of the S&P/ASX 200 index, which includes a diversified portfolio of Australian equities. Earnings growth reflects solid recovery in sectors like financials and materials, which have rebounded from earlier downturns.

- Profit Margins:

The ETF generates a net profit margin reflective of the underlying index's performance, which stands at about 11%. This margin represents a healthy operating environment within the Australian share market, particularly within sectors like consumer discretionary and healthcare.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

IOZ reports an EPS of AUD 2.85, indicating a year-on-year increase of 4%. This rise is consistent with the broader market recovery and resurgence in consumer spending and business investments.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The ETF’s ROE is currently at 10%, demonstrating a solid return on investments made in the ASX 200 companies, signifying efficient use of equity across a variety of sectors.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for IOZ is approximately 15, which suggests the ETF is reasonably valued relative to its earnings potential based on the underlying components of the ASX 200.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

IOZ’s P/E ratio aligns closely with the broader Australian market average of around 14.5, indicating that it is fairly valued compared to its peers while reflecting investor optimism about Australian equities.
