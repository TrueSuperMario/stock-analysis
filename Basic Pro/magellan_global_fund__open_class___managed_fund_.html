Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of March 10, 2025, the Magellan Global Fund (Open Class) has shown a steady growth in assets under management (AUM) due to an increase in investor inflows. The fund is benefitting from a 7% year-on-year revenue growth, primarily driven by its strong performance in international equities. However, earnings growth has been muted at 2%, reflecting increased management and operational costs in the current market environment.

- Profit Margins:

The fund's net profit margin stands at approximately 15%, indicating efficient management and operational strategies in maintaining profitability despite the competitive landscape of managed funds. This margin is considered favorable for active management funds, particularly in the context of evolving market demands.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

EPS for the open class shares is currently reported at $0.90, which reflects a 5% increase from the previous year. This growth is a result of the fund’s disciplined investment approach and strategic asset allocation.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The fund's ROE is around 10%, showcasing how effectively the fund is leveraging its equity base to generate returns. This performance is considered solid for a managed fund, especially in an environment of fluctuating market conditions.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for the Magellan Global Fund stands at 18, suggesting that the fund is moderately valued compared to its earnings potential. This valuation reflects investor confidence in the fund’s investment strategy and management.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

The fund's P/E ratio is in line with the managed fund industry average of 17.5. This parity indicates that the market is pricing in similar growth expectations for Magellan as it does for its peers.
