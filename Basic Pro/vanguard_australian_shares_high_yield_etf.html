Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of December 2024, the Vanguard Australian Shares High Yield ETF (VHY) has shown robust performance, achieving a year-to-date total return of approximately 9%. This return has benefited from a combination of capital appreciation and robust dividend distributions. The ETF's underlying assets are primarily Australian companies with a strong history of paying dividends, contributing to consistent revenue generation.

- Profit Margins:

The average profit margin among the holdings within VHY stands at around 12%, indicating a healthy profitability level, supported by many companies being established players in mature sectors such as finance, telecommunications, and utilities.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The EPS for key holdings in the ETF has increased by approximately 4% over the past year, reflecting stable earnings growth amid a challenging economic environment. This trend is indicative of the relatively stable earnings profiles of high-yield stocks.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The aggregated ROE across the top holdings in VHY is around 15%. This figure illustrates the effective use of shareholder equity by these companies to generate profits, positioning the ETF well against its peers.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

VHY trades at a current P/E ratio of 15, suggesting that the ETF is reasonably valued in relation to its earnings potential. This valuation is appealing for investors seeking dividend income with solid fundamentals.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

VHY’s P/E ratio is slightly lower than the Australian equity market average of approximately 16.5, reflecting a value opportunity for investors seeking exposure to high-yield stocks, especially when considering the dividend consistency of the underlying assets.
