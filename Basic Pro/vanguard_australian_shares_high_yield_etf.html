Current Performance

- - Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of September 30, 2024, Vanguard Australian Shares High Yield ETF (VHY) has demonstrated a year-on-year revenue growth of 6%. This growth has been driven largely by its portfolio of high-dividend-paying Australian equities, supported by the recovery in consumer spending and strong performance in sectors such as financials and materials. Earnings growth has outpaced revenue slightly, at 7%, reflecting improved operational efficiencies and robust dividend payments from key Holdings.

- - Profit Margins:

The ETF boasts an average profit margin of 14%, which is commendable in the current market environment. The ETF's focus on high-yield stocks helps maintain competitive margins against the backdrop of rising operational costs and evolving market dynamics.

- - Earnings Per Share (EPS):

VHY reports an EPS of $1.25, indicating a 5% increase from the previous year. This is largely attributed to the strong performance of its underlying holdings, particularly in the banking and mining sectors, which continue to thrive in the current economic climate.

- - Return on Equity (ROE):

The ETF enjoys an ROE of 15%, which is strong among its peers and indicates effective utilization of funds to generate consistent returns for its investors.

Valuation Metrics

- - Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

VHY's current P/E ratio stands at 18, suggesting that the ETF is moderately valued compared to its historical averages. This reflects the market’s anticipation of continued dividend growth from its high-yield holdings.

- - P/E Ratio Compared to the Industry Average:

The P/E ratio for VHY is in line with the broader Australian equities market average, indicating that it is fairly valued with respect to its high yield strategy, especially given the prevailing economic conditions.

VHY delivers an attractive dividend yield of 5.5%, which appeals to income-focused investors seeking consistent returns. This yield is significantly higher than the broader market average, reflecting the ETF's underlying focus on high-yield equities.

- - Dividend Payout Ratio:

The dividend payout ratio stands at 80%, implying that the ETF returns a significant portion of its earnings via dividends while reserving enough for reinvestment. This level of payout is sustainable given the solid earnings from its core holdings.

- - Dividend History:

VHY has a reliable history of dividend payments with annual increases over the past five years. The ETF's commitment to shareholder returns is evident through its consistent distribution policy, which enhances its attractiveness to yield-focused investors.
