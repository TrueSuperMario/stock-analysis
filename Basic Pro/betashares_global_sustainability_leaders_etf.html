Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of December 30, 2024, the Betashares Global Sustainability Leaders ETF (ETHI) has experienced a revenue growth of approximately 8% year-on-year, driven by increasing investor interest in ESG (Environmental, Social, and Governance) investments and a broader market shift towards sustainable companies. Earnings growth for the ETF has been more modest, at around 4%, influenced by market volatility and the performance of constituent stocks.

- Profit Margins:

ETHI's underlying assets boast a net profit margin of around 12%. This figure indicates that the companies within the ETF's portfolio are generally operating efficiently, despite some challenges presented by global economic fluctuations and inflationary pressures.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The EPS for ETHI is currently at $1.20, representing a 5% increase compared to the previous year. This growth trend illustrates the ETF's resilience and its constituent companies' ability to adapt to changing market conditions.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The ETF's ROE stands at approximately 10%, reflecting the effective use of shareholder equity to generate profits. This is considered a healthy figure, particularly given the focus on sustainability which often leads to varied performance across different market cycles.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

ETHI currently has a P/E ratio of 22, indicating that the ETF is moderately valued compared to its earnings potential. This metric suggests a strong market confidence in the growth potential of sustainable investments, especially given the increasing trend toward ESG-oriented businesses.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

The P/E ratio of ETHI is above the average for the broader ETF market, which typically hovers around 18. This premium valuation reflects investor enthusiasm for sustainable investments but may also suggest that the ETF is priced for continued strong performance in the future.
