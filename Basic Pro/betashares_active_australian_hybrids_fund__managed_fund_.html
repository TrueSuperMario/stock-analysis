Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of January 20, 2025, the Betashares Active Australian Hybrids Fund (Managed Fund) (HBRD) has shown consistent year-on-year revenue growth of approximately 6%. This performance is driven by the robust demand for hybrid securities and the fund's strategic investments in high-quality credit issuers. Earnings growth is steady but slightly lower at around 4%, influenced by interest rate movements and wider credit spreads.

- Profit Margins:

HBRD maintains a net profit margin of around 8%. This is commendable for a managed fund and reflects efficient management of costs associated with the fund's operations, particularly in trading and management fees. The slight margin compression observed recently is attributed to increased operational costs in response to rising market volatility.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The fund's EPS has reached $0.85, showcasing an increase of 3% from the previous year. This modest growth is aligned with the fund’s consistent income distribution strategy and reflects effective reinvestment of earnings back into higher-yielding assets.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

HBRD reports an ROE of 10%, indicating good utilization of shareholder equity to generate profits. This is a solid figure for a hybrid fund and suggests efficient management of the invested capital.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for HBRD stands at 15, which positions the fund fairly within the hybrid equity market. It reflects investor confidence in the fund’s underlying asset management strategy and income generation capability.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

HBRD's P/E ratio is slightly higher than the average of 14 in the managed fund sector. This suggests that investors are willing to pay a premium for the expected income stability and quality of the underlying hybrid securities that HBRD focuses on.
