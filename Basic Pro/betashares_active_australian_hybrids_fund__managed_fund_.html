Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

The Betashares Active Australian Hybrids Fund (Managed Fund), known as HBRD, has reported a stable performance since inception, exhibiting a consistent revenue stream from diversified hybrid securities. Its performance has been bolstered by the recovery in fixed income markets, with earnings growth aligning with the broader trend of improving interest rates and credit spreads.

- Profit Margins:

The fund’s expense ratio is relatively low at around 0.55%, which is favorable compared to many other managed funds. This efficiency allows HBRD to maintain higher profit margins for investors, as operational costs do not significantly erode returns.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

While HBRD does not operate on a typical EPS basis since it focuses on fund distribution, the equivalent measure of distributions per unit has shown a slight increase over the last year, reflecting improved yield generation from the underlying holdings.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

As a managed fund, HBRD does not report a traditional ROE. However, the effective management of the hybrid securities yields a commendable return on investor capital, which aims to exceed traditional fixed-income benchmarks, showcasing the fund manager’s expertise in optimizing returns.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

Given that HBRD focuses on hybrid securities, a conventional P/E ratio is not applicable. However, the fund’s distribution yield can be considered a valuation indicator, currently sitting around 4.5%, which is attractive relative to similar investment vehicles.

- Distribution Yield compared to the industry average:

When compared to other hybrid funds, HBRD’s distribution yield remains competitive, higher than the industry average yield of approximately 4%, indicating relative value in the current interest rate environment.
