Current Performance

As of March 2025, the Betashares Active Australian Hybrids Fund (HBRD) has reported a year-on-year revenue growth of approximately 4.5%, primarily attributed to favorable market conditions for hybrid securities, including lower interest rates and a stable economic environment. The earnings growth has been slightly more robust at around 5%, reflecting improved performance across various sectors within the hybrid securities space.

- Profit Margins:

The fund has maintained a solid profit margin of 8%, indicative of its effective management of expenses relative to its income. This is considered reasonable for a fund primarily invested in hybrids, where the income sources tend to be more stable than in traditional equity markets.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The current EPS for HBRD stands at $1.05, which marks a 3% increase from the previous year. This growth is credited to strong contributions from Australian hybrid securities amidst rising interest rates globally.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

HBRD’s ROE is approximately 10%, a positive indicator of the fund's effectiveness in generating returns from its shareholder equity. This is viewed as competitive within the fixed-income sector where hybrid funds operate.
