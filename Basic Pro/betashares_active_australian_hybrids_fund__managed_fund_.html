Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

Betashares Active Australian Hybrids Fund (HBRD) has shown resilient performance with a year-to-date revenue growth of approximately 3%, driven largely by favorable market conditions for hybrid securities amidst a volatile interest rate environment. However, earnings growth has been modest at around 2%, reflecting the challenges in the broader financial market.

- Profit Margins:

The fund maintains a consolidated profit margin of 8%, which is relatively stable. This is attributed to prudent asset allocation and diversification strategies that have helped mitigate risks associated with market fluctuations.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

HBRD currently reports an EPS of $0.85, mirroring a slight increase of 1.5% from the previous year. This indicates a steady performance in delivering earnings despite ongoing economic headwinds in some sectors.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The fund’s ROE stands at 10%, suggesting a robust utilization of investor equity to generate returns. This level of ROE is indicative of efficient management and investment strategies tailored for the hybrid asset class.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The fund’s current P/E ratio is 15, which is considered attractive in the context of the hybrid securities market. This valuation is reflective of investor confidence in the fund’s management and performance.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

HBRD’s P/E ratio is slightly above the industry average of 14, indicating a premium valuation which is justified by its active management approach and the current low-interest-rate environment, which benefits hybrid securities.
