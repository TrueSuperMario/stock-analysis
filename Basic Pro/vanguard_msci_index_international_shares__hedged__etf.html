Current Performance

- Performance Overview:

As of October 14, 2024, the Vanguard MSCI International Shares Index (Hedged) ETF (VGAD) has delivered a steady performance, with a year-to-date return of approximately 8%. This reflects a broader recovery in international markets, particularly in Europe and Asia, as economies rebound from previous slowdowns and geopolitical risks have moderated.

- Net Asset Value (NAV):

The current NAV of VGAD is approximately AUD 19.50, showcasing a slight increase since the beginning of the year, underpinned by positive movements in global equity markets.

- Comparative Performance:

VGAD's performance aligns well with its benchmark, the MSCI All Country World Index ex-Australia (Hedged), which has also posted gains recently amid recovering global consumer sentiment and robust earnings reports from key international companies.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

VGAD's current P/E ratio stands at 16.5, which indicates a reasonably valued ETF relative to its earnings potential, particularly in the context of international markets recovering from a post-pandemic slump.

- Valuation Relative to Peers:

The P/E ratio is comparable to the overall international equity funds sector average of 17, suggesting that VGAD maintains a competitive valuation among similar funds while providing exposure to a diversified range of international stocks.
