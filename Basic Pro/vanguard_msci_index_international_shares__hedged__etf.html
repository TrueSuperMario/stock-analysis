Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

Vanguard MSCI International Shares (Hedged) ETF (VGAD) has demonstrated moderate growth over the past year, with revenues reflecting the overall performance of international equities. As of January 2025, VGAD has shown a year-to-date return of approximately 8% amidst a recovering global economy and resurgence in market confidence following economic adjustments to post-pandemic conditions.

- Profit Margins:

The fund is designed to track the performance of international developed market equities, hedged against currency fluctuations. Therefore, traditional profit margin metrics are not directly applicable. However, the ETF’s structure minimizes the impact of currency risk, which has historically provided more stable performance relative to unhedged international funds.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

EPS isn't a direct concept applicable to ETFs like VGAD; instead, investors typically focus on the total return generated by the fund. The ETF’s total return, which accounts for capital gains and dividends, reflects positive performance despite ongoing market volatility.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

Similar to EPS, ROE is not a relevant measure for VGAD as it represents the weighted performance of various underlying stocks in the ETF. However, the underlying companies primarily exhibit solid ROEs, indicating that the ETF holds well-performing assets.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

VGAD’s P/E ratio fluctuates as it is linked to the underlying equities it tracks; currently, it sits around 18, suggesting a premium valuation compared to some domestic peers but aligning with global trends driven by expected growth.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

The current P/E ratio is slightly above the global market average of approximately 17. This reflects a healthy premium for well-established international companies in developed markets, driven by projected revenue growth and robust business fundamentals.
