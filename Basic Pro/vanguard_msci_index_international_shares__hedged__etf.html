Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of November 2024, the Vanguard MSCI Index International Shares (Hedged) ETF (VGAD) has reported a moderate revenue growth year-on-year of approximately 6%. This growth is attributed to the positive performance of international equities, particularly in developed markets, and the ETF's strategic hedging against fluctuations in currency which has enhanced returns for Australian investors.

- Profit Margins:

VGAD has benefitted from strong profit margins, with an average expense ratio of only 0.18%, which is favorable compared to industry standards. This reflects Vanguard's efficient management structure and commitment to minimizing costs for investors.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The ETF, as a fund, does not have a conventional EPS metric since it pools investor money to hold a basket of international stocks. However, the NAV (Net Asset Value) of VGAD has increased by around 8% over the past year, indicating strong underlying performance across the funds it holds.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

VGAD's indirect ROE is reflective of the performance of the underlying stocks, which constitutes high-quality international companies listed primarily in Europe and Asia. The weighted average ROE of the underlying portfolio is approximately 14%, indicating strong profitability among these global firms.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

VGAD currently has a P/E ratio of 18, which aligns closely with the average for international equities. This suggests that the ETF is relatively fairly valued within the context of its underlying market conditions.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

The international equity average P/E is approximately 17.5, thus VGAD’s P/E ratio suggests it is slightly overvalued by about 2.9%, reflecting a premium for its strong hedging strategy which protects Australian investors from currency volatility.

Analyst Recommendations

- Consensus Rating:

Analysts have positioned VGAD with a 'Buy' consensus, recognizing the ETF's diversification across international markets and the enhancements from hedging strategies. Analysts highlight the fund's exposure to quality companies as a strong reason for this rating.

- Price Targets:

The average price target for VGAD remains optimistic, with projections ranging from $57 to $62 over the next 12 months, indicating potential upside from current trading levels, which are around $55.

VGAD has consistently distributed dividends quarterly, reflecting a commitment to returning capital to investors. The dividends have gradually increased, affirming Vanguard's strategy to reward investors through stable cash flows.

Summary

The Vanguard MSCI Index International Shares (Hedged) ETF (VGAD) remains a compelling investment choice for Australian investors seeking exposure to international equities with the added benefit of hedging against currency fluctuations. With a moderately positive performance outlook, competitive valuation metrics, and a consistent dividend yield, VGAD stands out as a well-managed fund in a recovering global market. Analyst recommendations lean towards a ‘Buy’, reflecting confidence in VGAD’s strategy and underlying portfolio quality. As international markets continue their recovery, VGAD’s strategic positioning offers a combination of stability and growth potential, making it an attractive option for both growth-oriented and income-focused investors.
