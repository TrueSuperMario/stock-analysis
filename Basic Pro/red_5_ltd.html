Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of December 2024, RED 5 Ltd has experienced a revenue growth of approximately 12% year-on-year, primarily driven by increased gold production from its flagship mine, the Darlot Gold Mine. Earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization (EBITDA) have shown a consistent improvement of around 10%, reflecting efficient cost management and increased operational efficiency.

- Profit Margins:

The company's gross profit margin stands at 38%, indicating effective cost control amidst fluctuating gold prices. This margin reflects the operational efficiency RED 5 has achieved through optimized mining processes and reduced unit costs.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

EPS for RED 5 is currently reported at $0.15, marking a 5% increase from the previous year. This growth is attributed to higher production levels and strategic investments in resource exploration.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The company's ROE is now at 8%, a healthy figure for a mining company. This indicates a solid return on shareholder investments and effective use of equity to generate profit.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

RED 5’s current P/E ratio is 16, suggesting a moderate valuation relative to its earnings potential. This level indicates that the market is reasonably confident in the company's future profitability.

- P/E Ratio Compared to the Industry Average:

The mining industry average P/E ratio is around 14. RED 5’s higher P/E suggests that investors are willing to pay a premium for its growth prospects and improved operational performance.
