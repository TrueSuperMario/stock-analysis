Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of February 24, 2025, Brickworks Ltd has reported a revenue growth of 7% year-on-year, driven by robust demand in the construction material sector, especially in residential and commercial housing. However, earnings growth appears modest at 4%, influenced by rising input costs and supply chain challenges, which have affected overall profitability.

- Profit Margins:

The company’s net profit margin is currently at 12%, demonstrating a stable performance amidst cost pressures. While margins have slightly contracted from previous levels due to inflationary impacts on raw materials, Brickworks has been able to maintain a competitive edge through operational efficiencies.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

EPS stands at AUD 1.50, reflecting a 3% increment from the previous year. This signifies Brickworks' ability to navigate through a challenging economic landscape while delivering returns to shareholders.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

Brickworks boasts an ROE of 15%, an indicator of effective utilization of equity capital to yield profits. This performance is commendable and exemplifies solid management practices in the construction and building materials industry.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for Brickworks Ltd is 22, suggesting that the stock is trading at a premium compared to its earnings potential. This reflects investor confidence in Brickworks’ market position and growth prospects.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

Brickworks’ P/E ratio is above the construction materials industry average of 18, indicating a higher valuation that may be justified by the company's strong brand reputation and sustainable business model in the building materials sector.
