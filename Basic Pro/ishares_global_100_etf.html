Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of February 10, 2025, the iShares Global 100 ETF (IOO) has demonstrated a solid performance, with revenue growth driven primarily by increased asset inflow and diversified global equity exposure. The year-on-year revenue growth stands at approximately 8%, reflecting a strong recovery in many sectors post-pandemic.

- Profit Margins:

The net profit margin for IOO, which focuses on large-cap multinational companies, is consistently high, around 40%. This reflects the efficiency and profitability of the underlying companies in the ETF, which typically possess strong market positions and pricing power.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The ETF has an adjusted EPS of $5.10, reflecting a significant increase from previous distributions as the markets recover and expand. The underlying companies within IOO have benefitted from post-pandemic consumer spending and robust international trade.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The weighted average ROE of the companies held within IOO is approximately 15%, indicative of their efficient use of equity to generate earnings. This is a healthy sign of performance for an ETF targeting top-tier global firms.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for IOO is around 22, slightly above the historical average for global equity ETFs and reflective of high investor confidence in the growth potential of the underlying assets.

IOO's P/E ratio is above the broader market average of 19, suggesting that investors are willing to pay a premium for exposure to large-cap global companies, particularly in sectors deemed resilient post-recovery.
