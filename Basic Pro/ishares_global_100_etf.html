Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of February 3, 2025, the iShares Global 100 ETF (IOO) has seen moderate revenue growth over the past year due to the recovery of global markets and strong performances from its top holdings, which include major corporations like Apple, Microsoft, and Amazon. The ETF has achieved a year-on-year total return of approximately 8%, reflecting positive market sentiment and robust economic recovery post-pandemic.

- Profit Margins:

The effective fund expenses ratio for IOO stands at 0.40%, indicated by its efficient management and low costs in relation to its net asset value. This allows for better retention of profits for reinvestment and distributions.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

While ETFs do not have EPS in the traditional sense, the overall earnings yield from underlying assets has been robust, supported by the strong performance of the tech and healthcare sectors that comprise a significant portion of the fund.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

Although ROE is not typically reported for ETFs, the effective portfolio turnover has remained low, indicating that the management’s long-term approach aligns well with stable returns from its formidable holdings.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The ETF currently trades at a P/E ratio of 22, which is on the higher end of the spectrum. This valuation indicates that investors are paying a premium for expected future earnings growth, especially given the historical profitability of its major holdings.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

IOO’s P/E ratio exceeds the current average for global equity ETFs (around 18), suggesting that it may be overvalued compared to its peers, although this is justified by the quality of its constituent companies and their growth potential.
