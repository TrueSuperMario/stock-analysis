Current Performance

- Performance Overview:

As of October 21, 2024, the iShares Global 100 ETF (IOO) has demonstrated solid performance over the past year, with a total return of approximately 12%. This performance is largely driven by the strong recovery in global equity markets and the ETF's exposure to high-quality multinational companies.

- Top Holdings Contribution:

The ETF's top holdings, which include tech giants such as Apple, Microsoft, and Amazon, have significantly contributed to its overall performance. The technology sector, which constitutes a substantial portion of IOO's portfolio, has continued to outperform in the current market climate.

- Volatility Metrics:

IOO's annualized volatility has been relatively moderate at around 15%, indicating a stable performance compared to broader market indices. This level of volatility reflects the ETF's focus on large-cap, blue-chip companies, which are typically more resilient during market fluctuations.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

IOO currently has a P/E ratio of 22, suggesting that the ETF is trading at a premium relative to its earnings. This is typical given its selection of high-growth, high-quality firms that investors are willing to pay a higher price for in anticipation of future growth.

- P/E Ratio Comparison:

Compared to the S&P 500 index, which has a median P/E ratio of around 20, IOO's valuation is slightly elevated. This is indicative of the premium investors are willing to pay for the stability and growth potential of the underlying companies.
