Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

The Betashares Australia 200 ETF (A200) has shown a year-to-date return of approximately 10% as of October 14, 2024. This performance is primarily driven by robust gains in the Australian equities market, particularly in the materials and financial sectors. Earnings growth across the underlying companies within the ETF has been steady, with several constituents reporting positive quarterly results that have supported this growth trajectory.

- Profit Margins:

The overall net profit margin for the companies in A200 averages around 12%, reflecting healthy profitability from its largest sectors—finance and resources. The diversified nature of the ETF helps mitigate risks associated with sector-specific downturns, maintaining overall stability in profit margins.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The weighted average EPS for the underlying stocks in A200 stands at approximately AUD 3.15, representing a 7% increase over the past year. This growth trajectory reflects the continued recovery of the Australian economy post-pandemic and strong consumer demand in various sectors.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The ETF's constituents collectively exhibit an average ROE of 14%, signifying effective management and utilization of shareholder funds. This is particularly noteworthy in the finance sector, which comprises a significant portion of the ETF's holdings.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for A200 is approximately 18, which suggests that the ETF is trading at a moderate valuation compared to historical averages. This level indicates that investors are willing to pay a premium for the potential growth of the underlying companies.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

A200’s P/E ratio is slightly above the broader ASX 200 average of 17, reflecting the strong performance and growth potential of its top holdings, particularly in sectors like technology and healthcare.

Analysts maintain a 'Buy' consensus rating for A200, largely due to the ETF’s exposure to key growth sectors such as technology, health care, and consumer discretionary. The diversified approach of A200 is viewed positively as it mitigates sector risks.
