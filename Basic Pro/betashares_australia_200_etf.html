Current Performance

- Net Asset Value (NAV) Growth:

As of October 28, 2024, the Betashares Australia 200 ETF (A200) reports a year-to-date NAV growth of approximately 8%, reflecting positive market sentiment towards Australian equities, particularly in the resources and financial sectors. The recent bullish trends in commodities have substantially boosted its performance, marking a compelling recovery from previous market fluctuations.

- Performance Relative to Benchmark:

A200 has consistently outperformed its benchmark, the S&P/ASX 200 Index, which has grown by about 6% in the same period. This indicates that A200 effectively captures the upside of the leading ASX-listed companies, emphasizing its role as a key tool for passive investors looking to gain exposure to the Australian market.

- Year-on-Year Comparison:

Compared to the same period last year, A200's performance shows an improved trend, with a jump from approximately 4% growth in 2023 to 8% in 2024, suggesting stronger market dynamics and increased investor confidence.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

A200 currently has a P/E ratio of 16, aligning closely with the average for the ASX 200. This valuation indicates that the ETF is reasonably priced compared to its earnings potential, providing an attractive entry point for investors.

- Price-to-Book (P/B) Ratio:

The P/B ratio for A200 stands at 2.1, which is consistent with historical norms for the Australian market. This suggests that while the ETF is benefiting from growth, there is still relative value compared to its book value, encouraging long-term perspectives.

- ETF Expense Ratio:

A200 boasts a low expense ratio of 0.07%, making it one of the cost-effective options for investors seeking exposure to the ASX 200. The low fees enhance the net returns for investors over time.
