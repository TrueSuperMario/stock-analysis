Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of November 2024, the Betashares Global Sustainability Leaders ETF (ETHI) has shown strong performance with year-to-date returns of approximately 18%. This growth can be attributed to the positive performance of its underlying holdings, which focus on companies that adhere to high sustainability criteria. The ETF’s approach aligns well with increasing global investment trends toward sustainable and responsible investing, which has driven significant interest and inflows.

- Profit Margins:

ETHI's expense ratio stands at 0.59%, which is competitive within the ETF space. Despite fluctuations in global markets, the fund has maintained a robust return profile, reflecting the efficient management of expenses relative to the revenue gained from the fund’s investments.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

Since ETHI is an ETF, it does not report traditional earnings per share. However, the performance of the underlying stocks has positively impacted the Net Asset Value (NAV) per unit, experiencing a substantial increase this year, registering around $13.50 per unit.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

As an ETF, ETHI does not have a ROE in the traditional sense since it holds shares of various companies. However, the underlying companies within the ETF generally report strong ROE, often exceeding 15%, emphasizing effective management and profitability within the sustainability sector.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

ETHI currently has a P/E ratio of approximately 25, reflecting a valuation premium that is common among growth-oriented sustainability-focused funds. The elevated P/E ratio suggests that investors are willing to pay a premium for exposure to companies perceived as future-oriented and sustainable.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

The P/E ratio of ETHI is notably higher than the average P/E ratio of 20 for the broader market, indicating a strong investor sentiment towards high-growth sustainability companies as compared to traditional sectors.
