Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of March 3, 2025, the Betashares Global Sustainability Leaders ETF (ETHI) has demonstrated a year-on-year revenue growth of approximately 7%. This growth has been primarily driven by high demand for ESG (Environmental, Social, and Governance) investments and an increasing number of investors seeking sustainable investment options. The fund’s earnings have also seen a robust growth rate of 6%, benefitting from strong stock performance within its holdings of global sustainable companies.

- Profit Margins:

ETHI maintains a net profit margin of 12%, which is commendable for an ETF. This margin reflects lower operating expenses and efficient management of the portfolio, focusing on companies that prioritize sustainability while maintaining profitability.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The ETF reported an EPS of $1.25, representing a 5% increase from the previous year. This modest growth reflects strong underlying performance from the sustainable companies within the ETF’s portfolio, which have effectively navigated a challenging economic landscape.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The current ROE stands at 14%, indicating effective utilization of shareholder equity and strong performance relative to many traditional equity investments. This is indicative of the quality of companies chosen for the portfolio, which are adept at not only generating profits but also creating value for shareholders.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for ETHI is 22, suggesting a premium valuation compared to traditional equity benchmarks. This indicates high investor confidence in the sustainable investment theme and the future growth prospects of the companies within the ETF.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

ETHI’s P/E is above the average P/E of 20 for global equity funds, reflecting a market preference for growth and sustainability focused stocks, suggesting investors are willing to pay a premium for sustainability-oriented investments.
