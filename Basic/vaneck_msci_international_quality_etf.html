Current Performance

- Assets Under Management (AUM):

The VanEck MSCI International Quality ETF (QUAL) has approximately $3 billion in assets under management. This size reflects institutional interest and provides liquidity for investors.

- Year-to-Date Performance:

As of December 2, 2024, QUAL has shown a year-to-date return of approximately 15%. This performance indicates solid investor confidence, reflecting macroeconomic factors that favor quality stocks in the international market.

- Performance Relative to Benchmark:

QUAL has outperformed its benchmark, the MSCI World ex USA Index, by around 3% this year. This outperformance is attributed to the ETF’s focus on higher-quality companies with robust fundamentals.

- Sector Allocation:

The ETF is heavily weighted towards technology and consumer discretionary sectors, which have shown resilience during economic fluctuations. This strategic allocation has contributed positively to its performance.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

QUAL currently has a P/E ratio of 22, suggesting that the ETF is trading at a premium compared to the broader market, which may indicate strong investor expectations based on the quality of holdings.

- Price-to-Book (P/B) Ratio:

The P/B ratio stands at 3.5, indicating that investors are willing to pay more for high-quality companies, highlighting the premium investors place on stocks with strong profit margins and returns on equity.

- Forward P/E Ratio:

The forward P/E ratio is projected at 20, reflecting expected earnings growth in the coming year, suggesting optimism about the future earnings potential of the underlying assets.
