Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of January 13, 2025, the VanEck MSCI International Quality ETF (QUAL) has shown strong performance, with a year-to-date revenue growth rate of 8%. This is driven primarily by solid gains among the underlying stocks in its portfolio, which focus on high-quality companies with robust fundamentals.

- Profit Margins:

The ETF's weighted average profit margin for its holdings is approximately 15%. Given that QUAL emphasizes quality companies, this margin is healthy and indicates effective cost management among its constituent companies, maintaining profitability amidst varying market conditions.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

Currently, the ETF exhibits an EPS growth of 6% compared to the previous year, which is a reflection of the earnings resilience among the high-quality international firms it invests in. This marks a positive trend, supporting the ETF’s long-term investment thesis.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The ROE of the companies within QUAL stands at an impressive 18%, showcasing how well these companies utilize shareholder equity to generate profits. This statistic reinforces the ETF’s focus on quality, as it selectively invests in firms with strong historical performance and sustainable business models.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio of QUAL is around 20, suggesting that it is moderately valued in relation to its earnings. This metric reflects investor confidence in the quality of the underlying holdings and their future growth potential.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

QUAL's P/E ratio is in line with the global average for high-quality international equities, indicating that the market is pricing in both the quality and stability of the underlying companies. The average sector P/E is around 19, so QUAL is slightly above average, justified by its quality focus.
