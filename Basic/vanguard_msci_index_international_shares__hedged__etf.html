Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of November 2024, Vanguard MSCI Index International Shares (Hedged) ETF (VGAD) has seen a year-on-year revenue growth of approximately 8%. This growth is attributed to the broader recovery in international equities, particularly in developed markets, and a rebound in global trade.

- Profit Margins:

While VGAD is an ETF and thus doesn’t report traditional profit margins, the net expense ratio for VGAD is relatively low at 0.13%, which helps maximize investor returns by minimizing costs.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

Since VGAD is an ETF, it doesn't produce earnings per share in the traditional sense. However, the fund has tracked closely to the performance of its underlying index, reflecting the average performance of its constituents, which have shown modest positive growth.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

ROE is not applicable in the traditional sense for an ETF, but VGAD’s tracking of a broad index indicates that it represents a diversified exposure to international equities, suggesting a healthy utilization of capital across its underlying holdings.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

VGAD mirrors the P/E ratio of its underlying index, which currently stands at around 17. This indicates a reasonable valuation relative to historical averages for international equity markets, suggesting a balanced risk-reward profile.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

The P/E ratio of VGAD is generally in line with the average for international equity indices, making it an attractive option compared to more volatile or highly valued sectors in domestic markets.

Analyst Recommendations

- Consensus Rating:

Analysts generally view VGAD positively, often recommending a 'Buy' due to its diversification benefits and low cost structure. The ETF is seen as a strategic choice for investors looking to hedge against domestic market fluctuations.

- Price Targets:

As VGAD is an ETF, it does not have a price target in the same way individual stocks do. However, analysts typically expect it to track the performance of its underlying index closely, which projects stable mid-term growth.

Insider Activity

- Recent Transactions:

Given that VGAD is an ETF, insider trading activity is not a relevant metric. However, the Vanguard Group, as the ETF manager, consistently demonstrates strong support for the product, managing it in accordance with market demand.

- Overall Sentiment:

The general sentiment surrounding VGAD remains stable, as it is designed to provide passive exposure to international equities while hedging currency risks.

Dividend Analysis

- Dividend Yield:

VGAD offers a dividend yield of approximately 2.5%, appealing for investors seeking income alongside capital growth. While this is modest compared to some other asset classes, it is reflective of the underlying equity performance.

- Dividend Payout Ratio:

As an ETF, VGAD does not have a traditional payout ratio. However, the fund generally distributes income from dividends paid by its underlying equities to its shareholders.

- Dividend History:

VGAD has a consistent history of quarterly dividend distributions, which have generally increased in line with the overall performance of the international equities it tracks, indicating reliability in its income-generating capacity.

Summary

Vanguard MSCI Index International Shares (Hedged) ETF (VGAD) presents a compelling option for investors seeking diversified exposure to international equities while hedging against currency risks. With solid year-on-year revenue growth and a stable dividend yield of 2.5%, VGAD is well-positioned to navigate the current market challenges. Overall, VGAD offers a strategic tool for investors looking to enhance their portfolios through international equity exposure, making it a noteworthy consideration for long-term investment strategies.
