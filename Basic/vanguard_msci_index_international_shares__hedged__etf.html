Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

Vanguard MSCI Index International Shares (Hedged) ETF (VGAD) has shown moderate performance driven by underlying index performance. The ETF tracks the MSCI World ex Australia Index, focusing on large- and mid-cap equities across developed markets, and has seen an approximate 7% year-on-year revenue growth. However, earnings growth remains challenged due to economic fluctuations and market volatility.

- Profit Margins:

As a passively managed ETF, VGAD does not directly report profit margins. However, the constituent companies within the fund typically display a range of profit margins, generally averaging around 10-15% for managed and non-managed sectors in developed markets.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

EPS is not applicable for VGAD as it is an ETF and does not have its own earnings. Instead, the earnings potential is derived from the underlying index which reflects the collective performance of its constituents.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

ROE metrics are not applicable to VGAD directly; however, the underlying companies in the index exhibit varied ROE levels, with averages typically around 12-14%, suggesting a robust utilization of equity within sectors represented by the ETF.

Valuation Metrics

VGAD's P/E ratio is in line with the average P/E of similar international equity ETFs, which ranges from 16 to 20. This suggests VGAD is fairly valued in the context of its benchmarks and peers.
