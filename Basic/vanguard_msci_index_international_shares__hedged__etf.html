Current Performance

- Net Asset Value (NAV) and Market Price:

The Vanguard MSCI International Shares (Hedged) ETF (VGAD) currently has a NAV of approximately $48.25 per share, trading close to its NAV as market prices reflect underlying index performance. The ETF aims to provide investors with exposure to international equities while shielding them from currency fluctuations between the Australian dollar and foreign currencies.

- Year-to-Date Performance:

VGAD has seen a year-to-date return of about 10%, largely attributed to strong performances in international markets, especially in developed economies. Variability exists but has remained within expected ranges for a hedged ETF investing in international equities.

- Total Return Performance:

The total returns have been supported by capital appreciation and, although market conditions are volatile, the fund has generally outperformed its benchmark index, which reflects rising equity prices among its component stocks.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

VGAD has a P/E ratio of approximately 17, which aligns well with the broader international equity market. This value suggests a moderate valuation, reflective of market expectations for the future earnings growth potential of international companies included in the fund.

- Price-to-Book (P/B) Ratio:

The price-to-book ratio for VGAD is about 1.8, indicating that the shares are trading above their book value but are consistent with the evaluation metrics typically seen in international equities, especially considering growth prospects.
