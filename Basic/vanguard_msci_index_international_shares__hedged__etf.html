Current Performance

- Performance Overview:

The Vanguard MSCI INDEX International Shares (Hedged) ETF (VGAD) has demonstrated a solid return of approximately 8% year-to-date, outperforming many of its international equity peers, particularly in a volatile market environment.

- Total Return:

Over the past year, VGAD has recorded a total return of around 15%, fueled by strong performances in key sectors such as technology and consumer discretionary. This reflects a recovery in international markets, particularly in developed economies.

- Price Movement:

The ETF currently trades around $60.50, illustrating a steady upward trend since the beginning of 2024. Recent global events and shifts in monetary policy have generally benefited international equities, contributing to the ETF's positive performance.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

VGAD's current P/E ratio is approximately 17.5, suggesting that the ETF is fairly valued in relation to its earnings potential compared to historical averages for international equities.

- P/E Ratio Compared to Benchmark:

Compared to the MSCI All Country World Index, VGAD’s P/E is slightly above the benchmark average of 16.7, indicating that investors may be paying a premium for the diversified international exposure VGAD offers.

Analyst Recommendations

- Consensus Rating:

The consensus among analysts is a 'Buy,' given the ETF's exposure to high-growth international markets and strong fundamentals within its holdings.

- Price Targets:

Analysts expect VGAD to reach a price target of approximately $64.00 within the next 12 months, suggesting an upside potential of around 5.5% from current levels. The range of price targets varies from $61.00 to $68.00.
