Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of November 4, 2024, Vanguard MSCI INDEX International Shares (Hedged) ETF (VGAD) has displayed resilient performance in a challenging economic environment. The ETF has reported a year-to-date revenue increase of approximately 8%, attributed mainly to global market recoveries post-pandemic and rebound in international equities. Earnings growth is slightly behind at around 6%, reflecting the cautious sentiment in some international markets.

- Net Asset Value (NAV):

VGAD's current NAV is $25.60, showing a solid recovery since the beginning of the year, but still below pre-pandemic peaks. The ETF has consistently tracked the performance of the MSCI All Country World Index (ACWI) ex USA IMI Hedged to AUD, demonstrating its efficiency in hedging against currency volatility.

- Performance against Benchmark:

VGAD outperformed its benchmark index by 2% over the past year, a notable accomplishment given the broader market volatility.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The P/E ratio for VGAD stands at 18.5, which is in line with its historical averages and slightly below the global average of 20 for diversified equity ETFs. This suggests that the ETF is reasonably valued relative to its earnings.

- Price-to-Book (P/B) Ratio:

VGAD has a P/B ratio of 1.7, which indicates a slight premium over its net asset value. This valuation is reflective of the high-quality holdings within the ETF that are expected to produce stable earnings growth.

- Comparative Valuation:

Compared to its peers focused on international equities, VGAD’s P/E and P/B ratios are competitive, offering investors reasonable entry points considering the volatility.
