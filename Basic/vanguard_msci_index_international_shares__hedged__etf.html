Current Performance

- Total Return:

As of December 23, 2024, the Vanguard MSCI Index International Shares (Hedged) ETF (VGAD) has delivered a total return of approximately 12% year-to-date, primarily attributed to strong performances from dual-listed companies and multitiered earnings in the international markets it tracks.

- Net Asset Value (NAV):

The ETF's NAV currently sits around AUD 25.50, reflecting a slight premium over its market price, indicating healthy investor interest and efficient pricing mechanisms relative to its underlying assets.

- Performance Relative to Benchmark:

VGAD has outperformed its benchmark index, the MSCI All Country World Index ex USA, which has recorded a return of approximately 9% over the same timeframe. This reflects the successful hedging strategies employed and the ETF's focus on quality multinational stocks.

- Volatility:

The ETF exhibits a relatively low volatility of around 10%, underscoring its defensive characteristics despite exposure to international market fluctuations.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

VGAD has a P/E ratio of 18, which is in line with the broader international equity market but slightly above the historical average for the MSCI index it tracks.

- Price-to-Book (P/B) Ratio:

The P/B ratio stands at 1.8, indicating that VGAD is trading at a moderate premium compared to its book value. This might signal investor confidence in the long-term growth prospects of the international stocks within the portfolio.

Summary

The Vanguard MSCI Index International Shares (Hedged) ETF (VGAD) presents a compelling case for investors seeking international exposure while mitigating currency risk through effective hedging strategies. With strong year-to-date performance, attractive valuation metrics, and a solid dividend yield, VGAD appeals to both growth-oriented and income-focused investors. The ETF is well-positioned to benefit from favorable global market conditions and represents a diversified entry point into international equities. Overall, analysts maintain a positive outlook based on strong fundamentals and growth prospects in the international landscape.
