Current Performance

- Price Movement:

As of November 18, 2024, the SPDR S&P/ASX 200 Fund (STW) is trading at approximately AUD 60.50, reflecting a year-to-date increase of around 8%. This performance aligns with broader positive trends in the Australian equity market, bolstered by resilient corporate earnings and economic stability.

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

The underlying companies tracked by STW have reported a collective revenue growth of approximately 6% year-over-year, with earnings growth at around 5%. This is indicative of the fund’s exposure to various sectors experiencing steady demand, particularly in materials and financial services.

- Profit Margins:

The aggregate profit margin for the constituents of STW is around 12%, which is considered robust in the context of the Australian market, particularly given inflationary pressures faced by many companies.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The estimated EPS for the ASX 200 is expected to be AUD 3.80, reflecting a 4% increase compared to the previous year. This is a positive sign, showing that companies within the index are managing cost pressures effectively.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

STW’s underlying companies boast an average ROE of 14%, indicating that these firms are efficiently using shareholder equity to generate profits. This figure reflects strong operational performance in most sectors.

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The P/E ratio of STW is on par with the ASX 200’s historical P/E average of 17.5, suggesting that the index is fairly valued considering current earnings potential.
