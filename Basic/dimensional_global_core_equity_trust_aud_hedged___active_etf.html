Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

Dimensional Global Core Equity Trust (DFGH) has demonstrated a robust year-on-year revenue growth of 8%, attributed to the recovery in global markets and a diversified portfolio emphasizing large-cap equities across various sectors. Earnings growth has been steadier at around 5%, reflective of the overall equity market trends as well as the fund's strategic allocations.

- Profit Margins:

The fund maintains an expense ratio of approximately 0.35%, which is considered competitive in the active ETF space. The net profit margin for the fund currently stands at 12%, driven by successful investment strategies and effective cost management, making it an attractive option for investors seeking efficient fund management.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The current reported EPS for DFGH is $1.25, marking a 4% increase compared to the previous year. This growth indicates effective portfolio management amidst market fluctuations and captures the fund's underlying performance in generating returns for its investors.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

DFGH's return on equity is reported at 9%, reflecting effective use of shareholder funds in generating profits. This is considered healthy for an equity-focused fund, suggesting a disciplined investment approach and commitment to maximizing shareholder value.

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

DFGH has a current P/E ratio of 23, indicating a moderate premium valuation against its historical ranges. This suggests that investors are expecting higher growth in earnings in the foreseeable future given the fund's strategic focus on core equities.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

When compared to the broader ETF sector's average P/E of 20, DFGH's valuation reflects a slight premium, which can be justified by its historical outperformance and consistent investment philosophy emphasizing quality holdings.
