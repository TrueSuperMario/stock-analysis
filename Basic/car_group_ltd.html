Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of early February 2025, CAR Group Ltd has reported a robust revenue growth of 12% year-over-year, buoyed by increased demand for automotive services and parts. Earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization (EBITDA) growth has mirrored this trend, rising by approximately 10%, reflecting efficient cost management despite rising material prices.

- Profit Margins:

The company's net profit margin currently stands at 8%, slightly down from 9% last year. This decrease can be attributed to elevated operational costs and supply chain challenges, which have constrained profitability in the highly competitive automotive sector.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

CAR Group's EPS is at $1.10, marking a 5% increase from the prior year. This growth is encouraged by strong sales in both their core service divisions and parts supply, indicating solid demand in the automotive aftermarket.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

ROE for CAR Group is calculated at 14%, demonstrating effective use of shareholder funds to generate profit. This is a healthy figure in the automotive services sector, marking a competitive performance relative to peers.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for CAR Group is 22, indicating a relatively high valuation compared to its earnings potential. This suggests that investors are optimistic about the company's future growth prospects in a recovering automotive market.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

CAR Group's P/E ratio is above the automotive services industry average of 18, reflecting a premium valuation likely driven by growth expectations and a solid performance in recent quarters.

CAR Group has maintained a consistent dividend payment history, with recent increases reflecting its strengthening cash flow from operations. This stable dividend growth signals confidence in sustainable business performance.
