Current Performance

- Performance Overview:

As of February 24, 2025, the Betashares Active Australian Hybrids Fund (HBRD) has generated a return of approximately 7.5% year-to-date. This performance is attributed to a favorable interest rate environment, which has allowed hybrid securities to perform well, particularly in light of a steady demand for income-generating assets.

- Risk-Adjusted Returns:

The fund's Sharpe ratio stands at 0.85, indicating that HBRD has provided a good level of return relative to the risk taken. This reflects effective management in navigating the complexities associated with hybrid securities.

- Asset Allocation:

The fund maintains a diversified portfolio with exposure to both capital notes and convertible securities across various sectors. Notably, the financial sector comprises around 60% of the portfolio, followed by utilities and real estate.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Book (P/B) Ratio:

HBRD has a current P/B ratio of 1.10, suggesting that the market values the fund’s portfolio slightly above its book value. This is reasonable in light of the robust income-producing assets contained within.

- Yield Metrics:

The current yield of HBRD is approximately 4.5%, offering an attractive income stream for investors seeking regular distributions as interest rates moderate. This yield positions HBRD favorably against traditional equities and pure bond funds.
