Current Performance

- Fund Performance Overview:

As of February 3, 2025, the Betashares Active Australian Hybrids Fund (HBRD) has delivered a year-to-date return of approximately 6.5%. This reflects a recovery trend following the market's volatility in 2024 caused by interest rate fluctuations and economic uncertainty. Over the past year, the fund has achieved a total return of around 9%, outperforming many benchmark indices that have been struggling with rising bond yields.

- Return Distribution:

The fund's returns have primarily stemmed from income distributions, reflecting the portfolio’s focus on hybrid securities. The monthly distribution has averaged around 0.5%, contributing significantly to its total return.

- Risk-Adjusted Performance:

The fund's Sharpe ratio stands at 0.75, indicating that the fund has generated decent excess returns relative to its risk. This suggests effective management of risk, particularly in the current environment of rising interest rates and market volatility.

Valuation Metrics

- Price to Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

Although HBRD primarily invests in hybrid securities, its P/E ratio equivalent is derived from its underlying assets, which is approximately 15. This valuation is moderately attractive compared to industry averages of 18, hinting at relative undervaluation.

- Yield Metrics:

The trailing yield on the fund is approximately 5.7%, indicating a substantial return for investors seeking income. This yield is competitive compared to traditional fixed-income investments, particularly in a low-interest-rate environment.
