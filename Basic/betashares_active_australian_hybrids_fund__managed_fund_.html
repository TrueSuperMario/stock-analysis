Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

The Betashares Active Australian Hybrids Fund (HBRD) has demonstrated a moderate year-on-year revenue growth of approximately 4%, driven by consistent demand for hybrid securities as investors seek yield in a low-interest-rate environment. Earnings growth has been slightly higher at 5%, reflecting effective management of the underlying portfolio, which spans various hybrid securities.

- Profit Margins:

The Fund's net profit margin is around 9%, which is commendable given the complexities involved in managing a diverse range of hybrid instruments. The mix of fixed and floating rate exposures has helped mitigate risks associated with interest rate volatility.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

EPS for HBRD currently stands at $0.95, representing a noteworthy increase of 3% from the previous year. This growth is indicative of the fund's operational efficiency and the strategic selection of securities that provide stable returns.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The Fund has an ROE of approximately 10%, reflecting its capability to generate profits from its hybrid securities investments, affirming its strategic asset allocation and portfolio management.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

HBRD's current P/E ratio is 20, which suggests a premium valuation relative to many traditional equity funds. Despite this elevation, the valuation can be justified by the consistent yield and portfolio quality that the Fund offers.

- P/E Ratio Compared to the Industry Average:

The P/E ratio of HBRD is above the sector average of 17 for hybrid funds, indicating robust investor confidence in the fund's ability to navigate market fluctuations while delivering returns.
