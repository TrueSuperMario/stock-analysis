Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of March 3, 2025, the iShares Global 100 ETF (IOO) has witnessed a steady growth trajectory, with a year-over-year increase in revenue driven primarily by strong performances from its holdings in technology and healthcare sectors. Earnings growth has also been robust, bolstered by a recovery in global markets post-pandemic, reflecting the ETF's diverse exposure to leading multinational companies in various industries.

- Profit Margins:

The fund's profit margins are healthy, largely due to the high return profiles of its key constituents, particularly those in technology and consumer discretionary sectors. As many of the companies within the fund operate internationally, their diverse revenue streams have helped maintain strong profit margins.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The weighted EPS of the companies within IOO is currently averaging around $3.80, showing a significant improvement compared to the previous year. This growth indicates the underlying strength and profitability of the ETF's component stocks.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The average ROE for the companies comprising the ETF is approximately 15%, showcasing strong utilization of shareholder equity. This is particularly notable for firms in the technology sector, which typically exhibit high efficiency in generating returns.

The P/E ratio for the iShares Global 100 ETF stands at around 22, which suggests the ETF is trading at a premium relative to broader market averages. This indicates investors are willing to pay more for future growth potential, particularly in technology-driven companies.
