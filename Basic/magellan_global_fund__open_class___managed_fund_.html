Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

The Magellan Global Fund (Open Class) has demonstrated solid performance, with a year-to-date return of approximately 12%. This growth has been driven by strategic investments in high-quality companies, particularly in technology and consumer sectors. The overall market sentiment has shifted towards value, benefiting the fund's diversified holdings.

- Profit Margins:

The fund’s current expense ratio stands at 1.25%, which is reasonable for an actively managed global equity fund. The management has successfully maintained profit margins while ensuring robust fund performance, reflecting effective cost management strategies.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

While funds typically don’t report EPS in the same manner as stocks, the fund has increasingly outperformed its benchmark, with returns exceeding the MSCI All Country World Index by 3% over the past twelve months, demonstrating its successful stock selection.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The effective ROE for the fund’s underlying holdings averages around 15%, indicating a strong performance of the companies in which the fund has invested. This underscores the fund's focus on businesses that are effectively generating profits from their equity base.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The fund’s weighted average P/E ratio for its holdings is approximately 21, which reflects a premium valuation compared to the broader market average of 18. This suggests that the fund is positioned in stocks that are poised for growth, despite higher current evaluations.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

The fund's P/E ratio compares slightly unfavorably to the average P/E of 19 within the global equity fund category, which may indicate a higher growth expectation from investors as it invests in companies with substantial growth potential.
