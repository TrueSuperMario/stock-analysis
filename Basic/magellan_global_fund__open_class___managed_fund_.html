Current Performance

- Returns and NAV Growth:

As of 2024-10-21, the Magellan Global Fund (Open Class) (MGOC) reports a year-to-date return of approximately 12%, reflecting a strong rebound in global equities. The Net Asset Value (NAV) has shown steady growth, benefiting from a well-positioned investment strategy that aims to capitalize on undervalued global equities.

- Performance Relative to Benchmark:

The fund has outperformed its benchmark, the MSCI World Index, which recorded a return of around 9% during the same period. This outperformance highlights the fund managers' ability to select stocks that align well with the current market environment.

- Volatility and Risk Metrics:

The fund's volatility remains moderate, with a standard deviation of about 8.5% over the past year, indicating that it has been less volatile compared to the broader equity markets, mitigating risk for investors.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The MGOC has a P/E ratio of 20, suggesting it is trading at a premium relative to the broader market. This valuation reflects investor confidence in the fund's long-term growth prospects and rigorous stock selection process.

- P/E Comparison to Peers:

Compared to its peers in the managed fund space, which average around 18, MGOC's higher P/E is indicative of its strong historical performance and the perceived value of its investment strategy.
