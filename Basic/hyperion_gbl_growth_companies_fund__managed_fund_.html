Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of March 10, 2025, the Hyperion GBL Growth Companies Fund (HYGG) has shown strong growth, with annual revenue increasing by 8% compared to the previous year. This growth is predominantly driven by robust performances in technology and healthcare sectors, which comprise a substantial portion of the fund’s holdings. The fund's earnings growth has also been positive, recording a 7% increase year-on-year as the companies within the portfolio benefit from post-pandemic recovery trends.

- Profit Margins:

The fund maintains a healthy average profit margin of 12%. This reflects strong operational efficiency among its underlying holdings, particularly in high-growth sectors that often yield higher margins due to innovation and market demand.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The current EPS for the fund is approximately $1.20, which represents a striking 10% increase from the previous year. This indicates that the portfolio companies are not only growing revenues but are also managing costs effectively to enhance profitability.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The fund's underlying holdings exhibit an average ROE of 15%, showcasing effective utilization of shareholder equity to generate substantial returns. This is particularly impressive given the competitive nature of the sectors in which these companies operate.

The average P/E ratio for the broader growth fund category stands at 19, suggesting that HYGG is slightly above the industry average. This premium valuation can be attributed to the fund's focus on high-quality growth stocks with strong growth trajectories.
