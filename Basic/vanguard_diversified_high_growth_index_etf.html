Current Performance

- - Net Asset Value (NAV) Growth:

The Vanguard Diversified High Growth INDEX ETF (VDHG) has shown a strong performance year-to-date, with an increase in NAV of approximately 15%. This growth can be attributed to favorable market conditions and a robust underlying equity portfolio focused on high growth sectors.

- - Year-on-Year Performance:

Over the past year, VDHG has exhibited a total return of around 12%, benefiting from a strong recovery in global markets and increased investor appetite for growth-oriented assets. The ETF's diversified approach enables it to capture gains from various sectors, including technology and consumer discretionary.

- - Volatility:

The fund has maintained a volatility score (beta) of 1.1, reflecting a slightly higher risk relative to the broader market. This is characteristic of high-growth investment strategies, which can lead to more pronounced price fluctuations.

Valuation Metrics

- - Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

VDHG's underlying assets have a weighted average P/E ratio of 24, indicating that the ETF is trading at a premium compared to the market average of around 20. This reflects the market's expectations of higher future earnings growth from its holdings.

- - Price-to-Book (P/B) Ratio:

The current average P/B ratio is 3.5, suggesting that the ETF's constituents are valued higher than their book value, which is common in growth-oriented sectors with intangible assets.
