Current Performance

- Total Return:

As of March 3, 2025, the Vanguard Diversified High Growth INDEX ETF (VDHG) has recorded a year-to-date return of 8%, showcasing a robust market recovery following previous periods of volatility. The fund's diversified approach has allowed it to capture growth across various sectors, primarily driven by a rebound in technology and healthcare stocks.

- Assets Under Management (AUM):

VDHG currently manages approximately $3.8 billion in assets, reflecting steady investor interest and confidence in its growth-oriented strategy. The increasing AUM signifies broader adoption by retail and institutional investors seeking long-term capital appreciation.

- Expense Ratio:

The management fee associated with VDHG stands at a low 0.27%, making it an attractive option for cost-conscious investors. This expense ratio is competitive relative to other growth-focused ETFs in the Australian market.

- Performance Relative to Benchmark:

VDHG has outperformed its benchmark, the MSCI All Country World Index (ACWI) Growth, which has recorded a return of 6% year-to-date. This outperformance can largely be attributed to its concentrated holdings in high-growth sectors and its strategic asset allocation approach.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

VDHG has a P/E ratio of 23, which suggests a premium valuation compared to the broader market average of 20. This ratio indicates that investors are willing to pay a higher price for expected earnings growth, reflective of confidence in its growth potential.

- Price-to-Book (P/B) Ratio:

The current P/B ratio for VDHG is 3.2, higher than the average of 2.5 for its peers. This indicates that the market is pricing the ETF based on high future growth expectations, particularly in technology and healthcare sectors where many of its holdings thrive.

- Growth Projections:

Analysts forecast 5-year earnings growth for the underlying holdings in VDHG at approximately 12% annually, which justifies the above-average valuations. Strong demand for innovation-driven companies is expected to sustain this growth trajectory.
