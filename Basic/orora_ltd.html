Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

Orora Ltd (ORA) has shown robust year-on-year revenue growth of 8%, primarily driven by increased demand for its packaging solutions in the food and beverage sectors. Earnings before interest and tax (EBIT) growth stands at 6%, reflecting effective cost management and operational efficiencies that have partially offset rising material costs.

- Profit Margins:

The net profit margin for Orora is currently at 9%, indicating healthy profitability within the competitive packaging industry. The focus on sustainable practices and efficiency improvements has helped in maintaining these margins in a challenging cost environment.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

Orora’s EPS is reported at $0.58, representing a 5% increase from the previous year. This growth illustrates the company's ability to enhance shareholder value through effective management and strategic investments.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The company has a ROE of 14%, reflecting strong utilization of shareholder equity in generating profits, which is quite favorable in the consumer packaging sector and indicates effective management of capital.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

As of today, Orora’s P/E ratio stands at 15, suggesting that the stock is fairly valued relative to its earnings potential. This multiple indicates the market's confidence in Orora’s ability to sustain growth amid industry pressures.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

The industry average P/E ratio for packaging companies hovers around 17, positioning Orora slightly below this benchmark. This could imply an opportunity for investors if they view the company as undervalued given its consistent performance.

- Dividend History:

Orora has a consistent history of dividend payments, with gradual increases over the years, highlighting the company’s reliable cash flow and commitment to shareholder returns.
