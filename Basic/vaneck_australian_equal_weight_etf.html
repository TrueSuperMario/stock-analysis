Current Performance

- - Net Asset Value (NAV):

As of September 23, 2024, the Vaneck Australian EQUAL Weight ETF (MVW) has maintained a Net Asset Value of approximately AUD 25.75 per share. This represents a year-to-date increase of around 8%, reflecting a positive market sentiment towards diversified equity investments.

- - Return Metrics:

The ETF has delivered a total return of about 10% over the past year, outperforming the ASX 200 index, which returned around 6% during the same period. This outperformance can be attributed to its unique equal weight strategy, which allows for greater exposure to mid-cap stocks that have contributed positively to its performance.

- - Volatility and Risk:

MVW has exhibited lower volatility compared to market benchmarks, with a standard deviation of returns around 12% compared to 15% for ASX 200, suggesting that the ETF presents a relatively stable investment option in terms of price fluctuations.

Valuation Metrics

The current P/E ratio for MVW is about 16, which is aligned with the average P/E of the Australian equity market. This reflects reasonable valuation levels, taking into account the equal weight strategy that mitigates concentration risk.

MVW has a P/B ratio of 1.8, slightly above the market average of 1.5, indicating a potential premium valuation. This is typical for well-diversified ETFs that hold quality stocks across various sectors.

- - Earnings Growth Projections:

Analysts anticipate an earnings growth rate of approximately 7% for the underlying companies within MVW over the next year, which supports the ETF's current valuation levels.
