Current Performance

- - Revenue and Earnings Growth:

The Dimensional Australian Core Equity Trust - Active ETF (DACE) has demonstrated a solid year-to-date performance with a total return of approximately 7%. This aligns with broader market trends, driven by a rebound in key sectors such as financials and materials. Earnings growth for the companies held within the ETF is expected to be robust, with an anticipated 8% increase year-over-year, primarily supported by effective management strategies and a focus on high-quality value stocks.

- - Profit Margins:

The average net profit margin of the underlying holdings within DACE is around 12%, reflecting healthy profitability trends across the portfolio. This margin is slightly above the average for the Australian equity market, indicating effective cost management relative to peers.

- - Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The weighted average EPS for the ETF's holdings has seen a significant increase, currently estimated at $1.10, which represents a 10% growth from the previous fiscal year. This strong EPS growth is driven by the recovery in economic activity and robust consumer demand.

- - Return on Equity (ROE):

DACE's underlying assets exhibit an average ROE of 15%, signaling effective utilization of equity capital to generate profits. This metric positions the ETF well against benchmarks for both the Australian financial sector and the broader market.
