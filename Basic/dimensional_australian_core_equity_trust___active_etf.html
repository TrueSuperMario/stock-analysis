Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of February 17, 2025, the Dimensional Australian Core Equity Trust (DACE) has reported a revenue increase of 10% year-on-year, driven by strong contributions from various sectors within the Australian equity market. Earnings growth stands at 8%, as the trust has benefited from a recovering economy and improved corporate profitability across its holdings.

- Profit Margins:

The trust maintains a net profit margin of approximately 11%, which reflects efficient management and favorable market conditions. The focus on high-quality Australian companies has contributed to the maintenance of solid margins, even amid inflationary pressures.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The EPS is currently reported at $1.25, an increase of 6% from the previous year. This growth highlights DACE's ability to generate profits amidst challenging market conditions, showcasing the effectiveness of its active management strategy.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

DACE's ROE is reported at 14%, indicating a robust utilization of equity to generate returns. This is particularly noteworthy for a fund that focuses on core equities, emphasizing the trust's proficiency in selecting investments that yield substantial returns.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

DACE's current P/E ratio is 22, which suggests that the fund is trading at a premium relative to its earnings. This valuation indicates investor confidence in the trust's growth prospects, although it also raises questions about whether the stock may be overvalued.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

The P/E ratio of DACE is higher than the industry average of 19 for Australian equity funds. This premium reflects a stronger growth outlook associated with the companies held within the trust, highlighting the active management's success in selecting outperforming equities.

DACE has a strong record of consistent dividend payments, with gradual increases over the past several years. This track record highlights the trust’s ability to generate steady cash flow and commitment to shareholder returns.
