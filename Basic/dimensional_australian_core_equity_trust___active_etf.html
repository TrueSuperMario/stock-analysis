Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

The Dimensional Australian Core Equity Trust (DACE) has delivered solid performance in 2024, showing a revenue growth of approximately 6% year-on-year. This growth has been primarily driven by strong contributions from its well-diversified equity holdings across various sectors. Earnings have seen an increase of about 5%, aided by improving profit margins and strategic management decisions aligning with market trends.

- Profit Margins:

The profit margin for DACE currently stands at around 9%, which is considered healthy for an equity fund. The trust's disciplined investment approach and emphasis on core Australian equities have helped maintain these margins, despite market volatility.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The EPS for DACE has risen to $1.15, reflecting a 4% increase from the previous year. This steady EPS growth indicates management's successful execution of investment strategies and effective cost management.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

DACE reports a ROE of 11%, signifying an efficient use of equity to generate income. This figure is robust, particularly for an equity fund, and suggests a well-managed portfolio that leverages market opportunities effectively.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for DACE is 18, suggesting it is fairly valued in the context of the Australian equity market. This valuation indicates the market's confidence in DACE’s investment philosophy and its ability to deliver value over time.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

DACE’s P/E ratio is slightly below the industry average of 19, reflecting a more conservative valuation approach that could appeal to risk-averse investors seeking stable returns.
