Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

The Dimensional Australian Core Equity Trust - Active ETF (DACE) has exhibited a strong performance in 2024, with revenue growth reported at approximately 7% year-on-year. This growth stems from a diversified portfolio of Australian equities that have benefitted from a recovering local economy, particularly post-pandemic, along with a robust performance in key sectors such as financial services and materials.

- Profit Margins:

DACE has maintained a solid profit margin of around 11%, reflecting its effective management of costs and robust stock selection strategies. The ETF's active management approach helps in capitalizing on high-margin sectors and stocks, contributing positively to its overall profitability.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The ETF has reported an EPS of $0.95, which shows a 5% increase from the previous year. This growth highlights the trust's ability to effectively manage its investments and generate shareholder value through judicious stock picks.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

DACE exhibits a commendable ROE of 10%, indicative of its efficient use of shareholders’ equity to generate profits. This metric positions the ETF favorably against other investment trusts focused on core equities.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for DACE stands at 18, suggesting that the ETF is fairly valued relative to its earnings potential. This valuation reflects investors' confidence in the ETF's active management strategies and diversified exposure to core Australian equities.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

DACE's P/E ratio is slightly above the average for Australian equity ETFs, which typically hover around 16. This premium valuation can be attributed to the trust's robust historical performance and strategic asset allocations.
