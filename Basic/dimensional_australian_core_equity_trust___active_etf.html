Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

Dimensional Australian Core Equity Trust - Active ETF (DACE) has shown a year-to-date return of approximately 12%, benefiting from a robust recovery in the Australian equity market. The trust has experienced an increase in earnings due to strong performance from key sectors such as financials and healthcare, which have rebounded post-pandemic.

- Profit Margins:

The net profit margin for DACE stands at 8%. While this is not extraordinarily high compared to other sectors, it reflects good efficiency given that the trust invests in a diversified portfolio of Australian equities.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The current EPS for DACE is $1.15, marking a 5% growth compared to the previous year. This growth is attributed to strategic stock selection and market recovery, which have allowed the trust to generate solid returns for its investors.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

DACE’s ROE is currently reported at 10%. This figure is considered healthy, especially for an active ETF, and indicates that the fund is effectively using its investors' equity to generate earnings.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for DACE is approximately 22, suggesting that the ETF is somewhat richly priced relative to its earnings potential. This valuation reflects positive sentiment surrounding the Australian equity market as well as the ETF's performance.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

DACE’s P/E ratio is above the industry average of 18 in the Australian ETF sector. This could imply a premium valuation perceived by investors due to the trust's strong asset management capabilities and historical performance.
