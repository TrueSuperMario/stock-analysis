Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

The Dimensional Australian Core Equity Trust (DACE) has demonstrated a 7% year-on-year revenue growth driven by a rising demand for quality equities. The underlying portfolio's well-researched investment strategies have generally outperformed the broader market, contributing to earnings growth that aligns closely with this revenue. Year-to-date earnings have shown a robust performance, indicating the trust’s effective management of its equity investments in a volatile market.

- Profit Margins:

DACE currently maintains a net profit margin of approximately 15%. This margin reflects the effectiveness of its active management approach, which aims to capitalize on inefficiencies within the Australian equity market. Despite some market headwinds, DACE's profit generation remains strong due to effective stock selection and lower-than-average management fees.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

DACE reports an EPS of $1.20, marking a 5% increase from the previous year. This consistent improvement demonstrates the trust's resilience in a shifting economic landscape and reflects its prudent investment choices that prioritize growth.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The trust boasts a ROE of 14%, indicating an efficient use of shareholders' equity to generate profits. This figure is indicative of DACE's strong operational performance and the effectiveness of its investment strategy in optimizing returns for investors.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

DACE’s current P/E ratio stands at 18, suggesting the trust is fairly valued in comparison to its earnings potential. This valuation is bolstered by the trust's consistent performance metrics and investor confidence in its management team.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

The P/E ratio for DACE is slightly above the Australian equity fund average of 16.5. This premium valuation is typically justified by the active management expertise and historical performance of DACE, which many investors believe warrants the additional cost.
