Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

The Dimensional Australian Core Equity Trust - Active ETF (DACE) has shown a year-to-date revenue growth of approximately 8%, benefiting from a broad-based economic rebound and steady contributions from its diversified equity portfolio. Earnings growth is reported at 6%, with some gains driven by significant equity performances in major Australian sectors, including financials and materials.

- Profit Margins:

As an Active ETF, DACE doesn’t have traditional profit margins like a corporate entity; instead, it focuses on maximising total returns to unitholders. However, the fund's effective management has led to a distribution return that outpaces other passive funds in its category.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

As an ETF, DACE does not provide EPS in the conventional sense but distributes income to unitholders. The annualised distribution yield stands at about 4.5%, reflecting healthy underlying performance across its equity holdings.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

While ROE is not typically reported for ETFs, the fund has demonstrated excellent management efficiency in generating returns for unitholders. Historical data indicates consistent outperformance against benchmark indices, reflecting effective active management.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

DACE currently has a weighted average P/E ratio of 15, which is competitive within the Australian market context. This suggests that the fund is fairly valued given its underlying equity exposures.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

The fund’s P/E ratio is slightly below the average P/E for Australian equities, which is around 16, suggesting that DACE could be positioned attractively relative to the broader market.
