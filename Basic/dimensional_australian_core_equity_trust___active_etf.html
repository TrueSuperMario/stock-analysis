Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of October 2024, the Dimensional Australian Core Equity Trust - Active ETF (DACE) has posted an impressive revenue growth rate of 7% year-on-year, primarily driven by strong performances in key sectors such as finance and healthcare. Earnings have seen a robust increase of 8%, benefitting from improved operational efficiencies and a favorable economic environment.

- Profit Margins:

DACE's net profit margin is currently at 11%, indicating strong profitability within its investment portfolio. This margin reflects effective cost management despite rising market volatility and increasing operational costs, particularly amid fluctuating interest rates.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The EPS stands at $1.05, signifying a 5% increase from last year. This consistent growth exemplifies DACE's ability to generate value for its shareholders amidst a competitive market landscape.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

DACE has achieved a ROE of 13%, indicating commendable utilization of equity capital to produce earnings. This performance metric is particularly strong for an active equity ETF, highlighting the effectiveness of its management strategies.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for DACE is 20, which suggests a moderately high valuation compared to its earnings potential. This suggests that the market holds a favorable outlook on the ETF, likely due to its consistent performance.

- P/E Ratio Compared to the Industry Average:

DACE’s P/E ratio is slightly above the Australian equity fund average of 18, which can be attributed to investor optimism regarding the fund’s growth prospects and management strategy in a dynamic market setting.
