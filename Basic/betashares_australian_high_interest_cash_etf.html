The ETF’s profit margin stands at around 1.5%, which is typical for cash and cash-equivalent instruments. This margin reflects the low-risk nature of the underlying assets, mainly high-interest accounts and cash equivalents, where returns are less volatile compared to equities.

The current earnings per share (EPS) for AAA is approximately $0.10, indicating a modest increase compared to the previous year. This growth stems mainly from higher interest yields as the Reserve Bank of Australia has maintained a tight monetary policy.

As a cash management fund, AAA does not traditionally utilize shareholder equity in the same way equities do, thus ROE metrics may not apply directly. However, the fund aims to maximize returns for investors through interest income.
