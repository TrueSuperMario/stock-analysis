Current Performance

- Net Asset Value (NAV) and Returns:

As of January 6, 2025, the Betashares Australian High Interest Cash ETF (AAA) has shown a year-to-date return of approximately 3% in response to rising interest rates. This level of performance is driven by its objective to provide investors with exposure to cash-like returns through a portfolio predominantly composed of Australian cash management trusts and deposits.

- Cash Flow Generation:

The ETF’s cash flows have benefited from higher interest rates set by the Reserve Bank of Australia (RBA), with the average yield of the fund increasing to around 4.5%. This yield reflects the ETF's strategic allocation in environments with rising interest rates that have improved returns from cash investments.

- Liquidity and Trading Volume:

AAA has maintained good liquidity in the market with an average daily volume of approximately 200,000 units traded. This liquidity facilitates easier entry and exit points for investors, contributing to an efficient investor experience.

Valuation Metrics

- Expense Ratio:

The expense ratio for AAA is relatively low at 0.18%, making it an efficient choice for investors looking for cash equivalents without incurring high management costs. This efficiency contributes positively to net returns.

- Yield Comparison:

The yield of 4.5% is competitive relative to traditional savings accounts and other cash management products in Australia. Given the current interest rate environment, AAA offers an attractive yield compared to the sector average of 3.75%.
