Current Performance

  • **Revenue and Earnings Growth:**

    • Bapcor Ltd (BAP) has reported a year-on-year revenue growth of 7%, primarily driven by stronger performance in its Trade and Specialist Wholesale segments. Earnings growth has been more modest at 4%, reflecting higher costs associated with supply chain challenges and inflationary pressures on input costs.

  • **Profit Margins:**

    • The company’s net profit margin stands at 8.5%, which is consistent with previous years but reflects the rising cost environment. Bapcor has been relatively successful in passing on some of these costs to customers, supporting its margin stability.

  • **Earnings Per Share (EPS):**

    • EPS is currently at $0.58, representing a 3% increase from the previous year. This moderate growth reflects Bapcor’s ability to maintain operational efficiency amid economic headwinds.

  • **Return on Equity (ROE):**

    • Bapcor’s ROE is 14%, indicating effective utilisation of shareholder equity to generate profits. While this is a slight decline from the previous year's 15%, it remains strong relative to industry standards.

    Valuation Metrics

  • **Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:**

    • The current P/E ratio for Bapcor Ltd is 18, suggesting reasonable valuation relative to its earnings potential. This valuation reflects the market’s balanced outlook on Bapcor’s growth prospects and the broader retail automotive market conditions.

  • **P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:**

    • Bapcor’s P/E ratio is slightly above the automotive parts industry average of 16, indicating a small premium likely driven by the company’s strong market position and consistent financial performance.

    Analyst Recommendations

  • **Price Targets:**
