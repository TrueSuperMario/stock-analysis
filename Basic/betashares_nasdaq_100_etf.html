Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of October 2024, the Betashares Nasdaq 100 ETF (NDQ) has seen robust year-to-date performance, with a revenue increase attributed to the continued strength of technology stocks that dominate the Nasdaq index. Specifically, NDQ's performance mirrors a significant upward trend in the technology sector, with earnings growth reflecting a broader recovery post-pandemic and ongoing innovation in tech.

- Profit Margins:

Given that NDQ is an ETF that tracks the Nasdaq 100, typically comprising high-growth tech companies, the profit margins are inherently linked to the margins of its underlying assets. Overall, these companies have demonstrated improved profitability, with margins remaining above average for the sector as cost management efforts and higher consumer demand play a role.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

EPS for NDQ has shown strong gains, with an increase of approximately 8% over the past year, reflecting the positive earnings performances from key holdings like Apple, Microsoft, and Amazon. This increase illustrates strong financial health and the ability of the underlying companies to generate substantial profits.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The ROE for the leading companies within the ETF remains high, averaging around 25%. This indicates effective utilization of shareholder equity to generate profit, underscoring the financial strength of major constituents within the Nasdaq 100.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for NDQ stands at approximately 22, which is aligned with historical averages for the Nasdaq 100. However, this indicates that while the ETF is fairly valued, it does reflect investor confidence in sustained growth from tech sectors.

- P/E Ratio Compared to the Industry Average:

NDQ's P/E ratio is above the broader market average of 18, suggesting that investors are willing to pay a premium for growth potential in technology stocks, expecting continued innovation and expansion.
