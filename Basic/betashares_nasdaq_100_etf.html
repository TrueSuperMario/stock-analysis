Current Performance

The Betashares Nasdaq 100 ETF (NDQ) has recorded a robust year-to-date performance, with a revenue growth rate reflective of the underlying performance of the NASDAQ 100 index, which houses many leading tech companies. Currently, the ETF has experienced an impressive annualized return of approximately 20%, driven by strong earnings rebounds from technology giants.

- - Profit Margins:

NDQ’s profit margins are closely tied to the technology sector's overall profitability. The underlying index constituents boast average net profit margins of around 25%, supported by high demand for tech products and services amid digital transformation trends.

- - Earnings Per Share (EPS):

As an ETF tracking the NASDAQ 100, NDQ's effective EPS is derived from the constituent companies. On average, the index’s companies have reported an EPS growth of about 15% over the last year, indicating a strong recovery from previous economic downturns.

- - Return on Equity (ROE):

The ROE for companies within the NASDAQ 100 is approximately 18%, which indicates a strong ability to generate profits from shareholder equity. This strong ROE contributes positively to the ETF's overall performance.
