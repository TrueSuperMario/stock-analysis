Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

The Betashares Nasdaq 100 ETF (NDQ) has demonstrated robust performance with a year-to-date revenue growth of approximately 22%, reflecting the sustained demand for technology and growth-oriented stocks that primarily populate the Nasdaq 100 index. Earnings growth has mirrored this trend, driven by strong performance from tech giants like Apple, Microsoft, and Amazon.

- Profit Margins:

NDQ's net profit margin is indicative of high operational efficiency, particularly among its major holdings. On average, the companies within the ETF benefit from strong profit margins, often exceeding 20%, due to their dominance in respective markets and their ability to control costs.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

As of January 2025, the ETF showcases an EPS growth of 15% compared to the previous year, underscoring the continued profitability of its constituent companies even in a rising interest rate environment.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The ROE for the underlying companies in the NDQ is approximately 27%, showcasing exceptional efficiency in turning equity investments into profit. This high ROE is particularly driven by the technology sector's capacity for innovation and scalability.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio of NDQ stands at 28, reflecting a premium valuation typical for growth-focused ETFs. This ratio indicates that the ETF is valued higher than the broader market, aligning with investor expectations for continued growth.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

Relative to the average P/E ratio in the technology sector of around 25, NDQ's valuation highlights the market’s bullish sentiment towards growth stocks, particularly in light of their resilience during economic fluctuations.

The ETF has recorded consistent distributions over the past few years, which have mostly increased annually. The growing dividend is indicative of the strong cash flow from its technology holdings.
