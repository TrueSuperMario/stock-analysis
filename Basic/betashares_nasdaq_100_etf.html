Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

The Betashares Nasdaq 100 ETF (NDQ) has demonstrated robust performance in the year-to-date period, with a return of approximately 30%. This growth has been propelled by strong performances from major tech companies and the overall recovery in equity markets post-pandemic. Earnings growth for companies within the Nasdaq 100 has outperformed many sectors, reflecting a resurgence in consumer demand and digital transformation trends.

- Profit Margins:

The underlying companies within NDQ generally maintain high profit margins, aided by their strong market positions and competitive advantages in technology and innovation. The technology sector typically enjoys profit margins averaging around 20-25%, significantly above the broader market average.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The ETF’s effective EPS, derived from the earnings of its top constituents, showcases significant growth, with a collective increase of around 18% over the past 12 months. This growth is attributed to improved operational efficiencies and growing revenue streams.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The ROE for the top companies in NDQ, such as Apple, Microsoft, and Amazon, is substantially high at around 30%, reflecting strong utilization of shareholder equity in generating profits. This high figure is indicative of their dominance and competitive edge in their respective markets.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for NDQ stands at approximately 25, indicating a premium valuation compared to historical averages. This suggests that investors are willing to pay more for each dollar of earnings, reflecting confidence in future growth prospects.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

The Nasdaq 100's P/E ratio is higher than the average for the broader S&P 500, which is about 20. This premium is justified by the high growth expectations of the tech sector, as well as the increasing reliance on technology in various aspects of life and business.
