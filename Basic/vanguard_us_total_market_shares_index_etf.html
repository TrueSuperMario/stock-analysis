Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of November 2024, the Vanguard US Total Market Shares Index ETF (VTS) has demonstrated robust growth with a year-to-date revenue increase of approximately 8%. The earnings of underlying constituents reflect solid corporate performance across various sectors, with a notable year-over-year earnings growth rate of 7%, driven primarily by strong performances in technology and consumer discretionary sectors.

- Profit Margins:

The ETF's underlying assets exhibit a net profit margin average of around 12%, which is indicative of healthy profitability among large-cap U.S. companies. This margin has been stable, supported by ongoing efficiency improvements and a generally favorable economic environment.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The weighted average EPS for companies within VTS has risen to $11.50, marking a 5% increase over the past year. This growth underscores the resilience of the underlying firms and their ability to generate profits despite economic volatility.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The aggregate ROE for companies in the VTS is approximately 15%, which suggests effective management and use of shareholders' equity to generate profits. This level of ROE is considered strong in comparison to historical averages.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

The current P/E ratio for VTS is around 20, reflecting a moderate valuation given the earnings backdrop. This figure aligns with broader market expectations for future growth, anticipating solid earnings expansion in the coming quarters.

- P/E Ratio Compared to the Industry Average:

VTS's P/E ratio is slightly above the broader market average of 18.5. This premium can be attributed to the ETF’s diversified exposure to high-growth sectors like technology and healthcare, which often command higher valuations.
