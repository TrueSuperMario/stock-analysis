Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of January 27, 2025, Vanguard US Total Market Shares INDEX ETF (VTS) has demonstrated a robust revenue growth of 10% year-on-year, largely driven by a strong rebound in the US equity market following economic recovery. Earnings growth is steady at 8%, indicating that the ETF has effectively captured the rebound in various sectors, most notably technology and consumer discretionary.

- Profit Margins:

The net profit margin for VTS sits at approximately 15%, which is reflective of the ETF's diversified portfolio across multiple sectors of the US economy. This margin is supported by low expense ratios, a hallmark of Vanguard's management style, which allows for competitive performance in the ETF landscape.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The current EPS for VTS is around $93.50, representing a 4% increase from the previous year. This steady growth reflects the overall positive performance of the underlying assets, including large-cap stocks which form the bulk of the index.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

VTS's ROE is reported at 7%, which, while lower than individual companies may exhibit, is commendable for an ETF as it reflects the average return across a wide portfolio of stocks. Vanguard's broad investment approach mitigates risks, enhancing investor confidence.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

VTS is currently trading at a P/E ratio of 22, suggesting that the market values the ETF at a premium compared to historical benchmarks. This can be attributed to investor optimism regarding long-term growth prospects post-recession.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

The P/E ratio for VTS exceeds the industry average of 19, indicating a strong market confidence in the US economy’s recovery and growth potential. This premium aligns with the ETF's broad exposure across different sectors, particularly in growth-heavy areas.
