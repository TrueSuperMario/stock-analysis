Current Performance

As of March 2025, the Betashares Australia 200 ETF (A200) has shown moderate growth in its underlying index, the S&P/ASX 200. However, earnings growth among constituent companies has variably ranged from 5% to 10%, influenced by recent economic conditions and corporate performance.

The average profit margin for companies within the S&P/ASX 200 stands at approximately 12%. This is commendable, reflecting solid management and operational efficiency. High-margin sectors such as healthcare and consumer discretionary are propelling this average amid cost pressures faced by the industrials sector.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The weighted average EPS for the ETF constituents has increased to $1.25, marking a 4% rise year-over-year. This reflects overall corporate profitability amid a resilient economic backdrop and dividend announcements from many blue-chip stocks.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The weighted average ROE for the A200 holdings is around 15%, indicating robust utilization of equity to generate profits. This is particularly notable for companies in the technology and healthcare sectors, contributing positively to the ETF's performance.

The A200's P/E ratio is similar to the overall market average for Australian equities, which stands at approximately 17.5. This alignment indicates that the ETF is fairly valued relative to the broader market.
