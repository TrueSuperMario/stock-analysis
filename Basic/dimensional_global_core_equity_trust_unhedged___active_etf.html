Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of October 2024, the Dimensional Global Core Equity Trust Unhedged (DGCE) has displayed robust performance, with a year-to-date return of approximately 12%, driven by strong revenue growth in the underlying equity holdings across diversified sectors. Earnings have surged by around 8%, showcasing effective management and positive market conditions.

- Profit Margins:

The ETF's underlying holdings exhibit net profit margins of 15%, which is favorable compared to the broader market. This strong profitability indicates efficient cost management and operational excellence within its portfolio companies.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The current weighted average EPS for the ETF's underlying equities is around $3.40, reflecting a notable increase of 5% from the previous year. This growth underlines the performance resilience of the companies in the ETF amidst fluctuating market conditions.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

DGCE's underlying portfolio shows an average ROE of 13%, suggesting efficient utilization of equity capital by the component companies. This figure is indicative of solid business operations and competitiveness within their respective industries.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

DGCE currently has a P/E ratio of 18, positioning it slightly above the broader market average of 17. This suggests that investors are willing to pay a premium for exposure to the diversified mix of global equities that the fund offers.

- P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

Relative to its comparable peers, DGCE's P/E ratio is slightly elevated, indicating a perception of higher growth potential or lower risk associated with its holdings, particularly in stable sectors like technology and healthcare where many of its major investments reside.
