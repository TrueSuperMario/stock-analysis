Current Performance

- Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of February 24, 2025, the Dimensional Global Core Equity Trust Unhedged - Active ETF (DGCE) has reported a revenue growth of 7% year-on-year. This growth is primarily driven by strong performance in the technology and healthcare sectors, which have rebounded following recent market fluctuations. Earnings growth is noted at 5%, reflecting increased operational efficiency despite taking on some market risks.

- Profit Margins:

DGCE maintains a net profit margin of 8%. This is considered healthy for an equity ETF, indicating effective management of costs despite the volatility within global markets. The focus on diversified holdings has helped stabilize margins amidst challenging economic conditions.

- Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The ETF’s EPS has reached $1.10, marking a 3% increase compared to the previous year. This modest growth indicates a steady performance relative to industry peers and highlights DGCE's resilience in the face of global economic challenges.

- Return on Equity (ROE):

The current ROE stands at 10%, which indicates a robust utilization of equity capital to generate profits. This performance metric signifies effective management strategies at the trust level, especially in capital allocation across varied sectors.

Valuation Metrics

- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

DGCE currently has a P/E ratio of 18, suggesting that the ETF is reasonably valued relative to its earnings potential. This valuation reflects a balance between growth expectations and current earnings performance.

The P/E ratio is slightly above the average for similar ETFs, which stands at 16.5. The premium valuation is indicative of investor confidence in DGCE’s management and strategic sector allocation.
