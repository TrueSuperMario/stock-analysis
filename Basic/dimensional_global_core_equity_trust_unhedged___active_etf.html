Current Performance

- - Revenue and Earnings Growth:

As of September 2024, the Dimensional Global Core Equity Trust Unhedged - Active ETF (DGCE) reported a year-to-date revenue increase of 8%, driven by a diverse portfolio of international equities that capitalize on global economic recovery. Earnings have grown by 6% over the same period, reflecting a robust performance across various sectors, particularly in technology and consumer discretionary.

- - Profit Margins:

The ETF maintains a net profit margin of approximately 12%. This is indicative of effective portfolio management in capturing capital appreciation while minimizing expenses in an active investment strategy.

- - Earnings Per Share (EPS):

The fund's EPS stands at $1.50, marking a 5% increase from the previous quarter. This growth reflects the underlying strength of the companies within the ETF's portfolio and their ability to generate profits in a competitive global market.

- - Return on Equity (ROE):

DGCE has achieved a ROE of 15%, highlighting the ETF's effective use of shareholder equity in generating earnings. This performance metric indicates a strong return on investments made in international equities.

Valuation Metrics

- - Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio:

Currently, DGCE has a P/E ratio of 21, suggesting a relatively high valuation compared to historical averages, reflecting investor confidence in global equity growth.

- - P/E Ratio compared to the industry average:

The ETF's P/E ratio is above the global equity average of 18, suggesting that investors are willing to pay a premium for exposure to high-quality international equities captured in the fund's holdings.
